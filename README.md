# felt-agent

**A humanoid that generates its own reward.** One unified predict-and-act world-model cortex — built from proven robot-foundation-model methods (the GR00T / π0 / Helix / V-JEPA / Cosmos lineage), warm-started on the human-piloted data companies already have and trained end-to-end — carrying one **novel** part: a **grounded amygdala** that generates a self-generated, human-affect-grounded reward for the value system to develop toward. Backbone methods are `proven`; the felt layer is the `bet`. Full spec — the parts, the `z_t` contract, the staged plan 0→6 — in **[build/](build/)**.

## Current work — the hardware pilot (2026)

The first physical instantiation of this architecture, scoped to what one person and a $500 arm can falsify: **one Dreamer 4-style transformer world model** — vision (ViT tokens) + audio + proprioception fused in a single latent — with output heads for dynamics, policy/value, and the **grounded reward** (seeded from the Phase 0 result below). Demonstrations are collected by physically moving a LeRobot SO-101 leader arm; the model is pretrained offline, trained inside its own imagination, then deployed for **24/7 continual online RL**.

The deliverable is honest measurement, not a demo reel: where do **plasticity loss, catastrophic forgetting, and reward-grounding drift** bite on real hardware — and do the anti-wireheading (RPE-habituation) and real/imagined provenance interlocks hold outside simulation? Code, data, and the failure report ship here, open. Plan and status: **[experiments/pilot/](experiments/pilot/)**.

## The thesis

Standard reinforcement learning works because someone hands the agent a reward and it maximizes it. That works in small, closed worlds. It breaks as the world grows: the bigger and more open the environment, the less anyone knows what the reward even *is*. What is the reward function for *living* — acting well across a vast, open world with no episodes, no score, no designer watching? No one can write it. **Reward specification is the part that doesn't survive contact with real complexity** — so for open-ended agency in huge environments, we have no guiding principle at all.

Except one, and it isn't in our equations — it's inside us. A human moves through an environment of overwhelming complexity with no reward function anywhere, guided by **internal feeling**: a built-in, evolved read — a small, fixed set of innate reactions that flag survival-relevant things (danger, the needs of others, what demands attention) and read out as what draws us in and what we recoil from. Evolution seems to have faced the same reward-specification problem — unable to hardwire a value for every situation, it hardwired only the invariants and let a lifetime build the rest. It is the only existence proof of open-world agency we have, and it runs inside every person.

> **Simple RL** — the guiding principle is a reward we write.
> **Open-world RL** — no reward is writable, and the only guiding principle we know of is **human internal feeling.** So that is the thing to build.

**Why not just a learned reward model (RLHF)?** That's the mainstream answer to “no writable reward” — train a reward model on human preferences and optimize it. But a learned reward model is *trained externally, then fixed*: it grades against *someone else's* labelled preferences and can't ground on *this body's* unlived consequences — what a fall, a burn, or a caregiver's approval come to feel like *to this agent* over its life. The felt system is the opposite shape: a *small, fixed, innate* prior (the invariants) grounding a value that is **continually earned over lived embodied time.** RLHF gives you a frozen judge of human text/behaviour; the felt system grows a *first-person* value. Different problem — theirs is “align to stated human preference,” this is “generate a lived objective where none is written.”

**A theory, not a readout of the brain.** No one can watch the whole affective loop at once in a living, developing brain — you can't record the amygdala, the cortex, and the neuromodulators together over a lifetime, so *how* affect shapes what the cortex attends to, learns, and comes to value is underdetermined by what neuroscience can currently measure. So this isn't reporting settled biology; it is **our theory of how an amygdala-like appraisal drives a cortex-like learner**, offered as a computational hypothesis and an attempt to build it. The neuroscience is the inspiration and the sanity check — not the proof.

## The parts

**One part is the novelty — the amygdala, which *generates* the reward. Everything below is standard RL and world-model machinery, redirected to run on it.** In four groups:

**① The novelty — it generates the reward**

### Amygdala — a net that reads perception and outputs a pull
No mystery: it's **a small neural network that reads perception — mainly vision and audio — and outputs a pull.** Where a standard agent is *handed* a reward, this net *generates* one by pattern-matching what it sees and hears — think **pain receptors, but for complex percepts**: nociceptors fire on tissue damage; the amygdala fires *affect* on a face, a scream, a nurturing scene. Two jobs above all: **pull toward the good / away from the bad (approach/avoid), and be moved by *other people* (social)** — and it steers attention (what's worth looking at) as it does. The social half is load-bearing: most of what anyone values is learned from people, not solo trial-and-error (danger is the third flag, tied to pain). It reads out as two faces: **dopamine** = approach (rises toward the good and salient — a fixed read whose *phasic* pull fades as the good becomes predicted; see the value system, § Basal ganglia) and **cortisol** = threat/avoid (rises to danger and others' distress, and *lingers* — tonic). The mechanism isn't the hard part; *grounding* it is — there's no labeled dataset of "what feels good," so you seed it from human affect.

**Novel or borrowed?** The net is standard — a thin head (~10–50M) reading the cortex latent `z_t`, grounded by text-anchoring / a VLM / affect datasets ([build/TRAINING.md](build/TRAINING.md)); it's first *prototyped* on a frozen CLIP/SigLIP encoder to prove the data-spec, then re-grounded onto `z_t` (the CLIP head is scaffolding, not the shipped part). Novel is the *role*: the reward is **generated and grounded**, not handed — human-meaningful value, not content-free novelty.

**Why these flags, and no more.** The flags are the **world-invariants** — the few things true in *every* environment a body could inhabit: tissue damage is bad → pain/cortisol; a conspecific's distress matters → care/cortisol; sudden/looming/novel things → salience; resources and nurturing cues → approach/dopamine. Those are hardwireable; everything contingent is not. **Hardwire the invariants; learn the rest** — the minimal safe prior, and exactly the move the agent needs: don't write the open-world reward, install the invariant flags and let a lifetime build on top.

**② The machinery it drives** *(standard RL + a world model, now scored by the felt reward)*

### Imagine-and-feel — score imagined futures (→ actions) and plans (→ reasoning)
The agent **feels futures that haven't happened.** A single learned world model — not one imaginer per sense — renders the *whole multimodal state at once* (sight, sound, touch) into the **same workspace** the real senses use, and the amygdala reads it **blind to real-vs-imagined**. That is *imagine-and-feel*: roll a future, feel it as if real, act toward the one that pulls hardest and away from the one that raises threat. The one safeguard is a **reality-monitoring tag** so a felt-but-imagined threat isn't acted on as present (that tag failing is hallucination). The bottleneck is the **search**, not the feeling.

**The same loop steers *thinking*, not just acting.** The read is blind to *what kind* of content it is, so an imagined *scene* and an imagined *plan* get the same appraisal: the amygdala feels an imagined scene to pick an **action**, and feels an imagined plan's consequences to pick a **line of reasoning** — pruning thought-space the way a hunch prunes options instead of searching blind. It's the thesis one level up (you can't write a reward for "good reasoning" any more than for "living"). **The honest gap:** the appraisal is grounded on *percepts*, not abstract tokens — it can't directly grade “is this line of math promising,” so it grades a plan *through the imagined consequences it evokes* (a plan you imagine ending in someone's distress *feels* bad). That makes reasoning the **least-grounded** use of the loop — flagged, not sold as solved.

**Novel or borrowed?** The world model is borrowed SOTA (**V-JEPA-2-AC / Dreamer-over-`z_t`**, searched by **Sampled-MuZero / MPPI**). Novel is the *routing*: standard model-based RL scores imagined futures with a hand-written reward; here the amygdala scores them by *felt* value — over both imagined scenes (→ actions) *and* imagined plans (→ reasoning), one faculty, not a modality-split. The reasoning use is the least-grounded of the bets (somatic-marker flavoured — Damasio).

### Basal ganglia — value, the cache, and where habituation lives
Unlike a critic trained on a reward you supply, this value **cannot be pretrained** — it's about *this* agent's lived consequences, which no dataset contains — so it develops through embodied experience (accelerated by imagine-and-feel). In a mature agent it also **caches** the expensive imagine-and-feel computation, compiling slow deliberation (System 2) into fast reflex (System 1). Its learning signal is the **RPE (reward/value prediction-error)** — *distinct* from the amygdala's raw approach signal; neuroscience blurs them under one word, the engineering must not.

**Habituation lives here, for free.** Because the drive is the prediction *error*, not the raw reward, a good the value learner has come to *predict* produces no error → no phasic pull → the agent stops fixating on it and is drawn to *unpredicted* grounded-good instead. That's the classic reward-prediction-error dynamic (neuroscience's phasic dopamine), and it gives anti-wireheading and curiosity in one — "seek good" becomes "seek *new* good" — with no separate module. It's **appetitive-only** (a *predicted* injury still hurts, so **pleasure's pull fades, pain's does not**) and stays distinct from RND/ICM curiosity, which chases *world-model* surprise (what will happen) rather than *reward* surprise over grounded affect (how good it turns out).

**Novel or borrowed?** The learner is borrowed SOTA (**SimbaV2 high-UTD actor-critic**, ensemble twin-Q, RPE-trained). Novel is the *role*: unlike a critic trained offline on a supplied reward and then done, this value is **continual** — never pretrainable (it's about this agent's unlived life), never finished — with habituation built in as RPE-decay, so it *generates* curiosity instead of needing a novelty bonus.

**③ How it grows**

### Growing up — the agent outgrows its seed
The amygdala's seed can't run the show forever — the agent would never become more than it — so this agent **outgrows its seed.** As the BG's value gets well-grounded it earns more authority (confidence-gated via NE), plus a **deliberate developmental boost** — a slow scalar ramped up over development, over-weighting self-direction *regardless* of confidence, pushing the agent to venture and form its own assumptions. One hard constraint keeps it safe: the boost amplifies the **appetitive/venturing side only — never the aversive floor.** Pain stays **un-boostable** (a teenager takes reckless risks and still flinches from fire). The amygdala goes from *leash* to *check*.

**Novel or borrowed?** A **new application of the one idea**, not a new learning rule — the same grounded appraisal *hands authority* to the value it grew (innate seed → self-owned value, confidence-gated via NE, with an appetitive-only boost). What's new is the developmental *handoff*, not the components.

**④ The rest** *(the floor, plus modulation & upkeep)* — pain, drives, uncertainty, memory
- **Pain — nociception, the non-negotiable floor.** The one felt signal that **never habituates** (a pain that faded would stop protecting the body). A dedicated tissue-damage part with a **fast reflex that bypasses the planner** and a strong aversive learning signal — with the amygdala, one of the two innate affective sensors. The one cleanly-buildable part; its target *is* specifiable — damage. *(Built with: a momentum / disturbance observer — collision detection — + a small learned `pain(z)` head, ~1 kHz reflex below the policy; borrowed, nothing new.)*
- **Hypothalamus (drives), demoted.** Homeostatic setpoints — energy, temperature, damage load: slow, tonic, satiable. For an imitation-pretrained agent that already has a seeded appraisal and pain, drives reduce to minimal upkeep. *(Built with: interoceptive setpoint errors — homeostatic RL, Keramati & Gutkin.)*
- **Norepinephrine — a gain knob, not a reward.** A scalar gain driven by the agent's own surprise (`NE ← ema(prediction_error + ensemble_disagreement + novelty)`): surprise turns up the gain — scaling the felt signal and learning rate — and settles when predictable. It sets *how much* the signals count, never adds value.
- **Memory / hippocampus — and why sleep isn't a phase.** An episodic store of lived segments (the *buffer*, not a separate net); **continuous high-UTD replay** consolidates it into the value / world-model / felt heads — so there's no separate sleep stage, **replay *is* perpetual sleep.** What's kept is **value-gated**: the felt system decides what matters and lets the rest fade — selective forgetting, the way a brain does it, not a perfect record. This is where the **forever-policy** problem lives — plasticity loss + catastrophic forgetting over a lifetime — the honestly-flagged weakest area, though its load is shared: composition rides in the world model, and *what to keep* is the amygdala's call. *(Built with: a replay buffer + high-UTD replay + ReDo resets / replay-CLEAR rehearsal — borrowed.)*
- **Dopamine-decay = habituation.** The phasic pull is the *prediction error* over the amygdala's reward, so a good the value learner comes to predict stops pulling — "seek good" becomes "seek *new* good," giving anti-wireheading and curiosity in one. Appetitive-only (pain is never predicted away); no separate module — it emerges in the value system's RPE (full mechanism, § Basal ganglia). *(Built with: standard RPE / TD-decay — borrowed.)*

## Related work

Where this sits, stated as *how it differs* rather than as if it were unprecedented:

- **Active inference / the free-energy principle** (Friston) — the closest formal theory: an agent driven by its own generative model, acting to minimize surprise, with *prior preferences* over observations playing the role of value. Felt-agent is nearly "active inference with an affective prior" — the differences it stakes out: the prior is **grounded in a few human-affect invariants** (not generic preferences), **appraisal (amygdala) and value (BG) are separate systems**, and value is **earned over lived time**, not a fixed prior.
- **Intrinsic motivation / developmental robotics** (Schmidhuber; Oudeyer; Barto; Pathak — curiosity, RND/ICM, empowerment; ~20 years of open-ended development). These drive open-ended behavior from **content-free** signals (novelty, prediction error, control). The felt reward is instead **grounded and human-meaningful** (approach people, avoid distress), so it should pursue *meaningful* variety, not bare novelty. **This is the thesis's crux — and its least-tested claim** (see [The experiments, and the boundary they hit](#the-experiments-and-the-boundary-they-hit)); the honest open question is whether felt reward and a strong novelty/empowerment baseline produce *materially different* behavior.
- **"Reward is Enough"** (Silver, Singh, Precup, Sutton) — the head-on opposing view: reward maximization suffices for intelligence. The rejoinder: *yes, if you can specify the reward* — and the whole bet is that in open worlds you can't, so the reward must be **generated**, not given.
- **Homeostatic RL** (Keramati & Gutkin) — drives as setpoint errors; used here for the demoted hypothalamus.
- **Affective neuroscience & somatic markers** (Damasio; Panksepp) — the empirical grounding for "feeling steers decisions" and for a small set of innate affect systems. Inspiration and sanity check, not proof.
- **Cognitive architectures** — MicroPsi (Bach) builds an agent on drives and modulators, very close in spirit; this differs by running otherwise-standard deep RL over the generated reward rather than a symbolic motivation system.

## The experiments, and the boundary they hit

This repo used to say it had no experiments. It now has a short, honest track — [experiments/nursery/](experiments/nursery/) — run precisely because the thesis makes a testable *boundary* claim, and a boundary is worth **measuring** rather than asserting. Three things came out of it, and the honest framing is that **none is evidence *for* the distinctive thesis** — they confirm the mechanisms and leave the distinctive claim untested.

- **Grounding transfers (Phase 0).** Human-labelled **approach/avoid** is **linearly readable, zero-shot, from frozen features at AUC 0.95** (vs 0.50 chance) on held-out images — the *designed* read-out generalizing, the known part, **not** the developed value (the wall). It survives 64px, survives losing CLIP's language-alignment (a self-supervised encoder recovers it too), and transfers onto a *learned* world-model latent — but only in **early perception, not the recurrent state** (0.79–0.81 on the encoder embedding vs ~0.57 on the RSSM). That earned a concrete, evidence-based architecture change — the amygdala taps `z_enc`, pre-RSSM ([build/ARCHITECTURE.md](build/ARCHITECTURE.md)) — validated on static content and flagged for re-test on dynamic. *(Facial affect specifically, a subset.)*
- **The loop closes, and anti-wireheading works (Phase 1-lite / 1.5).** The generated reward drives an agent end-to-end; **habituation-as-RPE-decay** stops fixation on a predictable reward source — ablation-proven (remove it and the agent wireheads at ~3× the rate), and it survives an unfrozen backbone. Both are *known* mechanisms, confirmed — not novel.
- **The differentiator can't be shown cheaply (Phase 1.5) — and we tested our own excuse.** The grounded-vs-novelty contrast — *does felt reward pursue meaning where novelty chases noise* — did not appear. We suspected a setup artifact; we **pre-registered a test of that excuse, and it failed** — but the honest reading is narrower than "needs scale": our *test instrument* was mis-built (the noise trap fails to trap even a pure-novelty agent), so the differentiator was **never validly tested.** That's a **construct-validity failure, not a confirmation of Problem 1** — whether the differentiator requires large scale stays the thesis's **prediction, not a result.** We haven't even shown it *can't* be tested cheaply, only that our first cheap trap was broken.

**Why the rest still can't be tested small.** This architecture is designed for three things a toy environment does not contain — **reality** (a body living an open-ended life, where affect grounds on real faces and real damage and value is *earned* over time, not scored per episode), **imitation** (other beings to read, copy, and catch values from; a caregiver to scaffold the young agent), and **thought** (long, multi-step reasoning the felt signal has to steer). Take those away and **every system collapses into its standard-RL equivalent:** the amygdala → a plain shaped reward; the value system → any critic; habituation → plain RPE decay; imagine-and-feel → MPC; *feeling-steers-thinking* → nothing; *learning-from-others* → nothing. Phase 1.5 is *consistent with* this — but its trap was mis-built, so it shows our cheap test failed, not that the collapse itself was measured; the argument for the boundary stays theoretical.

**"Only works at scale" has the shape of an unfalsifiable claim — so it deserves suspicion, not faith.** The honest answer this repo can now give is only partway: everything testable below the wall was tested and behaved as designed — the *mechanisms* are confirmed. But the boundary itself is **not yet located empirically** — our one attempt to test the differentiator cheaply failed on a broken instrument, so *"it needs scale"* remains an **argument, not a measured result.** The claim stays concrete and falsifiable at scale — a felt agent should **(a)** keep developing with **no external reward** where standard RL stalls, and **(b)** refuse to wirehead (pursue open-ended variety) where a reward-maximizer collapses onto one stimulus — the test the build ends on (Stage 6). Fail it there and the thesis is wrong.

**Held honestly:** the learning core is regular RL — the two twists are that the **reward is generated, not given** and that it **habituates**, neither a new algorithm. The experiments confirm the *mechanisms*; they are **not** evidence for the distinctive thesis, which stays untested — and its boundary stays a *prediction*, not a measured result. The contribution isn't a method or a result; it's a **claim about where guidance comes from in the regime we have no method for** — with the hard parts named, and now the testable parts tested, rather than papered over.
