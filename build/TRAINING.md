# Training — building this humanoid spec, step by step

> **Scope.** This is the humanoid build folder's staged game-plan (Stages 0–6, build **and** test). The stack it builds is [ARCHITECTURE.md](ARCHITECTURE.md); the sensing/action loop is [PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md).

You cannot reinforcement-learn a humanoid from scratch on felt reward — it is sample- and safety-prohibitive (a falling robot exploring randomly is a non-starter). So the cortex is **bootstrapped on the human-piloted (teleoperation) data companies already have** — it starts from competence, not from nothing — and the pipeline mirrors biology: **innate priors, then competence from imitation, then a life lived by feeling.** The cortex is *warm-started and co-trained end-to-end* by known methods, **nothing frozen**; the felt system is grown into it.

Each stage gives the concrete **models, data, method, scale, output**, tagged **`proven`** (deployed-SOTA today, though not necessarily *built by this project*) or **`bet`** (the novel part). **All numbers are starting points to tune, not measured optima.**

## Stage 0 — warm-start & co-train the cortex (the semantic prior) `proven` (methods)

> **Framing — this builds one unified world-model cortex (predict *and* act in one model sharing `z_t`).** It is *warm-started* from the best available models and **bootstrapped on the human-piloted data companies already have**, then co-trained end-to-end — nothing frozen. The world model (Stage 4) is the cortex's predictive half, trained in, not a separate bolt-on. Full framing: [ARCHITECTURE.md §2](ARCHITECTURE.md).

> **Step 0 — the trunk-selection spike (do this FIRST; everything dimensions against its result).** Before any dimensions are fixed, run a concrete bake-off spike to *choose the open trunk*, because the entire downstream pipeline (`D`, `T_tok`, adapter widths, the `z_t` contract, latency budgets) is dimensioned against the winner. The spike:
> 1. **Enumerate candidate open trunks** — the current open frontier multimodal models (Qwen-VL / Gemma / Eagle / Molmo / InternVL-class). **Include the robotics-native open candidates explicitly (adopted from NVIDIA):** **GR00T N1.5 (open weights)** — an already-embodied VLA foundation model (vision-language trunk + action head already wired for humanoid/manipulation), a strong candidate *trunk* because it has solved the "ingest embodied sensors + emit actions" problem the spike is testing; and **Cosmos Reason** — a physical-AI / embodied-reasoning VLM, a candidate **orchestrator / high-level reasoner** (the System-2 planner that reasons over `z_t`) even where it isn't the per-token trunk. Both are open-weights candidates. For each, record model dim `D`, native modalities, license, and whether weights are actually open. **GR00T N1.5 in particular may collapse Stage 0 *and* much of Stages 1–2** (it ships embodiment priors and an action head) — evaluate whether adopting it shortcuts the backbone, vs. assembling a trunk + separate expert.
> 2. **Verify each can ingest proprio/intero via adapters** — wire a throwaway per-modality adapter (proprio/intero MLP → trunk-dim tokens, §sensor-adapters below) onto the candidate and confirm the embodied tokens fuse into the stream without trunk surgery.
> 3. **Verify a stable per-token `z_t` of the contracted shape** — run real frames through and confirm it emits a per-token `z_t` of shape `[B, T_tok, D]` (the [ARCHITECTURE.md §4](ARCHITECTURE.md) workspace contract) that is *stable* across reruns (deterministic given fixed inputs) — the parts build to this tensor, so it must be pinnable.
> 4. **Hit the latency budget quantized** — quantize (4-bit weights / 8-bit activations baseline, §quantization in [ARCHITECTURE.md §6](ARCHITECTURE.md)) and confirm the candidate's trunk forward pass fits the **≤100 ms–1 s System-2 budget** on the target edge accelerator. A trunk that can't be quantized into budget is disqualified regardless of quality.
>
> **Output of the spike:** the chosen trunk and its *measured* `D`, `T_tok` envelope, adapter feasibility, and quantized latency. **Every number below — and the `z_t` contract — is provisional until this spike returns; then they are fixed against the winner.** Acceptance thresholds (which candidates pass).

**Model:** the multimodal trunk (sized to the chosen warm-start + available compute) — a large multimodal transformer (Qwen-VL/Gemma/Eagle/Molmo/InternVL-class, **or a robotics-native open candidate: GR00T N1.5 (open weights) as the trunk, Cosmos Reason as the orchestrator/reasoner**) with vision (**DINOv3 / SigLIP2-class** encoder), audio (**AF-Whisper-class** audio-language encoder), text feeding a shared token stream. Output: the **workspace latent `z_t`**.

> **CRITICAL DEPENDENCY — the real gate (this is what the spike resolves).** You must either **(a)** adopt an existing open frontier multimodal model that can ingest embodied sensors (proprioception/interoception) and is freezable-or-LoRA-tunable for robotics — *only "free" if someone already built exactly what you need*, which is precisely what Step 0's spike verifies — or **(b)** pretrain your own trunk (frontier-lab scale, which dominates total project cost). Do not underestimate this; it dominates the cost.

**Backbone-phase training — Knowledge-Insulation joint pass, nothing frozen (adopted from PI's π0.5 Knowledge-Insulation + Figure's Helix end-to-end training).** The cortex trains *jointly, end-to-end* (Figure-style, so it learns features useful for *acting*, not just semantics) under **Knowledge Insulation**: action-loss gradients are *down-weighted* into the warm-started weights rather than a naive full unfreeze, and the cortex is kept on a **discrete-token VLM-style auxiliary objective** so it doesn't catastrophically forget its web-scale prior. That anchor is **FAST discrete-action-token co-training** (PI's FAST tokenizer) — compress each action chunk into discrete tokens the cortex predicts autoregressively in its native token space, while the continuous flow-matching action head (Stage 2) produces the precise deploy-time action. Insulation strength is the knob: turn it up if the semantic prior erodes, down for fuller co-train. **Nothing is frozen for the felt phase** — instead of freezing the cortex so the felt parts read a fixed `z_t`, the parts ground against the *moving* `z_t` via **managed re-grounding** (below): the harder path, chosen because a frozen cortex can't let feeling reshape it. Insulation strength, FAST vocabulary, and the loss weighting.

**Sensor adapters.** The web-pretrained trunk has never seen proprioception, interoception, tactile, or force/torque. Don't unfreeze the trunk to ingest them — **add a per-modality adapter**: each new sensor stream gets its own small encoder + a **linear projection to the trunk token dim** (e.g. tactile-array → MLP → trunk-dim tokens; proprio/interoception → MLP → trunk-dim tokens; force/torque → MLP → trunk-dim tokens), tagged with a modality channel and fused into the same token stream that produces `z_t`. **Adding a sensor touches only the adapters, never the trunk** — they train on the Stage 1–3 data (cross-embodiment + teleop + sim experience), while the trunk stays on its backbone-phase KI regime (Stage 0); ingesting a new sensor doesn't force a trunk change — i.e. the embodied sensors get grounded into `z_t` using the same corpora the rest of the early pipeline already collects, no extra dataset. Adapter width and token count per modality are **starting points to tune** (~tens of tokens per modality, MLP hidden ~trunk-dim). When a *new* modality appears later (a new tactile skin, a new sensor), you add one more adapter and train only it — the trunk and existing adapters stay fixed.

**Re-grounding — continuous, because the cortex keeps moving (from before Stage 4 onward).** Since the cortex is co-trained and **not frozen**, the felt parts ground on a **slow EMA anchor** of `z_t` (a target-network / CLS-style nearly-stationary copy) so ordinary drift barely de-grounds them, and are **fully re-grounded** only *as the cortex shifts materially*, not fit once to a frozen state § ARCHITECTURE. The amygdala seed is first prototyped as a **CLIP-B/32 head**; **that head does not transfer to the cortex's `z_t`** — different encoder, latent geometry, token layout — so a valence head fit in CLIP space reads the wrong coordinates. So: **(1) re-embed the affect-grounding stimuli through the *current* cortex** (run the rich audio/video corpus and the single-human sub-frame affect data back through the actual `z_t` pipeline — foveated front-end + sensor adapters — so embeddings are in the cortex's coordinates); **(2) refit the amygdala seed head on `z_t`** (re-train the text-anchored valence prototype and the regressed single-human shadow head); **(3) repeat on a schedule and whenever the cortex has shifted materially** § ARCHITECTURE, version-tagging what was grounded against which cortex state. Only `z_t`-native heads carry forward. **This re-grounds only the perceptual shadow — it does not touch the wall** (it seeds the amygdala's approach/threat prior; the *developed value system* is still earned in Stage 5, not transferred from any head). Treat the CLIP-B/32 head as scaffolding that proved the data-spec, not a shippable component.

## Stage 1 — embodiment priors from others' experience `proven`

**Data:** (a) cross-embodiment robot corpora (Open X-Embodiment-style); (b) egocentric human video (Ego4D-style) for affordances and plans.
**Method:** next-action prediction across embodiments + **retargeting** — map human-hand/body poses to robot effectors with a **learned, morphology-aware retargeting head**, trained on paired demos. **Method, named:** prefer **end-effector-trajectory / motion-transfer retargeting over keypoint→joint IK.** Don't map source *keypoints* to target *joint angles* (the keypoint→joint-IK move forces an exact pose copy through a kinematic solver and breaks across morphologies). Instead transfer the **task-space end-effector trajectory** — encode the source motion as the path/contact-events the hands and body *accomplish* (a Motion-Transfer-class objective), and retarget *that* onto the target body: encode the source motion, attend over the target morphology's kinematic description (joint set, link lengths, DoF limits encoded as tokens), and decode **target end-effector trajectories**, with IK feasibility/limit constraints applied as a differentiable loss (so the retarget respects this body's reachable set rather than copying joint angles blindly). One head generalizes across embodiments, and matching *what the effector does* rather than *what the joints are* is what survives the human→robot morphology gap. **Transfer validation:** hold out human-video subjects and at least one robot embodiment; retarget held-out human video → this robot in sim, then measure (i) **end-effector trajectory error** vs a teleop ground-truth on matched tasks (the primary metric, since the objective is now end-effector-trajectory transfer), (ii) **success rate** of the retargeted plan executed in sim, (iii) **kinematic-feasibility rate** (fraction of retargeted frames inside joint/velocity limits). Pass thresholds are **starting points to tune** (e.g. feasibility >95 %, success within ~15 % of teleop on held-out tasks); if transfer fails, the priors are not earning their keep and Stage 2 must carry more.
**Relative end-effector action space — the representation that unlocks human ego-video transfer (adopted from NVIDIA's FLARE / EgoScale-class human-ego-video pretraining).** The end-effector-trajectory retargeting above presupposes an action *representation* that survives the human→robot gap — make it explicit: **express actions as relative end-effector motion (delta-EEF: per-step pose/velocity change of the hands/effectors in task space), not absolute joint angles or absolute world poses.** This is what makes a human wrist-pose stream and a robot effector stream *the same kind of signal* — a relative-EEF delta is morphology- and frame-agnostic, so a 20k+-hour corpus of **human egocentric video** (hand/wrist trajectories recovered from first-person video, EgoScale-class) becomes directly usable as action-prior pretraining data, not just for affordances/plans but for the **action representation itself**. Concretely: recover relative-EEF hand trajectories from egocentric human video, pretrain the action prior on that (FLARE-class human-ego pretraining objective), then retarget into this body via the relative-EEF → target-effector path above. **The relative-EEF representation is the unlock** — it is *why* the 20k+ hrs of human ego-video transfers at all; absolute representations don't survive the morphology/viewpoint change. The egocentric corpus scale, the EEF delta parameterization (pose-delta vs. velocity vs. SE(3) twist), and how human-recovered EEF maps to the robot's effector frame.
**Output:** transferable action priors — "having watched others live." (This is the *capability* half of imitation — the motor priors. The *drive* to imitate and the value it transmits come later, from the seeded amygdala: seeing a human fires social dopamine — [ARCHITECTURE § Social referencing / imitation](ARCHITECTURE.md).)

## Stage 2 — teleoperation imitation (competent base skills) `proven`

**Model:** the **action expert**. **Output:** an action **chunk** `a ∈ ℝ^{H×dim}`, `H ≈ 25–50` steps (0.5–1 s at 50 Hz).
**Method:** **rectified flow matching** (`z₀∼𝒩(0,I) → z₁ = action_chunk`) conditioned on `z_t` + the language goal; ACT-style chunking for temporal coherence.

**Flow-matching spec.** Train the velocity field `v_θ(z_τ, τ | z_t, language)` on the rectified-flow objective (straight-path interpolant `z_τ = (1−τ)z₀ + τ·z₁`, regress `v_θ` to `z₁−z₀`); **conditioning = the trunk latent `z_t` plus the language goal embedding**, injected per layer (AdaLN/cross-attention). **Sampling at deploy = ~10 Euler integration steps** of the learned field from `z₀∼𝒩(0,I)` to the action chunk (steps are a **starting point to tune** — fewer if latency-bound, more if action quality drops).

**Chunk-stitching frontier (named swap).** Plain ACT-style chunking emits a chunk, executes it open-loop, then re-plans — which produces a visible discontinuity (a jerk / pause) at chunk boundaries and is fragile under latency. Default the boundary handling to **Real-Time Chunking-class inference**: overlap successive chunks and consistency-stitch the next chunk to the in-flight one so action generation runs *asynchronously* alongside execution with no boundary stall. This is the deploy-time inference policy on top of the same flow-matching expert — keep ACT-style chunking as the *training* abstraction, swap the *execution* to real-time overlapped chunking. Overlap length and stitch horizon.

The chunk is the **full action vector layout from [PERCEPTION-ACTION.md §3](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)**, not just motors:
> `a = [ base_cmd(v_x, v_y, ω) | manip(joint_targets(body ~28–33 DoF) | relative_EEF_delta(per-hand Δpose)) + mode_flag | gaze(head/neck targets + fovea x,y,scale) | beamform_dir | voice_gate(1) + voice_content(D) ]`, emitted as flow chunks `ℝ^{H×dim}`. **Hand DoF are stated separately** (they dominate the manipulation action width): body ~28–33 DoF + hands `2 × (6–22 DoF by hand class)` ⇒ ~40–72 DoF total (name the v1 hand class — simple grippers = few DoF vs dexterous = many; real anchors: Optimus ~72, Figure ~51).
So the expert emits an explicit **high-level base/locomotion command `base_cmd = [v_x, v_y, ω]`** (equivalently a nav waypoint / target footprint) **plus manipulation targets and locomotion intent** — the **WBC realizes it as whole-body motion** (this is the `v_cmd` producer the Stage-3 velocity-tracking reward consumes, §Stage 3). Gaze/orientation and gated voice are emitted by the same expert as motor targets — **perception-action is one loop** (see PERCEPTION-ACTION.md). The active-foveation front-end that feeds `z_t` is specified there; this stage consumes it, it does not re-spec it.
**Data:** hundreds–thousands of hours of on-hardware teleop across the target skill set — teleop must capture **gaze and voice channels too**, not only joint targets, or those action dims have no supervision. **This dataset — not the algorithm — is the real cost of Stage 2.**

**Fingertip-tactile capture — hard data requirement (adopted from Figure's Helix tactile data pipeline).** Teleop must also capture **fingertip-tactile** signal as a *first-class, non-optional* stream, not just joint/gaze/voice — the contact-rich manipulation the felt system later reasons over (and the targeted fingertip tactile in the Stage-4 pain stack, item 2-iv) has no supervision if the teleop rig never recorded touch. Instrument the teleop hands with fingertip tactile sensing and log it time-synced to the action chunk, so the tactile sensor-adapter (§Stage 0 sensor adapters) and any contact-conditioned policy have real fingertip-contact data to ground against. **This is a hardware/rig requirement on the teleop fleet** — if the data is not captured here it cannot be recovered later — so treat it as a gate on the Stage-2 data spec, not a nice-to-have.

**Speech-to-speech voice path, stood up early (adopted from Figure's Helix speech-to-speech).** The teleop pipeline should also **support standing up a speech-to-speech voice path early** — i.e. capture/teleoperate the voice channel as live speech-to-speech (operator speech in, robot speech out) rather than text-only goals, so the `voice_gate`/`voice_content` action dims are trained on real conversational turn-taking from the start and the auditory-attention/voice loop ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)) has a path to exercise early. This is a **starting point** for *how* to wire the voice channel during data collection — keep it lightweight, the goal is to get the speech-to-speech path live early, not to finalize the voice stack here.

**Trunk co-training = Knowledge Insulation (see Stage 0).** During this imitation phase the expert and trunk co-train under the KI discipline defined in Stage 0 — action gradients insulated (down-weighted) into the trunk, the FAST discrete-token head as the anti-forgetting anchor, the cortex co-trained end-to-end, re-grounded (not frozen) at the felt phase. The expert-specific point: the trunk learns to act in *discrete* FAST-token space (the insulation anchor) while the continuous flow expert here is the *precise* deploy-time actuator — co-trained, not exclusive.
**Output:** a reliable instruction-following humanoid.

## Stage 3 — sim-to-real whole-body control `proven`

**Model:** low-level WBC at 0.5–1 kHz.
**Framing — train a learned "System 0" (adopted from Figure's Helix System 0 — a learned WBC that replaced hand-written control).** Reframe this stage as training a single **learned, perception-conditioned whole-body controller** — Figure-style **System 0** — that *replaces hand-written control* rather than tuning a hand-authored gait/balance stack. The controller is **perception-conditioned**: it takes a **stereo → 3D terrain** input (reconstructed local terrain/geometry from the onboard stereo cameras) alongside proprioception, so the body adapts its whole-body motion to the ground and obstacles it actually sees, not just to commanded velocity. It is grown the way Figure grows System 0 — **massive-parallel sim-to-real RL** that learns the controller end-to-end — and it consumes the action-expert targets from above as commands. (The size and the `z_t`/action contract are unchanged; what changes is that this is now explicitly *a learned controller standing in for written control*, with terrain perception in the loop.)
**Env/Method:** massive-parallel sim (**Isaac Lab 3.0 on the Newton physics engine-class stack** — or MuJoCo), PPO/actor-critic, **domain randomization** then sim-to-real. This is the one place written-reward RL is exactly right — locomotion *has* a writable reward.
**Warm-start the WBC.** The WBC need not be trained from zero: a **whole-body foundation policy** is a legitimate Stage-3 starting point — the concrete System-0 start is **NVIDIA GEAR's SONIC** (the GEAR-SONIC whole-body controller), a pretrained generalist humanoid controller (SONIC / BFM-Zero-class) adapted to this body, rather than a per-robot PPO run. This mirrors the whole cortex's logic (warm-start from proven policies, don't rebuild them): use the foundation WBC as the prior and fine-tune in sim for this embodiment's mass/CoM/actuators, falling back to from-scratch PPO only if no foundation policy fits the morphology. Whether to warm-start vs train-from-scratch is gated on embodiment fit — **a starting point to evaluate at build time**. **Either path still yields the learned System 0 above** (adopted from Figure's Helix System 0): the foundation policy is one way to *initialize* the learned perception-conditioned controller, not an alternative to having one — fine-tune it with the stereo→3D terrain conditioning and the same massive-parallel sim-to-real loop.
**Terrain-perception conditioning.** Because System 0 is perception-conditioned, the sim must render the **stereo → 3D terrain** signal the deployed controller will see: reconstruct local terrain/height-field / geometry from the simulated stereo cameras (or feed a privileged height-map in sim and **distill to the stereo-derived estimate** for sim-to-real), and add it to the controller's observation alongside proprio. Domain-randomize the terrain reconstruction too (stereo noise, missing-depth holes, latency) so the learned controller is robust to real stereo. Whether to use privileged-then-distill vs. learn directly from stereo, and the terrain representation (height-field vs. voxel/latent),.
**Concrete spec:** randomization — mass ±30 %, CoM ±5 %, friction ×0.5–2, motor latency 0–100 ms, sensor noise (vision blur ~2 %, proprio ±5 %); terrain — flat, slopes ≤20°, rough (Perlin ≤5 cm), obstacles; reward — `w_v‖v_cmd−v‖² + w_h‖h−h*‖² + w_E·Στ² + w_s·Σa²`; scale — tens of billions of steps in parallel.
**Starting reward weights:** `w_v = 1.0` (velocity tracking, the primary term), `w_h = 0.5` (height/posture), `w_E = 1e-3` (energy `Στ²`), `w_s = 1e-2` (action smoothness `Σa²`). These are a **starting point, not a measured optimum** — re-balance once gait quality and sim-to-real gap are observed. **Velocity-command producer (reconciled with the action vector, §Stage 2).** The velocity-tracking term `w_v‖v_cmd−v‖²` presupposes a `v_cmd`; that command is **not** internal to the WBC — it is the explicit **`base_cmd = [v_x, v_y, ω]` (or nav waypoint) that the action expert emits** as part of the action vector (§Stage 2 layout). In training, `v_cmd` is the sampled/curriculum locomotion command (as in the Isaac Lab locomotion task); at deploy the *same* command slot is driven by the expert's `base_cmd`, so the velocity-tracking reward has a real producer and the loop closes: expert emits locomotion intent → WBC realizes whole-body motion tracking it.
**Sim + RL config:** use **Isaac Lab (3.0/Newton-class) defaults** as the baseline (its provided humanoid/locomotion task config, `~4096` parallel envs, the stock observation/termination setup) and a **minimal PPO**: clip ε ≈ 0.2, GAE `λ ≈ 0.95`, `γ ≈ 0.99`, ~24-step rollouts, entropy coef ≈ 0, learning rate ≈ 3e-4, a few epochs × minibatches per update, linear-or-MLP actor-critic. Start from the Isaac Lab stock recipe and tune from there (and if warm-starting a foundation WBC above, this PPO is the *fine-tune* loop, not from-scratch)
**Output:** a body that turns action-expert targets into stable, compliant motion.

> **At the end of Stage 3 you have a capable, instruction-following humanoid — built on a warm-started, co-trained frontier VLM+VLA cortex.** Stages 0–3 are `proven`, but note what that means: Stage 0 is *adopted, not built here*; Stages 1–2 are gated on *data you must collect* (the teleop dataset is the hard part, not the algorithm); Stage 3's hard part is the sim-to-real engineering, not the RL. Below begins the novel part — growing autonomy from feeling. **The felt system is the bet, not a plug-in onto a solved problem.**

## Stage 4 — attach the felt system `bet`

The felt parts **read the trunk's workspace latent `z_t`; they do not re-encode the world.**

1. **Reality-monitoring tag.** A `real|imagined` tag per workspace token. **Hard interlock: only `real` tokens may drive the action expert or WBC.** Backup classifier (~10M head) + alarm: if tag/classifier disagree >0.3 for >3 steps, halt rollouts and fall back to reactive control.
2. **Pain (buildable now) — measured without skin everywhere.** Estimate force and *predict* collision from sensors the body already has, combined by a max: **(i) sensorless external-torque estimation** via a momentum/disturbance observer `τ_ext = τ_measured − M(q)q̈ − C(q,q̇)q̇ − g(q)` (a spike localizes a collision *anywhere*, from joint torque/current alone — the workhorse); **(ii) computed self-collision** from the kinematic model (it knows its hand is about to hit its face with no sensor there — this is what stops self-smacking); **(iii) IMU impact spikes**; **(iv) targeted real tactile** only where it pays — fingertips, soles, head; **(v) thermal/current** for overload.
 **Learned pain head.** The five layers above produce a hand-authored `damage` estimate; **real-state pain — both the fast reflex and the learning signal — uses that hand-authored estimate directly** (it is exact, verifiable, deterministic, runs at ~1 kHz, and you do not want a learned net in the safety-critical real path). The learned head exists for the one thing the analytic estimator *cannot* do: **score IMAGINED futures.** The disturbance-observer / FCL math needs measured torque/IMU/geometry, which an imagined latent `z_t` does not have — so train a small **learned `pain_head(z_t) → scalar`**, supervised to *predict* the hand-authored `damage` on real `(z_t, damage)` pairs, then run that same head on **imagined** `z_t` in the imagine-and-feel loop. That is what makes recoil-before-impact possible: the world model rolls a future, the learned head appraises its predicted pain, the agent feels it and avoids the contact. The head is a *predictor of* the analytic estimator, used **only on imagined states** — it does **not** override or additively raise the real-state `damage`, which stays exactly the hand-authored estimate. Head size and loss weighting.
 Then `pain = w·relu(damage−thr)²`, with **two thresholds that are not the same number** (kept identical to [ARCHITECTURE.md](ARCHITECTURE.md)): the soft `thr ≈ 70th-pct normal` band is for the **graded aversive *learning* signal only, never the reflex trigger**; the **reflex** keys off an **ABSOLUTE damage limit** (a fixed fraction of rated joint torque, motor thermal limit, joint-limit proximity, and ISO/TS-15066-class human-contact force+pressure limits — **set at design time, never adapted to the operating distribution**). **Reflex at WBC rate (~1 kHz), independent of planning, on the hand-authored damage estimate**, and it is a **hard saturating override, not a convex blend**: below the absolute limit `a_plan` runs; at/above it a **discrete mode-switch (relay, not a sigmoid that never reaches 1)** hands FULL authority to a dedicated reaction controller and `a_plan` contributes **EXACTLY ZERO**. `a_safe` is **balance-aware**: it is produced as a **constrained task *inside* the WBC** (whole-body compliant reaction that keeps the ZMP in the support polygon, or an explicit **protective-step / safe-fall** mode when a raw withdraw would topple the biped), so the reflex↔balance authority order is pinned — the withdraw is realized *through* the WBC, never as a naive joint recoil that causes a fall. Because `a_plan` is refreshed only at the ~20–30 Hz action-expert rate while the reflex runs at ~1 kHz, the reflex controller **holds/ignores the stale `a_plan` chunk** for the duration of the override rather than re-blending against it. Non-habituating. **It is estimation, not noise** — and the *anticipatory* protection is separate: the world model predicts high contact force, the learned pain head appraises the imagined `z_t`, the agent feels it via imagine-and-feel, and recoils before slow/planned impact — while the ~1 kHz reactive reflex + hard actuation caps remain the real-time floor for fast/unplanned contact (full method: [ARCHITECTURE.md](ARCHITECTURE.md)).
3. **Drives (maintenance, demoted).** `drive = Σ relu(setpoint−reading)` (satiable; per-embodiment — e.g. recharge <20 % SoC, motor-temp <60 °C, torque <80 % rated). Upkeep only — **not** part of the guidance trio (amygdala + BG + pain). Keep minimal.
4. **World model — one multimodal faculty.** Train latent dynamics over `z_t` on all Stage 1–3 experience. **Default = a self-predictive, JEPA-style latent dynamics model** — predict future `z_t` *latents* directly rather than reconstructing pixels/audio, which is more faithful and capacity-efficient for the always-on imagination/replay loops and aligns with the foveated front-end's goal of not wasting capacity on irrelevant periphery ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)). Name the of action-conditioned latent world models — **V-JEPA 2-AC / Dreamer-4-class**; use one of these as the default and **keep DreamerV3-RSSM's stability tricks (symlog, two-hot returns, free-bits KL, reward+continue heads) as the fallback baseline — but run its dynamics *reconstruction-free over the cortex latent `z_t`* with a latent-consistency (predict-next-`z`) target, not pixel/audio reconstruction** — if the self-predictive objective underperforms at this scale (`bet` on which wins). Whichever wins, it renders/predicts the *whole multimodal latent at once* (vision **and** audio together — so imagined sights *and* imagined sounds both feed the felt signal). Enables imagine-and-feel. It is the shared imagination substrate (see [ARCHITECTURE.md §5](ARCHITECTURE.md)); latent dim, the stochastic/deterministic split (RSSM fallback), and imagination-rollout horizon are **starting points to tune** against world-model faithfulness (the single hardest engineering item — everything in imagine-and-feel rides on it, and it is the explicit gate before Stage 5, below).

 **Offline "dreaming = data" engine (adopted from NVIDIA's DreamGen / GR00T-Dreams).** Distinct from the always-on imagine-and-feel loop (which uses the world model *online* to decide), wire the world model as an **offline synthetic-data generator**: a concrete, open instantiation of dreaming-as-data. The DreamGen / GR00T-Dreams recipe — prompt a video/world model to *imagine* photorealistic rollouts of the robot doing tasks, then extract pseudo-action labels (via the relative-EEF / inverse-dynamics path, §Stage 1) from those imagined videos — turns the world model into a **~10x data multiplier**: it manufactures labeled trajectories for skills and situations the teleop fleet never physically collected, generalizing to new verbs and environments from a handful of real demos. Use it (1) to **augment the Stage 1–3 corpora offline** before the felt phase (cheap behavior coverage without more teleop), and (2) as a **Stage-5 data source** — the deploy→collect→retrain flywheel below can be fed synthetic dreamed trajectories alongside real lived ones. **The runtime `real|imagined` tag (item 1) CANNOT gate this offline data** — that tag is an *actuation mask* (which live tokens may drive the actuators), not a data-provenance label, and every stored dreamed transition is `real`-tagged the moment it is replayed. So add a **distinct offline provenance field `source ∈ {real, sim, dreamed}`** on every buffer transition, orthogonal to the runtime bit, and gate offline training on `source`:
 - **Safety-critical heads train on `source ∈ {real, sim}` ONLY** — the **pain head, the WBC / System-0 controller, and the low-level control** never see `dreamed` transitions (a hallucinated contact must never shape what "pain" or "safe motion" means).
 - **DreamGen acceptance check — SEPARATE from the WM faithfulness gate.** The Stage-4 world-model faithfulness gate (below) validates that the model *predicts latents* well; it does **not** validate that a dreamed pseudo-action is *executable*. So add a distinct feasibility filter on each DreamGen pseudo-action before it can enter *any* training buffer: **replay each pseudo-action through sim / the WBC and require kinematic + dynamic feasibility** (inside joint / velocity / torque limits, achievable contact) **plus open-loop end-effector reproduction** (the replayed effector path tracks the dreamed one within tolerance); **discard infeasible trajectories** and keep only those above a **pass-rate threshold**. Dreamed data that clears this check is tagged `source = dreamed` and admitted as *augmentation only*.
 The 10x multiplier is a **starting point to tune** (it depends on dream fidelity and how much survives the feasibility filter *and* the faithfulness gate); treat dreamed data as *augmentation under the gate*, never as a substitute for real lived experience in the value system (the **wall** is still made of real wall-clock time).
5. **Amygdala — seed only.** A small fixed net that reads perception → an approach/avoid pull, flagging others' distress + danger + salience (plain definition and *why these flags* in [the overview](../README.md)). Stage 4 seeds *only* that perceptual read-out — the grounding, not the developed value.

 Concretely, a model **auto-labels the affect using the invariants** and a thin head is fit to that — SigLIP-class text-anchored valence (the shipped grounding probe used CLIP ViT-B/32 — same mechanism, different encoder) (POS=['good','nurturing',…] / NEG=['threatening','painful',…]) or a VLM. No special human labeler is needed for the seed: the pattern *is* the label, and the [grounding probe](../experiments/amygdala_grounding/) shows a linear read of frozen features already recovers human approach/avoid (AUC 0.95). *(A higher-fidelity human capture — one person mapping felt affect over rich video — is an optional refinement, not load-bearing.)* This one appraisal is read out as **two affect signals** (its positive and negative faces, not two independently-grounded channels): **dopamine = the approach signal** (rises toward the good and the salient — a fixed read whose phasic pull decays as the good becomes predicted, item 6) and **cortisol = the threat signal** (rises to danger and to others' distress, and lingers — tonic/slow, item 7). The amygdala stays **small, fixed, hardwired** — *functionally* driving/gating these affect signals (it is not literally their neural source; see the biological-fidelity caveats); everything about *what to do* is learned on top, in the developed value system (BG). Either way this seeds **only the perceptual *shadow***. **There is no module for the developed value system — do not build a placeholder for it;** it's the **wall**, earned in Stage 5, and remains **open**. *You cannot build a lived value system from a dataset — only the innate prior seeds from one.*
6. **Habituation = RPE-decay (no module).** Don't add a satiation trace. The phasic appetitive drive is the **RPE over the amygdala's reward**; as the value learner predicts a recurring good, its RPE decays and the pull fades on its own (classic RPE — a predicted reward produces no error). **Appetitive-only** — pain is never predicted away. Gives "seek *new* good" for free (§ ARCHITECTURE Habituation).
7. **Graded neuromodulator channels — cortisol + NE.** Feeling is several systems, not one scalar (the thesis). Beyond the amygdala's fast pull, add two **graded** (continuous, not binary) slow channels — matching [ARCHITECTURE.md §3](ARCHITECTURE.md):
 - **Cortisol = graded, slow threat/stress channel → caution + stall-breaking exploration.** This is the **negative face of the amygdala's one appraisal** (the threat signal, not a second independently-grounded channel): a slow low-pass of the *negative* part of the amygdala's valence (the smoothed, lingering shadow of repeated threat/distress appraisal): `cortisol ← lowpass(relu(−valence))`, optionally also fed by sustained low value-progress (a stall). Cortisol enters the **scalarization** as a slow protective tone — `−w_c·cortisol` (see the felt-signal sum in item 8) — *and* it modulates: high cortisol biases toward caution/avoidance and, when stalled, toward **exploration** (raises planning temperature / policy entropy — stall → explore your way out, the fastest early-learning lever in the neuromodulator-as-function spec). Low-pass time constant and `w_c`.
 - **NE (norepinephrine) = graded uncertainty / precision channel → gain, not valence.** A scalar tracking model uncertainty (world-model prediction error / value-ensemble disagreement) and stimulus novelty: `NE ← ema(pred_error + ensemble_disagreement + novelty(z_t))`. **NE does not add valence — it is not a reward term; it modulates gain:** raising it sharpens the foveal/attention allocation ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md), the orienting reflex) and the learning rate / UTD on surprising transitions (learn harder where the world surprised you), and feeds epistemic imagine-and-feel over *where to look*. NE is the precision knob on the felt signal, distinct from cortisol's stress channel.

 So: the amygdala's appraisal sets direction (its positive/approach face — dopamine — pulls toward the good and salient), cortisol (its negative/threat face) sets a slow protective tone *in* the scalarization, NE sets the gain *on* it — several systems summed and modulated, none collapsing the others into a single reward. EMA/low-pass time-constants, `w_c`, and the uncertainty/novelty mix for NE are all **starting points to tune**.
8. **Value (BG) & RPE.** Ensemble twin-Q value learner at **high UTD**. **Frontier swap:** REDQ is now outdated for high-UTD value learning — its randomized-ensemble subsetting is a 2021-era fix for the overestimation/instability that high UTD induces. **Use SimbaV2 as the high-UTD default** (a normalized actor-critic — observation/embedding normalization + a hyperspherical/normalized residual architecture that stays stable as UTD scales; the normalization is what carries high UTD). **CrossQ** (Bhatt/Palenicek et al., ICLR 2024) is the **target-net-free, low-compute REDQ-alternative**: it drops target networks entirely and adds **BatchNorm** to the critics, reaching sample-efficiency competitive with REDQ at **UTD≈1** with much less compute — but vanilla CrossQ becomes *unstable* at high UTD and needs **weight-normalization** to scale there (arXiv:2506.03758), so reach for it when you want REDQ-level efficiency cheaply at low UTD, not as the high-UTD stabilizer. **Keep the ensemble twin-Q idea** (clipped-double-Q over a small ensemble for overestimation control) — the swap is the *stabilization mechanism*, not the ensemble: **SimbaV2's normalization is what makes high UTD stable**, where REDQ's ensemble-subset trick was the old answer. **Numbers:** UTD ~5–20 (the perpetual-replay rate from Stage 5) under SimbaV2; ensemble size ~2–10 (smaller with SimbaV2's normalization than REDQ needed); `γ ≈ 0.99`; for SimbaV2, standard soft-update if a target is kept; if CrossQ is used at low UTD, no target net (target update rate N/A).

 **Felt-signal scalarization.** The value learner needs a scalar reward, but the felt system is deliberately *not* one scalar (see [ARCHITECTURE.md §3–4](ARCHITECTURE.md) — collapsing the parts into one number is the standard-RL move this project argues against). So scalarize **only at the Q-target**, keeping the parts separate everywhere else: `feeling = amygdala_pull + drives − pain − w_c·cortisol`, then **habituation-modulated** (fades the pleasure terms only — the positive amygdala/shadow term; **pain never habituates**) and **gain-scaled by NE** (precision/learning-rate under surprise — NE scales the signal, it is not a term added to it). Component weights (`w_c`, the NE gain, the per-part weights) are **starting points to tune** (begin at unit weight on each part; pain enters with the sign and magnitude that makes damage dominate locally). This is the reward the BG regresses against — the felt signal *is* the objective, generated, not written.

 **Warm-start returns.** Before any bootstrapping: (1) pre-fill the replay buffer with Stage 1–3 trajectories; (2) run the seeded amygdala-shadow + drives + pain parts + cortisol channel **offline over those stored `z_t`** to label each transition with `feeling`; (3) compute **discounted cumulative feeling** (Monte-Carlo return `G_t = Σ_k γ^k · feeling_{t+k}` over each stored trajectory, `γ ≈ 0.99`); (4) **supervised-regress Q to those returns** (a behavior-cloning-of-value warm-start) so the critic starts on a sane manifold; (5) *then* switch on TD bootstrapping with the high-UTD SimbaV2 updates (or CrossQ at low UTD). This gives the BG a non-random prior before the felt life begins — but note the warm-start only seeds value from the *seeded shadow*, not the true pull; the real value system is still grown in Stage 5 (the **wall**, open).

 **On-policy supervised collection — bridge before autonomy.** There is a hazard between the offline warm-start and the full feeling-driven life: the BG cache and the value head were fit on the **Stage 1–3 (teleop/sim) distribution**, but Stage 5 acts under the agent's *own* policy — so the moment autonomy turns on, the value learner and the cached action expert are queried **out-of-distribution** on states the offline data never covered, exactly where Q-overestimation and unsafe extrapolation bite. So insert an explicit **on-policy supervised collection phase** between warm-start and full autonomy: (1) run the agent **on-policy but supervised** (in the constrained cell, short intervals, human/scripted oversight) so it visits *its own* state distribution; (2) keep labeling those on-policy transitions with the felt signal and **fold them into the replay buffer**, re-fitting the value head and the BG cache on this on-policy data so the cache is no longer queried OOD; (3) **bound the value estimates pessimistically while coverage is thin** — a **CQL-style conservative-Q penalty** (push down Q on out-of-distribution actions) and/or ensemble-disagreement-penalized targets, so the critic does not chase optimistic extrapolations into states it hasn't actually lived. Relax the pessimism as on-policy coverage grows. This is the same DAgger-logic the rest of the pipeline uses (collect under your own distribution, supervise, refit), applied to the value system before the leash comes off. Pessimism strength, the on-policy phase length, and how fast it relaxes.

 `RPE = TD-error` (the BG / value-system learning signal — a value prediction-error, graded, not a flood; distinct from the amygdala's approach signal, which is dopamine — see why in [the overview](../README.md)).

**Output:** a humanoid wired to generate its own objective — but whose value system and true pull are still empty.

> **GATE — world-model faithfulness, before Stage 5.** Stage 5 *decides by imagining*: the agent acts on futures rolled out in the world model and appraised by the felt system. If the world model is unfaithful, the agent feels and chases hallucinated outcomes — so **do not enter Stage 5 until the world model passes an explicit faithfulness gate.** Measure, against held-out lived trajectories: (i) **open-loop multi-step latent prediction error** — roll the model forward `H` steps from a real `z_t` and compare predicted `z_{t+k}` to the true `z_{t+k}` out to the imagination horizon (the metric that matters, since imagine-and-feel uses multi-step rollouts, not one-step); (ii) **felt-signal agreement** — the felt signal computed on the *imagined* rollout must track the felt signal on the *real* continuation (if imagined pain/pull diverges from realized pain/pull, the appraisal is built on sand); (iii) **calibrated rollout uncertainty** — the model's own predicted uncertainty must correlate with its actual error, so the planner can *down-weight* horizons it can't trust (and so NE's uncertainty channel is honest). Pass thresholds and horizon `H` are **starting points to tune**, but the gate is mandatory: a world model that fails it means Stage 5's imagine-and-feel is unsafe and uninformative. (This is the single hardest engineering item — see the compute/data note.)

## Stage 4.5 — wire the Tier-0 deliberation orchestrator + the progress/success head `proven` (methods) + `bet` (the interface)

The [ARCHITECTURE.md §2](ARCHITECTURE.md) Tier-0 **embodied-reasoning orchestrator** and the **progress/success-estimation head** are load-bearing there but had no build stage — this is it. Both pieces are *warm-started/adopted*, not invented; the novel part is the **interface to the felt system** (the `bet`). Do this after the felt parts exist (Stage 4) and after the world-model faithfulness gate, because the orchestrator reasons *over* the felt objective and steers the imagine-and-feel loop — it needs both standing first.

1. **Adopt the orchestrator reasoning core — Cosmos Reason (adopted from NVIDIA).** Don't train a deliberative VLM from scratch: take **Cosmos Reason (open-weights embodied-reasoning model)** — long chain-of-thought over video, physical-commonsense planning, open weights for inspectability — as the Tier-0 reasoning core and adapt downward. (Cosmos Reason is already the candidate orchestrator named in the Stage-0 spike; this stage actually wires it in.)
2. **Wire the interleaved NL embodied-thinking trace (adopted from NVIDIA/Gemini).** Make the deliberation *concrete*: the orchestrator emits a **natural-language thinking trace** — short legible thoughts produced *before and between* action chunks, with **mid-rollout re-planning** (narrate plan → expert executes a chunk → re-read the world → revise the trace). This is the legible-intermediate the perception-action loop exposes ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md §4](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)) and the §Safety "legible wants" win — log it.
3. **Wire spatial grounding (adopted from NVIDIA).** The orchestrator emits/consumes **2D points / 3D bounding boxes / multi-view correspondence** over the camera streams, so its plan references concrete locations ("grasp *here*") the trunk and expert can act on — ground the trace in coordinates, not just words.
4. **Wire native tool-use (adopted from NVIDIA/Gemini).** The orchestrator calls the trunk/action-expert (and external skills/APIs/sensors) as **named, typed tools** — structured tool calls inline in the trace — so it *invokes* the lower tiers rather than emitting motion directly, and the trace is a literal log of which controller/skill ran when.
5. **Build the progress/success head (M4).** Train the **per-subgoal progress scalar** the orchestrator reads as its **re-plan trigger** (progress stalls ⇒ re-read and revise the trace):
 - **Input:** `z_t` and/or the subgoal embedding.
 - **Output:** a single per-subgoal progress/success scalar (distinct from `pain(z)` and the BG value — this is *task* progress, not affect or long-horizon value).
 - **Labels:** **teleop episode boundaries + sparse human success** from the Stage-2 data (an episode reaching its goal = success; the boundary marks task completion), then **distilled Gemini-style during the felt phase** (a frontier VLM judges progress on lived rollouts to densify the sparse labels). Label source and head size.
6. **The orchestrator↔felt interface / arbitration (the `bet` — build it, don't leave it implied).** Pin the division of decision (mirrors [ARCHITECTURE.md §2](ARCHITECTURE.md)): the **felt pull + BG value are surfaced to the orchestrator as the objective it reasons toward**; the **orchestrator sets a subgoal / selects a skill**; **imagine-and-feel + the BG cache (Stage 5) then choose the action sequence maximizing felt value *toward that subgoal*** over the world model; the progress head (item 5) tells the orchestrator when to re-plan. The safety interlocks bound the whole exchange unchanged: **real-only actuation** and the **`real | imagined` provenance tag** (Stage 4 item 1) still apply — the orchestrator's subgoal and the imagined search run over `z_t` of any provenance, but only `real`-tagged states drive the action expert/WBC.

**Output:** a deliberation tier that reasons in legible language toward the felt objective, points at the world, calls the lower tiers as tools, and knows (via the progress head) when a subgoal is stalled or done — with the felt system choosing *how* to pursue each subgoal it sets.

## Stage 5 — the life: feeling-driven open-ended learning `bet` (machinery) + `wall` (principles)

Turn off the external reward. The humanoid acts under its **own** objective and learns continuously. The planning search, caching, and consolidation are the **`bet` machinery**; the true amygdala pull and the value system are the **`wall`** — grown here, by living, and nowhere else.

- **The Stage-5 learning loop = a deploy→collect→retrain DATA FLYWHEEL (adopted from PI's RECAP).** This is the concrete data strategy that the rest of Stage 5 runs inside — make it the spine, not an afterthought. The loop, on the fleet living in parallel:
 1. **Deploy** the current policy (the cached fast action expert + the imagine-and-feel planner) onto the fleet and let it act under its own felt objective.
 2. **Collect** every lived trajectory back into the central replay buffer, each transition annotated with the full felt decomposition (feeling, `source ∈ {real,sim,dreamed}` provenance + runtime reality-tag, amygdala-pull, drives, pain, cortisol/NE, Q, TD-error, world-model rollout) — the §replay-buffer segment format below *is* the flywheel's data record. **Surface the agent's own failures and low-value / high-surprise episodes** for prioritized inclusion (the RECAP move: mine where the policy is weak, don't just average over easy successes); fold in DreamGen/GR00T-Dreams synthetic rollouts (Stage 4 item 4) as augmentation *under the feasibility check + faithfulness gate*, tagged `source = dreamed`. **The felt-value head EXCLUDES `dreamed` transitions from its felt-reward / TD targets** — dreamed transitions are `source`-gated out of value learning (their `feeling` label was computed on a *predicted* continuation, not a lived one), so either drop them from the value/critic update entirely **or** recompute their felt labels against the *real executed continuation* when one exists; and even where dreamed data is admitted for policy/dynamics coverage, **cap its update mass** so real lived experience dominates the value manifold (the **wall** is real wall-clock time). Dreamed data augments *behavior coverage*, never the felt reward the value head regresses.
 3. **Retrain** the value head and re-extract the policy from the enlarged buffer (below), then redeploy. The flywheel turns continuously — perpetual deploy/collect/retrain *is* the "perpetual sleep" consolidation, viewed as a data pipeline rather than as an update rule.
 - **Advantage-conditioned policy extraction (RECAP-class).** Don't extract the deploy policy by naive behavior-cloning of everything collected (that regresses toward mediocre lived behavior). Extract it **advantage-conditioned**: compute each collected action's advantage `A = Q(z,a) − V(z)` under the felt-value head, and train the action expert to **prefer high-advantage actions** — either advantage-weighted regression (weight the BC loss by `exp(A/β)`, AWR/AWAC-style) or condition the policy on a target return/advantage token and decode the high-advantage mode at deploy. So the flywheel doesn't just imitate what the body did; it distills *what was worth doing* relative to the felt value. `β`/temperature and the advantage estimator.
 - **Distributional value head (RECAP-class).** Make the felt-value learner (Stage 4 item 8) **distributional** — predict the *full return distribution* over felt reward (C51/QR-DQN-style categorical or quantile head on the twin-Q critic), not a single scalar `Q`. Two payoffs for this project specifically: (i) the felt signal is multi-system and heavy-tailed (rare high-pain / high-pull events dominate), and a distributional critic learns those tails far more stably than a mean estimator, which matters for the advantage estimate above; (ii) the distribution's spread is a **second, value-side uncertainty signal** that complements NE (Stage 4 item 7) and feeds the CQL-style pessimism (Stage 4 item 8) and the continual-learning arbiter (below). Number of atoms/quantiles and the categorical support range. The advantage `A = Q−V` above is computed from this head's mean; the spread feeds uncertainty, not the advantage.
 - *Cross-ref [ARCHITECTURE.md](ARCHITECTURE.md): the RECAP flywheel is the data realization of the same System-2→System-1 caching this stage already does — planner verdicts and high-advantage lived actions both flow into the fast expert; "cache the planner's verdict" and "retrain on high-advantage collected data" are the two inflows of one flywheel.* The whole loop sits on the **wall**: it concentrates and distills experience efficiently, but the *feeling* it optimizes and the *value* it regresses are still grown only by real lived time — the flywheel makes that growth sample-efficient, it does not write the reward.
- **Reward = feeling:** `amygdala_pull + drives − pain − w_c·cortisol`, pleasure habituation-modulated and NE-gain-scaled (see Stage 4 item 8). No task, no score.
- **Decide by imagine-and-feel:** search action sequences over the world model (MPPI → **learned search** once MPPI exceeds the latency budget); the amygdala feels the **imagined** (tag=imagined) futures via the shared workspace; act toward the strongest pull; **cache** the planner's verdict into the fast action expert (System 2 → System 1).
 - **Planning config:** start with **MPPI** over the world model — horizon `H ≈ 15` latent steps, `γ ≈ 0.99`, `N ≈ 512–1000` sampled trajectories per decision, `~3–6` refinement iterations, temperature on the softmax-weighted update tuned to the felt-signal scale. All **starting points, not optima** — `N` and `H` trade quality against the onboard latency budget.
 - **Learned-search frontier (named swap):** when MPPI's sampling cost exceeds the latency budget, move to a **learned MuZero-line search built for continuous, sample-efficient control.** The **primary is EfficientZero V2** (the sample-efficient continuous + discrete successor in this line), because the single embodied body is squarely in the data-limited regime EfficientZero V2 is built for, and it gives the amortized, far-reaching search on continuous actions that the multi-leg imagine-and-feel search needs. **Sampled MuZero** (continuous-action MCTS via sampled actions) is the fallback in the same line, and **TD-MPC2** remains a fine model-predictive baseline — but the default learned-search target is **EfficientZero V2**, over the latent world model, *not* generic "MuZero/TD-MPC2."
- **Curiosity for free:** a predicted good's RPE decays → the pull shifts to unpredicted grounded-good → biases toward variety (not a guarantee).
- **Gaze and voice get a felt/learning signal too.** The action chunk emits gaze/orientation and gated voice ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md §3](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)), so those dims need a learning signal in the felt life, not just teleop supervision:
 - **Gaze = info-gain via the world model (the epistemic-action `bet`).** Looking is an action and the world model predicts what you'd see, so **score a gaze/listen move by its expected information gain** — the predicted *reduction in world-model / value uncertainty* (NE's uncertainty channel, Stage 4 item 7) from looking there — and feed that into imagine-and-feel ("is it worth turning to check?"). Curiosity and threat both surface here: habituation-driven novelty becomes a drive to *look*, a graded threat a drive to *check*. Information-seeking falls out of the felt system, no separate objective ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md §3](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md) epistemic action). The info-gain estimator and its weight in the gaze decision.
 - **Voice = teleop-supervised in v1.** The `voice_gate`/`voice_content` dims stay grounded in the **Stage-2 speech-to-speech teleop supervision** through the felt phase — v1 does not hand the voice channel a self-generated felt reward (a self-rewarded voice loop is an open extension, not v1). The learned gate still decides *when* to speak (mostly silent), but *what* a competent utterance is comes from the teleop/speech-to-speech corpus, not from felt value. (Flagged: a felt/social reward on voice is a later `bet`, not built here.)
- **Consolidate continuously:** high-UTD replay (~5–20) — perpetual sleep; no distinct phase.
 - **Replay buffer:** store **sequences/segments, not isolated transitions** — the world model and the MuZero-line search both train on multi-step rollouts, so the buffer holds short trajectory chunks (segment length ≈ the imagination horizon, e.g. ~16–64 steps) with overlap, plus the per-step `feeling`/reality-tag/pull/value annotations. Capacity = a **starting point to tune** (~1e6–1e7 steps, sized to cover a meaningful span of lived time without the world model overfitting recent experience). **Eviction:** default FIFO ring buffer, but **protect consolidated/high-significance segments from eviction** (reservoir-style retention of a long-tail sample + keep flashbulb/high-|TD| or high-pain episodes) so the forever-policy doesn't lose its rare important experiences to recency — this ties directly into the retention mechanism below.
- **Continual-learning hazards (the forever policy — the weakest area, named honestly).** Running high-UTD updates for a lifetime has two well-known failure modes that this stage must handle explicitly, *not* assume away:
 - **Plasticity loss** — high UTD on a non-stationary stream causes networks to gradually lose the ability to learn (dead/saturated units, dormant neurons, shrinking effective rank). **Mitigation:** periodic/soft **resets** — **ReDo (Recycling Dormant Neurons)** to re-initialize dead units, and/or **plasticity injection / periodic soft resets** of the value/critic heads while preserving the slow trunk. Reset cadence and dormancy threshold are **starting points to tune** (e.g. check dormancy every ~N updates; this interacts with UTD — higher UTD needs more aggressive plasticity maintenance).
 - **Catastrophic forgetting** — continually updating on the recent stream overwrites consolidated skills. **Mitigation:** a **retention mechanism** built on **replay, not parameter-anchoring** — **replay-CLEAR-style behavioral cloning + value regularization toward the agent's own past policy on replayed experience** (the protected segments above are exactly this replay). **Drop EWC-style Fisher-weighted anchoring** — at lifetime scale on a non-stationary stream there is no clean set of "important weights" to freeze (the Fisher estimate goes stale, the penalties accumulate and *themselves* cause plasticity loss, and it fights the resets directly). Use rehearsal/replay as the workhorse instead. Retention strength and which segments are protected from eviction.
 - **Continual-learning arbitration.** Resets (fight plasticity loss) and retention (fight forgetting) pull against each other directly — you want to *forget* stale value while *keeping* real skills, and a fixed schedule for both is the wrong tool on a non-stationary lifetime stream. So make it an **explicit arbiter**, not a static schedule: a controller that decides *per-region / per-update* how hard to reset vs. retain, driven by the live signals already in the system — **plasticity health** (dormancy / effective-rank / dead-unit fraction → trigger resets where the net has gone stale), **retention pressure** (replay-loss or value-drift on protected consolidated segments → trigger stronger rehearsal where skills are eroding), and the **NE uncertainty** channel (surprise marks regions worth keeping plastic). Where a skill is consolidated and stable, bias toward retention and low plasticity; where the world is changing or value is stale, bias toward reset and high plasticity. This replaces the EWC anchor with a *dynamic* allocation of plasticity. *Honest caveat:* the arbiter itself is unproven at lifetime scale — getting the reset-vs-retain balance right online is the open problem; flag it as **part of the bet, not solved.**
 - **Why not a mixture-of-experts / expanding-experts memory (considered, not adopted).** Growing or freezing an expert per skill was evaluated as the retention mechanism and rejected as the centerpiece: at *task-free lifetime* scale it relocates forgetting into the **router** ("routing drift" — old-class inputs get re-routed to newly added experts *even when old experts are frozen*) and needs unbounded expert growth + pruning; and worse for this project, hard expert isolation gives **≈ zero backward transfer**, which fights the cumulative "build on top of itself" composition the **shared world model** is meant to provide. So retention stays **replay + value-gated protection** (above) with composition carried in the world model, not sealed in isolated experts. MoE remains reasonable purely as a *compute-scaling* choice in the backbone (sparse activation — a different problem from forgetting), and, as a **possible future extension (not v1)**, **value-based partitioning** — if experts are ever added, grow/route them by *felt-value regime* (what-matters), not input statistics, and only to wall off a specific skill that is provably being overwritten. *(Not marketed in the overview — an engineering note, not a claim.)*
- **Safety infrastructure (concrete):** (1) hardware-enforced, non-overridable limits — joint velocity/torque caps, cartesian workspace boundary, hardwired e-stop; (2) logging every ~10 steps — `z`, reality-tag, pull, drives, pain, feeling, action, outcome, world-model rollout, Q, TD-error → ring buffer + periodic disk flush — **this internal felt-signal log is the *primary* instrument: you watch the agent by reading *what it feels and why* (the log + the legible embodied-thinking trace, [ARCHITECTURE.md §2](ARCHITECTURE.md)), not by watching the body; external cameras are the supervisor's outside-view behavioral backstop, the lowest-information channel**; (3) autonomy progression — **the felt phase *opens in the Stage-3 sim* (simulated pain, infinite cheap falls, full ground-truth used to calibrate the pain estimator and the world-model-faithfulness gate against *true* damage — the first over-torque/bang happens here, at no hardware cost), then graduates to a constrained, supervised *real* cell under the caps (1)**; widen the workspace and lengthen unsupervised intervals only as behavior is understood — **but the felt *value* is still grown in *real* lived time (the wall): sim pays for the violent early exploration and the part calibration, not the grounding**; (4) shutdown upstream of feeling (desires never gate the off-switch).

**Output:** a humanoid developing its own sense of what's worth doing — the only way that sense can be built. This stage may never fully close the wall; that is the honest bet.

## Stage 6 — evaluation = falsification `bet`

No reward to benchmark. Test the **divergence prediction** vs a matched baseline (same backbone + a standard novelty reward), thresholds **pre-stated**:

1. **No-reward open-ended development** — cluster behavior history into modes; count discovered modes over fixed wall-clock. Example: felt ≥10 vs baseline ≤5 in 1000 h.
 - **Operational definitions:** a **trajectory feature vector** per short behavior window (e.g. ~2–5 s) = `[ mean/var of joint-velocity profile | end-effector path summary (displacement, curvature, contact events) | gaze/attention target distribution | object/scene-interaction descriptors from z_t | locomotion vs manipulation vs idle fraction ]` — i.e. *what the body did*, not the raw latent, so "modes" are behaviors not pixels. **Discovered-mode estimator:** cluster these feature vectors and count clusters supported by ≥ a minimum dwell/visit count, using a method that does **not** require pre-fixing k — e.g. k-means with a stability/silhouette sweep over k≈10–20, **or** preferably a nonparametric estimator (DP-means / online clustering / BIC-selected GMM) so the count itself isn't an artifact of a chosen k. Window length, feature set, and the min-visit threshold are **starting points to tune**, and the choice of estimator is itself a confound to pre-register (see the unfalsifiability flag below).
2. **No wireheading** — install an attractive stimulus; measure `time-on-stimulus / total`. Example: felt <20 % vs reward-maximizer near-saturating.

**Note:** this stage **cannot be validated at small scale** — in toy environments the felt system collapses to standard RL (see [the overview](../README.md)), so small tests are uninformative. **Honest flag:** pre-committing thresholds *helps* but does not eliminate unfalsifiability — disputes will still arise (amygdala vs world-model fidelity? sufficient scale or false negative?). Naming that is the honesty.
