# Architecture — this humanoid spec

The full stack, from sensors to actuators, with the felt system attached as a separate module reading `z_t`. Two halves: the **cortex** (built on proven robot-foundation-model methods, warm-started then co-trained) and the **felt system** (the bet — the [root thesis](../README.md) guiding principle, embodied).

*Every external model/method named here (DINOv3, SigLIP 2, V-JEPA-2-AC, SimbaV2, RECAP, DreamGen, GEAR-SONIC, …) is the current SOTA generation as of writing and points to a fast-moving line — verify the exact version and availability at build time; the line matters more than the specific checkpoint.*

## 1 · Sensing

A humanoid is a partially-observed system; it needs rich, time-extended sensing.

- **Vision** — multiple cameras: head-mounted stereo (egocentric), wrist cameras for manipulation, wide-FOV for situational awareness. Encoded by a pretrained vision encoder (ViT / **DINOv3** / **SigLIP 2**-class — consider a **C-RADIO**-style unified encoder that distills multiple teachers into one backbone), and **foveated, not uniform high-res** — a wide low-res peripheral stream plus a movable high-res foveal crop aimed by attention. Full active-perception design (vision, audio, and outputs) in **[PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)**.
- **Audio** — microphones for speech, environmental sound, and contact acoustics. Pretrained audio encoder (**AF-Whisper** / Audio Flamingo 3-class).
- **Touch & proprioception** — joint encoders, IMUs, and tactile arrays on hands/feet, plus force/torque at wrists and ankles. Not optional: contact-rich manipulation and balance are impossible without them. Encode tactile through a **semantic-aligned tactile encoder** (a learned encoder that maps touch into the shared latent alongside vision/language — e.g. a touch-vision-language-aligned tactile backbone), **not** a raw per-taxel MLP; this lets contact signals carry meaning the trunk and amygdala can read, the same way the vision encoder does (`standard` direction). **Fingertip tactile is a hard requirement, fused end-to-end (adopted from Figure's Helix — fingertip tactile fused into the policy).** Upgrade from "tactile where it pays": **high-sensitivity fingertip sensing (~3-gram-class detection — a starting point to tune)** is a **hard requirement**, not a strategic option, and its signal is **fused end-to-end into the policy** through the semantic-aligned encoder above (kept), not bolted on as a separate threshold. Figure proves this is buildable *and* load-bearing for contact-rich manipulation — fine-grained fingertip force is what lets a hand grasp, slip-detect, and modulate force on delicate objects. (The sparse-but-strategic targeting in §3 still applies to *non-fingertip* surfaces — soles, head/face; fingertips are now a floor, not a trade-off.)
- **Visual proprioception** — the agent **sees its own hands and body in-frame** and uses that to self-calibrate (adopted from Figure). Observing its own limbs in the camera stream lets it close the loop between commanded pose and *seen* pose — correcting kinematic drift, calibration error, and wear without external instrumentation. It is cheap (no added hardware — it reuses the §1 cameras) and **fleet-scalable** (every body self-calibrates from its own video, so calibration improves across a fleet without manual per-robot tuning). This complements joint-encoder proprioception rather than replacing it: encoders give the internal estimate, vision grounds it against the world (`standard` direction, adopted from Figure).
- **Interoception** — the body's own state (battery/energy, actuator temperature and load, structural strain). This is what the **drives** and **pain** parts read.

All exteroceptive streams are tokenized and fused; interoception feeds the felt system directly.

## 2 · The cortex — one unified predict-and-act model

> **The architecture is one unified predict-and-act cortex — a single model that perceives, predicts, and acts over a shared latent `z_t`, trained end-to-end.** It is not an assembly of frozen borrowed networks. It is **bootstrapped by running the human-piloted (teleoperation) data companies already have through it** — so it starts from competence, not from nothing — and then trained on lived experience. Crucially **nothing stays frozen**: the felt system shapes the *whole* cortex, because a frozen cortex could never let feeling mould what it attends to and comes to value, which is the thesis. The “tiers” below are **functional roles inside that one model** — a slow deliberative role, a perception-and-prediction role, a fast action role — which best current methods (GR00T, π0, Helix, JEPA/Cosmos) are *warm-started from* and then co-trained, not separate frozen tiers bolted together. (One reconciliation: this rejects the **LLM as the sensorimotor cortex** — language is the wrong substrate for the felt, embodied core — but keeps an **LLM-class deliberation role** on top, the orchestrator below.)

The current frontier for general robot control is the **vision-language-action (VLA)** foundation model, organized as coupled systems running at different speeds — the pattern shared by GR00T, π0, and Helix-class systems. The original dual-system split (trunk + action expert) is now generalizing to a **three-tier** organization in the current frontier: an **embodied-reasoning orchestrator** that does slow deliberate reasoning and task decomposition, a **thinking trunk** that fuses modalities into the shared latent, and an **action expert** that emits motion. The tiers below map onto this; the felt system **attaches as a separate module reading `z_t`** above all of them.

**Figure's Helix validates this tiered shape (independent convergence).** Figure's Helix stack — a slow deliberative tier handing intent to a fast onboard action layer, with a learned System 0 controller beneath — arrived at the same coupled-tier organization independently of GR00T/π0, which is corroborating evidence that the tiered-VLA shape this cortex adopts is the right one (not a single-lab artifact). Several of the specifics below are adopted directly from Helix and flagged inline as such.

### Tier 0 — embodied-reasoning orchestrator (slowest)
A reasoning layer that sits *above* the trunk: it does explicit, deliberate task reasoning — multi-step planning, tool/skill selection, long-horizon goal decomposition — at the slowest rate, handing structured intent down to the trunk. This is the "System-2-of-System-2" the frontier has split out from the single multimodal trunk; in the dual-system framing its work lived inside the trunk's semantic-reasoning head, now made its own tier. It is where the agent's *deliberation* lives; the amygdala's appraisal and imagine-and-feel (§3) feed it the objective it reasons toward — **and the amygdala judges the reasoning *itself*, not just the goal:** because the orchestrator's embodied-thinking trace and inner speech are tokens in `z_t`, the amygdala re-scans each thought / branch (promising vs. dead-end) and steers which lines it pursues, pruning the search over thought-space the way imagine-and-feel prunes over imagined futures (the `real|imagined` mask still gates action). This is the LLM-class deliberation tier *over* the cortex — accepted as the orchestrator though rejected as the sensorimotor skeleton (see the §2 framing note). (`standard` direction)

**The orchestrator↔felt interface / arbitration (build it, don't leave it implied).** Pin how the deliberation tier and the felt system divide the decision, because both produce "what to do" and the boundary must be explicit: **the felt appraisal + the BG value are surfaced to the orchestrator as the objective it reasons toward** (not a command it obeys, an objective it deliberates over); **the orchestrator sets a subgoal / selects a skill**; **imagine-and-feel + the BG cache (§3) then choose the concrete action sequence that maximizes felt value *toward that subgoal*** over the world model. So the orchestrator picks *where to go*, the felt system picks *how to get there by feeling the futures*, and the felt value is also the orchestrator's own criterion. The safety interlocks still bound the whole exchange: **real-only actuation** and the **`real | imagined` provenance tag** (§3, §4) apply unchanged — the orchestrator's subgoal and the imagined search run over `z_t` of any provenance, but only `real`-tagged states ever drive the action expert. (Build stage: TRAINING Stage 4.5.)

**Warm-start the deliberative role — don't train it from random init (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA).** The deliberative role is *warm-started* from a strong reasoning VLM and then co-trained into the cortex — not trained from scratch, and not frozen. The named starting-point backbone is **Cosmos Reason (NVIDIA)** — a strong open-weights embodied-reasoning model built for exactly this tier: long chain-of-thought over video, explicit spatial/temporal grounding, and physical-commonsense planning. Use it as the Tier-0 orchestrator's reasoning core and adapt downward; don't reinvent a deliberative VLM. Its open weights also keep the deliberation tier inspectable, which the §Safety story wants.

**The concrete Tier-0 mechanism — an interleaved embodied-thinking trace (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA/Gemini).** Make the orchestrator's deliberation *concrete* (not an abstract "reasoning layer") by having it emit an **interleaved natural-language embodied-thinking trace** — short legible thoughts produced *before and between* action chunks, with **mid-rollout re-planning**: the orchestrator narrates its plan, the action expert executes a chunk, and the orchestrator re-reads the world and revises the trace before the next chunk rather than committing the whole plan up front. This is what closes the Tier-0 loop in practice — the trunk and expert run their fast tempos while the orchestrator thinks in language on its slow clock and steers between chunks. It is also a **free observability/safety win**: the trace makes the agent's *wants and reasoning legible in plain language* (logged and inspectable), which directly serves the §Safety "legible wants" goal — the felt appraisal supplies the objective, the trace exposes the deliberation toward it.

**Spatial grounding + a progress/success-estimation head (currently ABSENT, load-bearing).** The orchestrator cannot close its loop on language alone — it needs to *point at the world* and *know whether it is making progress*. Add two pieces, both currently missing from this stack and both load-bearing:
1. **Explicit spatial grounding** — the orchestrator emits and consumes **2D points / 3D bounding boxes / multi-view correspondence** over the camera streams, so its plan references concrete locations ("grasp *here*", "the gap is *there*") that the trunk and expert can act on. This is the spatial half of Cosmos-Reason-class grounding; ground the trace in coordinates, not just words.
2. **A progress / success-estimation head** — a **success detector** the orchestrator reads to judge whether a step worked, when to re-plan, and when a subgoal is done. Without it the mid-rollout re-planning above has no feedback signal. It is a small head whose **input is `z_t` and/or the subgoal embedding** and whose **output is a per-subgoal progress scalar**; it is **consumed by the orchestrator as its re-plan trigger** (progress stalls ⇒ re-read the world and revise the trace). Labels come from **teleop episode boundaries + sparse human success in Stage 2, distilled Gemini-style during the felt phase** (build details + build stage: TRAINING Stage 4.5). (This head is distinct from, and complementary to, the felt `pain(z)` head and the BG value: it estimates *task* progress/success, not affect or long-horizon value.)
Both are `standard` direction (adopted from NVIDIA)

**The orchestrator calls the controller as a tool (native tool-use interface, `standard`, adopted from NVIDIA/Gemini).** The interface from Tier 0 down is **native tool-use**: the orchestrator treats the trunk/action-expert (and external skills, APIs, and sensors) as **tools it calls**, emitting structured tool calls inline in its embodied-thinking trace and consuming their results. "Orchestrator calls controller-as-a-tool" is the concrete handoff — the deliberative tier doesn't directly emit motion, it *invokes* the lower tiers as named, typed tools, which keeps the boundary clean and makes the trace (above) a literal log of which controller/skill was called when.

### System 2 — the thinking trunk
A large multimodal transformer that fuses vision, audio, touch, proprioception, and language over a **time window** (seconds of history = working memory). It carries internet-scale semantic priors from VLM pretraining and produces:
- a **latent world state** — the representation everything downstream reads, and
- **semantic reasoning** — goal understanding, language grounding, task decomposition (now shared with / handed up to the Tier-0 orchestrator).

Runs slowly (~1–10 Hz): it sets *intent*, not joint torques.

**Sensor adapters (a web-pretrained warm-start has no body).** A web-pretrained trunk ingests vision/audio/language but has **no native channel for proprioception or interoception** — joint encoders, IMUs, tactile arrays, force/torque, battery/thermal/strain. Add a small per-modality **adapter**: a lightweight encoder (MLP / small transformer per stream) that projects each non-VLM sensor into the trunk's token space `D` and tags it with a learned modality embedding, so proprio/intero arrive as just more tokens the trunk attends over. The adapters are trained alongside the cortex; this is the standard "tokenize a new modality into a pretrained backbone" move (LLaVA-style projector, but for body sense). Token counts per stream are starting points to tune — see the §4 contract.

### System 1 — the action expert
A **flow-matching / diffusion** policy that consumes the trunk's latent state and emits **action chunks** — short sequences of **manipulation targets plus an explicit locomotion intent** — which the WBC then realizes as whole-body motion. The action vector carries **manipulation targets (joint/EEF)** *and* an explicit high-level **base/locomotion command `base_cmd:[v_x, v_y, ω]`** (equivalently a nav waypoint / target footprint); the expert emits *manipulation targets + locomotion intent*, and the **whole-body controller produces the actual whole-body motion** from them. This is what supplies the velocity command the velocity-tracking WBC reward (TRAINING `w_v=1.0`) tracks — without it that reward has no command producer. The chunks are emitted at the action-chunk sample rate (~50–200 Hz, see §6 for the re-plan vs. sample-rate distinction). Action chunking gives temporal coherence; flow matching handles the multimodal, high-DoF continuous action distribution that plain regression can't. This is the manipulation/skill layer.

The chunking scheme is the frontier here: plain ACT-style chunking re-plans each chunk from scratch and stutters at chunk boundaries, so move to **Real-Time Chunking (RTC)** — overlapping, asynchronously-refreshed chunks that stay coherent across boundaries and tolerate inference latency, the right fit for a partly-tethered trunk feeding an onboard expert.

**Action representation — add a relative / state-relative end-effector mode (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA EgoScale).** The default action target here is an **absolute joint-target vector** (the WBC tracks commanded poses). Add a **relative / state-relative end-effector (EEF) action mode** alongside it: actions expressed as *deltas of the end-effector pose relative to the current state* rather than absolute joint angles. This is the action space NVIDIA's EgoScale uses, and it is **what makes human ego-video transfer**: a relative-EEF action is **shared across human↔robot embodiments** (a human reaching and a robot reaching produce the same relative-EEF trajectory even with different kinematics), so the vast pool of human egocentric video becomes usable demonstration data for the expert — which an absolute-joint-target space, tied to one body's kinematics, cannot ingest. Keep absolute joint targets as the WBC-facing representation; add relative-EEF as the **policy-facing mode that unlocks ego-video pretraining**.

**Two conditioning paths into the expert — the rich workspace *and* a cheap async intent latent (adopted from Figure's Helix S2→S1 handoff).** The action expert is conditioned on the trunk in **two** ways, kept distinct on purpose:
1. **The full `z_t` workspace (kept, the rich path).** The trunk's working-memory state — the shared workspace the felt parts and world model read and write (§4). This is the high-bandwidth representation; it is *not* given up.
2. **A compact intent latent (added, the cheap async fast-path).** Alongside the workspace, the S2/trunk distills a **compact intent latent** — a small projected vector summarizing current intent — that is **projected into and concatenated with the action expert's token stream** and **updated asynchronously, in the background, on a decoupled clock** from the expert's fast loop. The expert runs at its own ~50–200 Hz tempo against the latest available intent latent without blocking on the slow trunk; the trunk refreshes the latent whenever it next completes a pass. This is Figure's proven Helix handoff — decoupled-clock conditioning so a fast onboard expert and a slow trunk run at independent rates — and it pairs naturally with RTC's latency tolerance. The division of labor: **`z_t` is the rich workspace the felt parts and world model read; the intent latent is the cheap, async fast-path conditioning the expert tokens consume** (`standard` direction, adopted from Figure Helix).

**Keep the cortex plastic — Knowledge Insulation, not a freeze (`standard`, adopted from PI).** The cortex is warm-started and then **co-trained end-to-end** — it is *not* frozen. Use **Knowledge Insulation (KI)** (the π0.5-style approach — insulate the web-scale priors from being washed out by action-only gradients, so you get adaptation without catastrophically forgetting the pretraining) so the warm-start survives while the cortex keeps learning. The one thing this costs: the felt parts ground against a **moving** `z_t`, so grounding is handled by **managed re-grounding** — periodic re-anchoring of the amygdala/BG/pain heads as the cortex shifts (§3) — *not* by freezing the cortex. A hard freeze would make grounding trivially stable, but it would also stop feeling from reshaping the cortex, which is the whole bet; so we pay the re-grounding cost to keep the cortex plastic. **The reframe's central new risk, named:** refusing to freeze trades one hard problem (a frozen cortex that can't learn to feel) for a subtler one — a **three-timescale control problem**, the cortex's Knowledge-Insulation strength × the EMA-anchor rate × the re-grounding cadence, whose *joint* stability over a lifetime is the open problem this design now owns. It is a real research bet; it is named here rather than buried.

### Low-level whole-body controller
A separately-trained controller for balance, locomotion, and contact/force regulation, running at ~0.5–1 kHz. Trained by massive-parallel RL in simulation with domain randomization, then transferred sim-to-real. It turns the action expert's targets into stable, compliant whole-body motion.

**A learned, perception-conditioned WBC — not a blind tracker (adopted from Figure's Helix System 0 — a learned WBC that replaced hand-written control).** Frame this as a single **learned net** in Figure's "System 0" mold: massive-parallel sim-to-real RL (100k+ parallel envs is the starting point to tune) that **replaces hand-written control entirely** — Figure's System 0 replaced ~109k lines of C++ — rather than a hand-tuned controller the action expert merely steers. Critically, it is **perception-conditioned**, not a blind joint-target tracker: it ingests **stereo → 3D terrain perception** (the §1 head-mounted stereo, lifted to a 3D terrain/contact representation) so the controller *sees* stairs, slopes, gaps, and contact geometry and pre-shapes footfall and balance against them. Figure's System 0 is now terrain-conditioned in exactly this way (`standard` direction, adopted from Figure Helix). The action expert still hands down manipulation targets plus a locomotion intent (§ System 1, `base_cmd`); the difference is the WBC produces the whole-body motion realizing them with its own learned, terrain-aware sense of the ground rather than executing them blind.

**System-0 starting point — GEAR-SONIC, with our terrain-conditioning on top (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA).** Don't train the System-0 controller from zero either: start from an **open behavior-foundation whole-body controller** and add the terrain-conditioning above. The named starting point is **GEAR-SONIC** (NVIDIA GEAR's open whole-body-control / behavior-foundation model) — a pretrained, general WBC for humanoid locomotion and balance — used as the System-0 base, with our **stereo→3D terrain-perception conditioning** layered on top so the warm-started controller gains the terrain-aware footfall/balance shaping it doesn't have out of the box. Borrow the behavior foundation; add the terrain sense.

This backbone, on its own, is a competent instruction-following humanoid. What it lacks is *what to do when no one is instructing it* — which is the felt system's job.

## 3 · The felt system — the bet (embodied)

The felt parts sit **above** the backbone. They read the trunk's latent world state (and interoception) and produce the agent's own objective. They are separate modules on purpose — collapsing them into one scalar reward is the standard-RL move this whole project argues against. **The *guidance* is a trio — amygdala (the perception→pull net) + basal ganglia (value) + pain (the floor).** The hypothalamus is demoted to maintenance.

### Hypothalamus — drives `maintenance, demoted`
Reads interoception. Innate homeostatic setpoints — energy, thermal, structural load — that **pull the agent toward** resources (charge, rest). Tonic, slow, satiable. **Not** part of the guidance trio: it's upkeep (recharge when low, don't overheat) plus the infant bootstrap. An imitation-pretrained agent with a seeded amygdala-appraisal and pain already has its attractor and repulsor — keep this minimal.

### Pain — nociception
Reads interoception for **tissue/hardware damage** — impact, over-torque, joint-limit strain, thermal overload. Two outputs: a **fast protective reflex** at ~1 kHz that overrides the action expert, and a **strong, non-habituating** aversive learning signal. Pain is the one felt part that is cleanly buildable, because its target — damage — is specifiable. Distinct from the hypothalamus: damage/withdraw, not deficit/approach; and it must **never habituate**.

**The reflex is a hard override, and it is balance-aware (not a raw withdraw).** Above an **absolute damage limit** — a fraction of rated joint torque / motor thermal / joint-limit proximity / ISO-TS-15066-class human-contact force+pressure limits, **fixed at design time and never adapted to the operating distribution** — the reflex hands **full authority** to a dedicated reaction controller: it is a **discrete mode-switch / relay**, `a_plan` contributes **exactly zero** (not a sigmoid blend that never quite reaches 1). The graded "70th-percentile-of-normal" band is used **only** for the soft, graded aversive *learning* signal, **never** for the reflex trigger. The reaction is **produced through / arbitrated with the WBC** so it is whole-body compliant and keeps the ZMP in the support polygon — a raw limb withdraw on a biped can topple it, so `a_safe` is realized as a **constrained task inside the WBC** (or an explicit protective-step / safe-fall mode when withdrawing would destabilize). **Authority order:** the reflex outranks `a_plan` absolutely, but the balance/WBC arbitration shapes *how* the reflex is realized — the reflex sets the protective intent, the WBC keeps the body upright while executing it. `a_plan` refreshes at ~20–30 Hz while the reflex runs at ~1 kHz; on a stale `a_plan` chunk the reflex still fires on the ~1 kHz clock and the last chunk is held.

**Measuring pain without skin everywhere.** A humanoid can't have tactile sensors on every surface — so you don't measure contact, you **estimate force and predict collision** from sensors it already has. Five layers, combined by a max:

1. **Sensorless external-force estimation (the workhorse).** A momentum/disturbance observer estimates external torque from the gap between commanded torque and the dynamics model: `τ_ext = τ_measured − M(q)q̈ − C(q,q̇)q̇ − g(q)`. Every joint already has torque/current sensing, so an unexpected `τ_ext` spike *localizes* a collision **anywhere on the body** — no skin needed. This is standard collision detection in robot arms; it gives whole-body coverage. **The observer model drifts** (wear, payload, thermal, calibration), so feed the **visual self-calibration / online dynamic-parameter ID** (§1 visual proprioception) into `M`, `C`, `g` to keep them current, and **monitor the observer residual on known contact-free motion as a health check**. Set the collision threshold **above the worst-case model-error torque envelope** so drift alone never trips it; the cost is the **false-negative behavior under drift** — a genuine light contact whose `τ_ext` sits under the widened threshold is missed by this layer, and coverage falls back to the tactile/IMU/thermal layers (and, if residuals on contact-free motion exceed their band, the health check flags the model for re-ID before the threshold is trusted).
2. **Computed self-collision.** The robot knows its own kinematics, so it computes predicted self-contact geometrically (capsule/mesh proximity) — it knows its hand is about to hit its face *without a sensor there*. Imminent self-contact = pain. This is how it stops smacking itself.
3. **IMU impact spikes.** Sudden acceleration/jerk = impact; cheap and global.
4. **Targeted real tactile, only where it pays.** Sparse but strategic: fingertips/palms (manipulation), soles (contact), and high-value surfaces (head/face). You don't need skin everywhere — you need it where damage is likely or costly. (Note: fingertip tactile is no longer optional here — §1 makes high-sensitivity fingertip sensing a *hard requirement* fused end-to-end, adopted from Figure's Helix; the "only where it pays" trade-off now governs *non-fingertip* surfaces — soles, head/face — while fingertips are a floor.)
5. **Thermal/current.** Motor over-current and temperature = strain/overload.

**It's estimation, not noise.** You do *not* inject noise to "feel around" — the signal is a precise model-prediction *discrepancy*, not a stochastic probe. Anticipatory protection is **scoped to slow/planned motion** — a **1–10 Hz planning-horizon bias**: the world model + self-model let the agent *imagine* a trajectory, predict high contact force, and feel anticipatory pain through imagine-and-feel, so on deliberate motion it recoils from the *imagined* collision before committing to it. It does **not** cover fast or unplanned contact — for that, the **~1 kHz reactive reflex plus the hard actuation caps are the real-time floor** (there is an unavoidable latency band between an unforeseen contact and the reflex firing; anticipation cannot close it). Reactive estimation is the floor; predicted pain biases *planned* trajectories away from hard contact in the first place.

**Anticipatory pain needs a *learned* pain head — the analytic estimator can't run on latents.** The five-layer estimator above runs on *real* sensors (measured torque, kinematics, IMU, taxels). An imagined future is a predicted **latent `z`**, not a sensor stream — there is no `τ_measured` to difference against, so the momentum/disturbance observer simply cannot be evaluated on it. So train a **learned pain head `pain(z) → scalar`**: a small regression head that maps the trunk latent (real or imagined) to expected nociceptive magnitude, **supervised by the analytic estimator on real experience** (the analytic max is the label; the head learns to predict it from `z`). At wake time the analytic estimator is still the ground truth (and the reflex source); the learned head exists so anticipatory pain can be felt on imagined `z` during imagine-and-feel, where the analytic estimator is undefined. It carries the same `real|imagined` provenance as any other read of `z` (§4). Distillation target, label source, and head size.

### Amygdala — a net that reads `z_t` and outputs a pull
**A small net over the trunk latent.** It reads `z_t` and emits one signed pull — approach toward the good and toward people, avoid from danger — flagging above all the **distress / needs of others** (the social core), plus **danger** and **salience**. It is **social first**: it reads and mirrors other beings as much as it detects threat, which is what makes social learning (§ Social referencing / imitation) its main job, not an add-on. The seed we actually build is exactly that — a **face-gated emotional mirror** (happy human → approach, distressed → aversive, only with a person present). One grounded appraisal, read out as two faces (below); the plain full definition is in [the overview](../README.md).

**Where it taps `z_t` — evidence-based amendment (Phase 0).** "Reads `z_t`" is refined by a measured result: a world-model's *recurrent* state discards current-frame affect (the prediction filter keeps what dynamics need, and static affective appearance is not that) — approach/avoid reads at **0.79–0.81 from the encoder embedding (`z_enc`, pre-RSSM)** but collapses to **~0.57 from the filtered world-state** ([experiments/nursery/phase0](../experiments/nursery/phase0/)). So **`R_φ` taps early perception (`z_enc`), not the deliberated world-state** — which is also the biological wiring (the amygdala reads early sensory cortex, not a deliberated model). *Caveat: shown on static billboards at 6M-param scale; the Nursery's affect cues are dynamic and reward-relevant, so a larger, dynamics-relevant state may retain more — re-test before treating this as load-bearing.* This couples with the reconstruction-free world model (§ Imagination): if `R_φ` reads `z_enc`, imagine-and-feel needs the model to predict future `z_enc`, which a JEPA-style latent predictor does natively.

**Why these principles (the motivation).** Evolution faces the *same* reward-specification problem this thesis describes: it cannot hardwire a value for every situation an animal might meet. So it hardwires only the **invariants** — the few flags true in *every* environment a human could inhabit: tissue damage is bad (the physics of bodies) → pain/cortisol; a conspecific's distress matters (social-species survival) → care/cortisol; sudden / looming / novel things → salience/attention; resources and nurturing cues → approach/dopamine. Hardwire the world-invariant flags; learn the rest. The amygdala is the minimal safe prior evolution could install — and it is exactly the move the agent needs: don't write the open-world reward, install the invariant flags and let a lifetime build the rest.

**One appraisal, two functional faces (not two channels).** The amygdala **drives / gates** — functionally, this is an engineering model, not literal neuroanatomy (the amygdala is not literally the source of these hormones) — two affect signals that are the two dynamical faces of the *one* appraisal: **dopamine = the approach signal** (rises toward the good and the salient — a fixed read whose **phasic** pull decays as the reward becomes predicted, § Habituation), and **cortisol = the threat signal** (rises to danger and to others' distress, and **lingers** — tonic / slow). These are the positive and negative faces of a single grounded signal, **not** two independently-grounded channels (this preserves the prior "one grounded signal; cortisol is its slow-negative" structure — it just names the two faces).

The embodiment-specific consequence: the amygdala stays **small, fixed, hardwired** — it cannot be pretrained or grounded in language, and everything about *what to do* is learned on top (the developed value system, the BG, is the wall). At most, human affect data seeds the perceptual *shadow* — and a model **auto-labels that data using the invariants** (CLIP/SigLIP text-anchoring, a VLM, or affect datasets — Stage 4 in [TRAINING.md](TRAINING.md)); the [grounding probe](../experiments/amygdala_grounding/) shows a linear read of frozen features already recovers human approach/avoid (AUC 0.95), so no special human labeler is required (a higher-fidelity human capture is an optional refinement, not load-bearing). Even that only seeds the shadow. The appraisal grounds a fixed prior; the developed value system on top of it is the wall: earned through lived experience, not built from data.

### Social referencing / imitation — the drive to copy is social dopamine
Because **others' distress / needs** is an innate flag, **observing a human — above all a caregiver — fires the amygdala's approach signal (dopamine).** That is the mechanism of imitation here: attending to and copying a human is *intrinsically rewarding* because the felt system marks humans as salient and good, and the caregiver's own affect (approval, distress, where they attend) is read as value — social referencing. Imitation is therefore not a separate objective bolted on; it is **the amygdala's "others matter" flag expressed as reward.** Division of labor with the backbone: the **Stage 1–2 behavior-cloning supplies the motor *capability*** (retargeted human/teleop demos); the **seeded amygdala supplies the *drive* to imitate and transmits *value*, not just skill** — the agent catches *what the caregiver cares about*, the cheap on-ramp to the value system (§ Basal ganglia). It stays self-generated (the agent's own dopamine on seeing a human, not an external reward), carries the `real|imagined` provenance like any read of `z` (§4), and is the front half of the **growing-up** handoff (caregiver-scaffolded → self-directed; § Growing up in [the overview](../README.md)).

### Threat / cortisol and NE / uncertainty — the felt signal is several systems
Feeling is not one scalar — it is several systems running at once (the thesis is explicit that the felt signal is a *trio* plus modulators, not a single reward). **Cortisol is NOT a separately-grounded channel — it is derived from the single appraisal:** the amygdala emits exactly one grounded signal, a signed `valence` (the one appraisal, §Amygdala), and cortisol is a deterministic transform of it (the slow low-pass below), never labeled or grounded on its own. This is the negative face of the appraisal made durable; dopamine (the approach signal) is its phasic positive face. Beyond the amygdala's fast approach signal, two slower neuromodulatory channels enter the felt signal and its scalarization:

- **Threat / cortisol — a graded, slow stress channel.** A **graded** threat estimate (not the binary pain reflex) integrated into a slow stress level. Model it as a **slow low-pass of `relu(−valence)`** — i.e. cortisol accumulates from the *negative* part of the amygdala's valence over time and decays slowly, so sustained aversive context raises a tonic stress that a single bad moment does not. This is the standard "fast affect vs. slow stress hormone" split: the amygdala spikes, cortisol is the smoothed, lingering shadow of repeated negative valence. High cortisol biases the scalarization toward caution/avoidance and (per the root-thesis spec) toward **exploration when stalled** — a stall-breaking drive, not just a damper. The low-pass time constant and the cortisol weight in the scalarization.
- **NE / uncertainty — a precision/arousal channel.** A norepinephrine-style channel carrying **uncertainty / surprise** (model-prediction error, value-ensemble disagreement, novelty). It does not add valence; it **modulates gain** — raising it sharpens attention and learning rate under surprise (and feeds the orienting reflex in PERCEPTION-ACTION §1–2, where novelty pulls gaze). It also feeds epistemic action (imagine-and-feel over *where to look* — PERCEPTION-ACTION §3). NE is the precision knob on the felt signal, not a term added to it.

These are consistent with "feeling is several systems": the amygdala sets direction, cortisol sets a slow protective tone, NE sets the gain. All three feed the scalarization in §4; none collapses the others into a single reward.

### Habituation
Curiosity is not a separate module, and it is **not** the amygdala's appraisal changing — the read stays a fixed grounded reward (a good thing always reads good). Habituation lives in the **reward-prediction loop**: the phasic drive is the **RPE over the amygdala's reward**, and as the value learner comes to *predict* a recurring good, that RPE decays to zero and the pull fades. Novel grounded-good is unpredicted → positive RPE → strong pull; a satiated source is predicted → no pull. This is the classic RPE dynamic (a predicted reward produces no prediction error — neuroscience's phasic dopamine) — "seek good" becomes "seek *new* good" with no extra machinery.

**Why this over a decay module or a context-conditioned appraisal.** It keeps the amygdala a clean fixed read-out (nothing to make context-dependent, nothing new to ground) and reuses the RPE you already compute for value — habituation is *emergent*, not bolted on. It stays distinct from content-free curiosity: the error that drives it is **reward** prediction error over grounded affect (how good it turned out), not **world-model** prediction error (what will happen — the RND/ICM signal).

**Two hard constraints (or it breaks).** (1) **Appetitive-only** — pain/aversion is never predicted away; a *predicted* injury still has to hurt, so the aversive channel is a fixed floor, not RPE-gated (pleasure's pull fades, pain's does not). (2) **The drive is the phasic RPE, not the raw value** — a policy maximizing the raw appraisal would sit on a predicted good (wirehead); driving on the RPE is what makes not-sitting-on-it emergent. Homeostatic necessities (drives/hunger, § Hypothalamus) are handled separately and are *not* habituated away — you still go to the known food when hungry; RPE-decay governs the discretionary, open-ended appetitive pull. It biases toward variety, it doesn't guarantee it (an agent could still time-share sources); promising, untested at scale.

### Imagination — one multimodal faculty
A single learned latent dynamics model over the trunk's representation — **not one imaginer per sense.** When it imagines a future it renders the *whole multimodal latent at once* (vision, audio, touch, proprioception together), because the world is all of them together; auditory and visual imagery are the same faculty in different channels (inner speech is the one near-exception — it also borrows the speech-motor/voice head, covert articulation). The imagined latent is written to the same workspace the real senses write to (§4), tagged `imagined` and carrying the same per-token modality and `fovea|periphery` tags as a real frame — including the foveated structure, so imagination wastes no capacity rendering periphery it didn't attend to (active/foveated perception is specced in **[PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)**, cross-referenced here, not re-specced). Enables **imagine-and-feel**: roll futures forward, let the amygdala feel them, act toward the future that pulls hardest. The bottleneck is faithful, far-reaching, multi-leg **search** — not the feeling. Both are load-bearing: the world model predicts the futures and the amygdala must appraise them; neither works without the other.

**Frontier defaults (`standard` baselines, all numbers are starting points to tune):**
- **World model:** the *faithfulness* frontier has moved **reconstruction-free** — self-predictive / **JEPA-style** dynamics that predict in latent space rather than reconstructing pixels/audio, which avoids spending capacity on perceptually-irrelevant detail (exactly the foveation argument, applied to the world model). So **make JEPA-style latent prediction the default**, not just the upgrade path: the current line is **V-JEPA 2-AC** (an action-conditioned video-JEPA world model trained for planning/control) and **Dreamer-4** (the latest Dreamer line, scaled and faster). **DreamerV3** is the known-good floor if a JEPA-style model won't train at this scale — but its RSSM is adopted here **reconstruction-free over the trunk latent `z_t`** (a latent-consistency / predict-next-`z` target, keeping DreamerV3's stability tricks with *no* pixel/audio reconstruction), so even the fallback honors the latent-only contract. Which objective wins at this scale is still a `bet`; the default is now JEPA, with the DreamerV3-RSSM-over-`z_t` fallback as the known-good floor.
- **Planning / learned search:** for the search stage, name the continuous/efficient MuZero line rather than generic MCTS — **Sampled MuZero** (handles continuous, high-DoF action spaces by planning over sampled actions, which a humanoid requires) and **EfficientZero V2** (sample-efficient learned-model search, strong in the data-limited regime that matters for a single embodied body). TD-MPC2 remains a viable model-predictive-control alternative for the continuous-control inner loop. The search runs over the latent world-model rollout and is appraised by the amygdala.

**World-model faithfulness acceptance gate — before Stage-5 reliance.** Imagine-and-feel is only as safe as the world model is faithful: a model that rolls out plausible-but-wrong futures will let the agent feel — and then act toward — things that won't happen. So **the agent must not *rely* on imagined rollouts to drive behavior until the world model passes an explicit faithfulness gate.** Concretely, gate the transition to the imagination-reliant stage (the felt/autonomous "Stage 5") on held-out metrics over real trajectories, all **starting points to tune**: multi-step **latent open-loop prediction error** stays bounded out to the planning horizon `H` (not just one step); **calibration** — the model's predicted uncertainty tracks its actual error (so the agent can down-weight rollouts it shouldn't trust); **`pain(z)` agreement** — anticipatory pain on imagined `z` matches the analytic estimator's value when those futures are actually executed (the safety-critical channel must be faithful before it's trusted); and **no reward/feasibility hallucination** — imagined high-approach or low-pain futures are not systematically more optimistic than realized ones. Below threshold, imagine-and-feel runs in **shadow mode** (logged, scored, not in the action loop); it earns the right to steer behavior only after it clears the gate, and re-clears it after any major re-grounding. This gate is itself part of the safety story (§Safety).

**One workspace, two writers.** Both the real senses and the world model write to the **same perceptual latent** (the trunk's working-memory state), and the amygdala reads that workspace **blind to real-vs-imagined** — which is why the agent can feel a future it only imagined. The required safeguard is a **reality-monitoring tag** on every state (real vs imagined) so feeling an imagined threat never triggers acting on it as if present.

**The tag is a hard provenance bit, not a learned guess.** Do *not* implement reality-monitoring as a classifier that infers "does this look real?" — make it a **hard, immutable provenance bit set by the writer at the plumbing level**. The real sensory front-end sets `real` when it writes a token; the world model sets `imagined` when it writes; the bit travels with the tensor and **no downstream module may flip it**. The action path then enforces a **`real`-only mask at the plumbing level** — the action expert physically cannot consume `imagined` tokens, because the masking happens in the tensor pipeline below any learned component, not as a policy the network could learn around. This makes "act only on reality" a structural guarantee rather than a behavior we hope the agent acquires. A reality-monitoring **classifier is still useful, but demoted**: it runs as a watchdog whose *only* job is to catch **pipeline bugs** — a mismatch between the provenance bit and what the content looks like flags a plumbing fault (a writer mis-tagging, a leak between buffers), not an agent decision. So hallucination here is reclassified from "the agent believed an imagined thing" to "the pipeline mis-set or leaked a bit," which is a testable, fixable engineering fault rather than an emergent behavior.

### Basal ganglia — the value system
What is worth pursuing over long horizons. The anticipatory appraisal made durable — and the second wall: it **cannot be pretrained** (it's about this body's lived consequences, the unlived value system — keep this flagged as the wall, not given fake certainty). It develops through embodied experience and serves as a **cache** of the imagine-and-feel computation (System 2 → System 1). **RPE — value prediction-error** — trains it (note: this is the BG's *value*-learning signal, distinct from dopamine, which now names only the amygdala's approach signal, §Amygdala; the two are no longer the same word).

**Ground on a slow anchor of the cortex; re-ground as it shifts; version every re-grounding.** The felt parts (amygdala, BG, pain head, habituation key) all learn *on top of* the cortex latent `z`. Because the cortex is co-trained and **not frozen**, `z` drifts underneath them, so grounding must *track* the moving representation rather than assume a fixed one. Two mechanisms, in order:
- **Slow EMA anchor (the primary fix — biological *and* proven).** Don't ground the felt parts on the live, fast-moving `z`; ground them on a **slow exponential-moving-average copy of the affect-relevant `z`** — a **target-network-style** stable anchor. This is standard practice for exactly this problem: value-based RL uses a slow target network so it isn't bootstrapping against a moving target; self-supervised learning (BYOL/MoCo/DINO) uses a slow EMA *teacher* encoder for stable targets; and biologically it is the **complementary-learning-systems** fast-cortex / slow-consolidated split (and multi-timescale synapses, Benna–Fusi). The anchor **still drifts, slowly**, so feeling can *still* reshape what it grounds on over a lifetime — but it moves slowly enough that grounding barely de-grounds between updates. This keeps the cortex **fully plastic** while handing the parts a nearly-stationary target — the middle path between a hard freeze (which kills the thesis) and grounding on raw drift (which de-grounds constantly). The EMA rate is the knob: slow enough for stable grounding, fast enough that affect still tracks the cortex.
- **Managed re-grounding (the backstop).** When the cortex shifts *materially* (a capability upgrade, a new modality — not just slow drift), **re-anchor / re-validate** the parts against the current `z`, re-clearing the world-model faithfulness gate, and tag every consolidated felt-memory and value with a cortex **version id** so a value learned against an old representation isn't blindly trusted against a new one (stale-tagged items are replay/re-grounding fodder, not directly trusted).

The slow anchor makes re-grounding **rare and cheap** rather than constant. A hard freeze would make grounding trivial but stop feeling from reshaping the cortex (the whole bet); tuning the anchor rate and keeping this stable at lifetime scale is itself part of the bet, not solved.

**Value-learner recipe (`standard`, starting point to tune).** Keep the **ensemble twin-Q** idea (robust, low-overestimation value estimates), but the current default is **not** REDQ — REDQ is outdated for the high-UTD (update-to-data) regime this design runs in (continuous high-UTD replay, §memory). Use a **high-UTD recipe** instead:
- **SimbaV2** (the high-UTD default) — a hyperspherical / normalized actor-critic that stays stable at high UTD via architectural normalization (the network can absorb many gradient steps per environment step without diverging).
- **CrossQ** (ICLR 2024) — a target-net-free, low-compute REDQ-alternative: it adds **BatchNorm** to the critics and **removes target networks** entirely, matching REDQ's sample efficiency **at UTD≈1** with much less compute. It is *not* itself a high-UTD stabilizer — vanilla CrossQ is documented to become unstable at high UTD and needs **weight normalization** (arXiv:2506.03758) to scale up. Reach for it when the win you want is REDQ-level efficiency at low compute, not high-UTD stability.

Why the swap: at high UTD, target-network and ensemble-only stabilization (REDQ-style) leave performance on the table and are prone to the **plasticity loss** that high-UTD training induces (see memory/consolidation below); **SimbaV2** is designed for exactly the many-updates-per-step regime the BG runs in (CrossQ only reaches high UTD with added weight normalization). Name **SimbaV2** as the high-UTD default; keep the twin-Q ensemble on top. Ensemble size, UTD ratio, and normalization details.

### Memory / hippocampus (the replay buffer, not a separate parametric model) and consolidation
An episodic store with **continuous high-UTD replay** — *experience replay is perpetual sleep*. There is no distinct sleep phase; consolidation runs while the agent acts. (A real offline pass is warranted only if the deployed body genuinely cannot learn while acting.) **What this budget line actually buys (pin this — it has read as a mystery model):** it is the **replay buffer / episodic store**, not a separate *parametric* network. The "hippocampus" function here is the stored stream of lived segments (the §Stage-5 replay-buffer segment format) plus the consolidation machinery that replays it into the value/world-model/felt heads — there is no extra trained model to build for it. (If a future variant wants a *learned* episodic module — a parametric read/write interface over `z_t` with its own build step — that is a flagged option, `bet`, not the default; the default is "the budget is the buffer.")

**The "forever policy" gap — continual-learning hazards (the weakest area, flagged honestly).** A body that learns for a lifetime under high-UTD updates faces two failure modes the rest of this design does *not* automatically solve. Name them and budget mitigations in from the start; all knobs below are starting points to tune, and whether these mitigations are *sufficient* for true lifelong embodied learning is itself open — `bet`, not `proven`.

1. **Plasticity loss (the network stops being able to learn).** High-UTD training degrades a network's ability to fit new targets — dead/saturated units, collapsing effective rank, dormant neurons. This is *the* hazard the SimbaV2 value swap partially addresses (normalization), but it needs explicit handling too. Mitigations: **periodic / soft resets** of the most-saturated parameters — **ReDo** (recycle dormant neurons), and broader **plasticity injection** / soft-reset schedules that re-randomize a fraction of weights while preserving learned ones. Reset cadence and the fraction reset.
2. **Catastrophic forgetting (new skills overwrite consolidated ones).** Continual updates on the recent stream erode skills the body already earned. The retention mechanism is **replay**, layered on the episodic store: **replay-CLEAR-style** behavioral-cloning + value-distillation off a long-horizon replay buffer (keep rehearsing consolidated skills, not just recent experience). Replay is the workhorse. **Drop EWC** — Fisher/quadratic-anchor regularization is a known-failed mitigation in this regime (it tends to collapse to plain fine-tuning and entrenches without actually protecting skills); do not budget for it. Lean on replay coverage instead, widened by the consolidation store.

**The arbitration — reset vs. retain, and on which sub-nets.** Resets and retention pull against each other (resets fight plasticity loss but can *cause* forgetting; retention fights forgetting but can *entrench* and reduce plasticity), so the continual-learning subsystem must **arbitrate** between them rather than run both blindly. Make the trigger explicit and **per-sub-net** (the value heads, the world model, the felt heads, and the adapters each get their own decision — never reset the trunk). Monitor three metrics per sub-net (all thresholds are starting points to tune):
- **dormancy fraction** — share of dead/saturated units (the ReDo signal);
- **effective rank** — collapse of the representation's effective dimensionality;
- **forgetting on a held-out probe** — a frozen, periodically-evaluated probe set of already-consolidated skills.

Arbitration rule: high dormancy / collapsed effective rank with *stable* probe performance ⇒ **plasticity loss is the binding problem ⇒ reset** (ReDo recycle the dormant units / soft-reset that sub-net's most-saturated parameters, re-randomizing a fraction while preserving learned ones). Rising probe-forgetting with *healthy* dormancy/rank ⇒ **forgetting is the binding problem ⇒ retain** (lean harder on replay-CLEAR rehearsal, throttle the update-to-data ratio). The two metric families point at *different* sub-nets at different times, which is why the decision is per-sub-net and metric-gated rather than a global schedule. Reset cadence, the fraction reset, probe contents, and all thresholds are starting points to tune; whether this arbitration is *sufficient* for true lifelong embodied learning remains the open problem of the forever policy — `bet`, not `proven`.

## 4 · How the felt system attaches as a separate module reading `z_t` (the interface)

```
 trunk latent state ──► amygdala (appraisal → valence; dopamine=approach) ─┬─────┐
 │ │ relu(−valence), slow low-pass
 trunk latent ────────► threat/cortisol (slow) ◄──┘ ▼
 interoception ───────► drives ─────────────────────────┐ cortisol (tonic stress) ─┐
 interoception ───────► pain ────────────────────────────┤ │
 trunk latent / WM PE ► NE / uncertainty (gain) ──────────┤ │
 ▼ ▼
 felt signal = dopamine(approach) + drives − pain − w_c·cortisol, gain-scaled by NE  [habituation = RPE-decay, downstream]
 │
 ├─► reward for online learning ──► basal ganglia (value)
 world model ◄── trunk latent ──► imagine-and-feel ───────┘ │
 RPE (value pred-error) ◄─────┘
 pain reflex ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────► overrides action expert (fast loop)
```

The scalarization is **`felt = dopamine(approach) + drives − pain − w_c·cortisol`**, **gain-scaled by NE** (precision/learning-rate under surprise, §3). **Habituation is not a term in `felt` at all** — `felt` is the immediate grounded reward; habituation emerges one level down, in the **RPE** the value learner computes over it (§ Habituation): a predicted reward's RPE decays, so its phasic pull fades. Appetitive-only — pain is not predicted away. It is deliberately *not* one part's scalar — the trio (the amygdala's approach signal, value, pain) plus the slow channels (cortisol, NE) are several systems summed and modulated, which is the whole point. `w_c` and the NE gain.

Four rules: **it reads, it doesn't replace** (the parts take the trunk's latent state as input, not raw pixels); **it generates the objective** (the felt signal *is* the reward — no external reward function); **it plans by feeling futures** (imagine-and-feel over the world model, cached by the BG); **it never wireheads** (habituation fades pleasure only).

### The `z_t` workspace tensor contract (authoritative)

`z_t` is the trunk's working-memory state — the **shared workspace** every felt part reads and both writers (real senses, world model) write. This is the load-bearing interface; pin it once and build to it. All counts and dims below are **starting points to tune**, not measured optima.

**Shape.** `z_t` is a **sequence of tokens**, `bfloat16`, shape **`[B, T_tok, D]`** — batch × token-count × trunk hidden width. It is *not* a flat vector; the parts attend over tokens.

- **`D` = trunk hidden width** — the System-2 trunk's model dimension. Every modality is projected into this one width (the §2 sensor adapters do this for proprio/intero). Starting point: `D ≈ 4096` (tie to the chosen trunk — fix when the backbone is chosen).
- **`T_tok` = sum of per-modality token counts**, `T_tok = N_vision + N_audio + N_proprio + N_intero + N_lang`, where `N_vision = N_fovea + N_periphery` (per camera). Example starting counts (per timestep, single camera; tune):

 | modality | tokens (`N_*`) | source |
 |---|---|---|
 | vision — fovea | ~256 | high-res foveal crop → DINOv3 ViT-L-class (PERCEPTION-ACTION §1) |
 | vision — periphery | ~96 | wide low-res stream → DINOv3 ViT-S-class (PERCEPTION-ACTION §1) |
 | audio | ~32 | attended stream + ambient mixture (PERCEPTION-ACTION §2) |
 | proprioception | ~16 | joint encoders, IMUs, F/T → §2 adapter |
 | interoception | ~8 | energy, thermal, load, strain → §2 adapter |
 | language | ~64 | instruction / inner-speech tokens |
 | **`T_tok`** | **~472** | (per camera; multiply vision by #cameras) |

 These are seeds. Multi-camera multiplies the vision rows; a history window over the trunk's time window multiplies `T_tok` (or is summarized into memory tokens) — both are tuning decisions.

**Per-token tags (every token carries them).** Alongside its `D`-vector, each token has:
- **modality tag** — `{vision, audio, proprio, intero, lang}` (a learned modality embedding, the §2 adapter's output).
- **`real | imagined` tag** — the **reality-monitoring** bit, implemented as a **hard, immutable provenance bit set by the writer**, not an inferred label. Real senses write `real`; the world model writes `imagined`; no downstream module may flip it. The amygdala reads blind to this tag (so it can feel an imagined future), but it is a **hard safety interlock**: only `real`-tagged states may drive the action expert, enforced as a **`real`-only mask at the plumbing level** below any learned component. A reality-monitoring classifier is demoted to a watchdog for **pipeline bugs** (provenance-vs-content mismatch = a plumbing fault), not the safeguard itself. Tag mis-set or buffer leak = the failure mode (the engineering version of "hallucination").
- **`fovea | periphery` tag** — for vision tokens (and by analogy `attended | ambient` for audio), so downstream knows what was attended vs. ambient. Carried through imagination too, so imagined frames keep the foveated structure (§3 imagination).
- positional / gaze metadata — gaze `(x, y, scale)` and beamform direction enter as tokens (PERCEPTION-ACTION §1, §3).

**Who reads `z_t`:** the amygdala (feels it), the basal ganglia (values it), the threat/cortisol and NE/uncertainty channels (§3), the learned **`pain(z)` head** (anticipatory pain on real or imagined `z`, §3), the drives/pain pathways (via interoception tokens), and the world model (predicts the next `z_t`). **Who writes `z_t`:** the real sensory front-end (`real`) and the world model (`imagined`) — one workspace, two writers (§3). Provenance and the `real`-only action mask are as defined in the tag bullet above (§3).

**The action expert is conditioned by a *derived* compact intent latent, not by reading `z_t` directly (§2, adopted from Figure Helix).** `z_t` is the rich workspace for the felt parts and world model; the action expert instead consumes a **compact intent latent distilled from the trunk** and concatenated into its own token stream, refreshed **asynchronously on a decoupled clock** (§2 System 1). This keeps the heavy workspace on the slow felt/deliberative side while the fast expert runs against a cheap, async summary — the two paths are deliberately separate.

## 5 · Sizing & shape

**No fixed parameter budget.** This is one unified cortex, not an assembly with a per-part budget — its size follows the chosen pretrained initialization and the compute available, and pinning pseudo-precise counts on an unbuilt system is false precision. What matters is the **shape**: perception, prediction, and action share one latent `z_t` and are trained end-to-end, and **feeling brackets the control hierarchy** — pain is the fastest loop (reflex), the amygdala the slowest (direction-setting). Control-rate / latency budget is § 6.

## 6 · Actuation & embodiment

- **Actuators** — high-torque-density electric actuators with series elasticity or torque sensing for compliance and safe contact. Count the **body** and the **hands** separately, because hand DoF dominates the manipulation action-vector width and is best stated on its own. **Body: ~28–33 DoF** — 2×6 legs, 2×7 arms, 1–3 waist, 2 neck. **Hands: 2×(6–22 DoF by hand class)** — a simple parallel/underactuated gripper is a few DoF per hand, a dexterous multi-fingered hand is many; the v1 hand class should be named explicitly (v1 default: simple grippers for de-risking, upgrading to dexterous hands where manipulation demands it). **Total ≈ 40–72 DoF** depending on hand class. Real anchors: Optimus ~72, Figure ~51.
- **Hands** — multi-fingered with tactile sensing; the limiting factor for general manipulation. The hand DoF above is stated separately from the body because it is what sets the width of the manipulation action vector.
- **Voice** — an audio-output head for vocalization.
- **Onboard compute** — a model this size does not run fully onboard at these rates. Realistic split: the low-level WBC, pain reflex, and action expert run **onboard** (hard real-time, latency-critical); the System-2 trunk, world model, and amygdala appraisal run **on a tethered/edge accelerator** or are **distilled** (the BG cache is exactly this — compiling slow deliberation into a fast onboard reflex). Quantization and MoE sparsity make the trunk tractable.

**Export path (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA).** The onboard models reach the robot through an **ONNX → TensorRT** export/optimization path: train in PyTorch, export to ONNX, compile with TensorRT for the target accelerator (kernel fusion, the NVFP4/FP8 numeric formats below, layer-wise precision). This is the standard route onto Jetson Thor-class onboard silicon and edge Blackwell — name it as the deployment path for the onboard/distilled loops.

| System-2 trunk | ~1–10 Hz | **≤100 ms–1 s** | edge/tethered |
| Tier-0 embodied-reasoning orchestrator (Cosmos Reason, CoT over video) | **sub-Hz, fully async on a decoupled clock** | seconds | edge/tethered — never blocks chunk emission |

Note the two rates on the action side are **different things**: the **onboard neural re-plan rate is ~20–30 Hz** (the ~23 Hz Jetson-Thor anchor, §"Measured anchors"; ~50–80 Hz tethered/RTX), while the **~50–200 Hz figure is the action-chunk *sample* rate the WBC interpolates between re-plans**, not how often the network re-plans. Raw imagine-and-feel is **not** a 10–50 Hz loop: full MPPI over the world model costs on the order of **N·H·iters world-model passes** per plan (with reduced defaults, e.g. N≈64–128, H≈5), which runs sub-Hz to a few Hz off-board; its verdict is **amortized into the BG cache**, and the **BG cache carries the reactive loop** at single-digit world-model passes. The onboard loops have hard deadlines (missing one is a fall or a collision); the felt/deliberative loops are slow by design (the amygdala sets direction, not torques). The BG cache exists precisely to pull amygdala-quality decisions *down* into the action-expert budget.

**Measured anchors for the action-expert rate (replacing pure guesswork).** The action-expert *neural re-plan* rate is anchored to two reference deployments — and note this is the **re-plan** rate, distinct from the ~50–200 Hz action-chunk *sample* rate the WBC interpolates (see the table above):
- **Onboard, distilled (NVIDIA Jetson Thor / RTX-class).** A VLA action expert distilled and TensorRT-compiled (export path above) runs an end-to-end policy at roughly **~23 Hz on Jetson-Thor-class onboard silicon** and **~50–80 Hz on an RTX-class tethered accelerator** — measured anchors for what the fully-onboard expert re-plan loop actually achieves today, not an aspiration. So the onboard neural re-plan sits in the **~20–30 Hz** band, and the 50–200 Hz figure is the chunk sample rate the WBC reads between re-plans.
- **Cloud/edge backbone, async (Gemini Robotics-class).** A tethered reasoning backbone in the cloud answers with a **<160 ms backbone latency** and **~250 ms end-to-end** including the round trip, while the onboard control loop still runs at **~50 Hz** against the latest available intent — i.e. the slow backbone and the fast loop run on decoupled clocks (exactly the §2 async-intent-latent / RTC design, adopted from Gemini). This is the measured profile that justifies the tethered-trunk + onboard-expert split.

These are *anchors to tune against*, not contractual numbers — but they replace the earlier pure guesswork with what current-generation silicon and cloud backbones are measured to deliver.

**Quantization & sparsity defaults:**
- **Trunk:** weight-quantize to **4-bit** (e.g. NF4/GPTQ/AWQ-class) for the edge-resident trunk where it holds accuracy; fall back to **8-bit** for accuracy-sensitive layers and for the onboard-distilled paths. Default: **4-bit trunk weights, 8-bit activations**, profile and lift any layer that loses too much.
- **Deployment numeric format:** target **NVFP4** — the 4-bit floating-point format on current-generation accelerators (**Blackwell** datacenter / **Jetson Thor**-class onboard) that holds accuracy far better than integer 4-bit by keeping a floating exponent, with hardware support for the matmuls. Default to NVFP4 where the silicon supports it, **fall back to FP8** for accuracy-sensitive layers and on hardware without FP4 support.
- **MoE:** if the trunk is mixture-of-experts, the cost that matters is **active** parameters, not total. Starting point: **top-2 of N experts active per token** (≈ activate ~2/`N` of expert params) — tune the active-expert count against the per-loop latency budget above; fewer active experts buys latency at an accuracy cost.

## Safety

General framing: an agent with self-generated, persistent, grounded desires acting with a body is the maximal-autonomy configuration AI safety treats as the core risk — build it small, observable, bounded. The embodiment-specific bounds and interlocks this spec adds:

- **Hard-bounded actuation** — physical torque/velocity/force limits below the controller, non-overridable by any learned policy. Pain's reflex protects the body; a separate hard layer protects the world.
- **Certified power-and-force-limiting (PFL) — the world-protection floor, distinct from the per-joint clamps.** Add a **certified PFL layer** enforcing **ISO/TS-15066 transient + quasi-static force/pressure limits** (or a verified energy/momentum bound), **separate from** the per-joint torque/velocity clamps above — the joint clamps bound each actuator, PFL bounds the *human-interaction force* the whole body can deliver. Route the **collision-force estimate `τ_ext`** (§ Pain, layer 1) **into this hard limiter** — not only into the learned pain head — so contact force on a human is bounded by a verified layer **independent of any learned policy**. This is the "protects the world" layer made explicit and certifiable, below the pain reflex and the learned felt system.
- **Observability** — the felt signal, the amygdala's appraisal, the value estimates, and imagine-and-feel rollouts are all logged and inspectable; the orchestrator's embodied-thinking trace (§2) makes the deliberation legible in plain language. An agent whose *wants* are legible is far safer than one whose objective is hidden in weights.
- **`real`-only actuation + the world-model faithfulness gate** — the plumbing-level `real`-tagged mask (§3, §4) means only real states drive the action expert, and imagine-and-feel may not steer behavior until the world model clears its faithfulness gate (§3). (Embodiment-specific interlocks the general framing doesn't carry.)
- **Bounded autonomy, earned slowly** — the open-ended feeling-driven stage runs under supervision and in constrained settings first; autonomy widens only as behavior is understood.
- **The off-switch is upstream of feeling** — drives and reflexes can be externally clamped; the agent's desires never gate its own shutdown.

The autonomy that makes it interesting is the autonomy that makes it dangerous.
