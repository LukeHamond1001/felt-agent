# Training — building humanoid-vision, step by step

You cannot reinforcement-learn a humanoid from scratch on felt reward — it is sample- and safety-prohibitive (a falling 200B robot exploring randomly is a non-starter). The pipeline mirrors biology: **innate priors, then competence from imitation, then a life lived by feeling.** The backbone is *borrowed and assembled* by known methods; the felt system is attached and then *grown*.

Each stage gives the concrete **models, data, method, scale, output**, tagged **`proven`** (deployed-SOTA today, though not necessarily *built by this project*) or **`bet`** (the novel part). **All numbers are starting points to tune, not measured optima.**

---

## Stage 0 — VLM trunk (the semantic prior) `proven` (borrowed)

> **Step 0 — the trunk-selection spike (do this FIRST; everything dimensions against its result).** Before any dimensions are fixed, run a concrete bake-off spike to *choose the open trunk*, because the entire downstream pipeline (`D`, `T_tok`, adapter widths, the `z_t` contract, latency budgets) is dimensioned against the winner. The spike:
> 1. **Enumerate candidate open trunks** — the current open frontier multimodal models (Qwen-VL / Gemma / Eagle / Molmo / InternVL-class — *current SOTA generation; verify exact version and open-weights availability at build time, this moves fast*). **Include the robotics-native open candidates explicitly (adopted from NVIDIA):** **GR00T N1.5 (open weights)** — an already-embodied VLA foundation model (vision-language trunk + action head already wired for humanoid/manipulation), a strong candidate *trunk* because it has solved the "ingest embodied sensors + emit actions" problem the spike is testing; and **Cosmos Reason** — a physical-AI / embodied-reasoning VLM, a candidate **orchestrator / high-level reasoner** (the System-2 planner that reasons over `z_t`) even where it isn't the per-token trunk. Both are *current SOTA generation — verify exact version and open-weights availability at build time, this moves fast.* For each, record model dim `D`, native modalities, license, and whether weights are actually open. **GR00T N1.5 in particular may collapse Stage 0 *and* much of Stages 1–2** (it ships embodiment priors and an action head) — evaluate whether adopting it shortcuts the backbone, vs. assembling a trunk + separate expert.
> 2. **Verify each can ingest proprio/intero via adapters** — wire a throwaway per-modality adapter (proprio/intero MLP → trunk-dim tokens, §sensor-adapters below) onto the frozen candidate and confirm the embodied tokens fuse into the stream without trunk surgery.
> 3. **Verify freeze + a stable per-token `z_t` of the contracted shape** — freeze the trunk, run real frames through, and confirm it emits a per-token `z_t` of shape `[B, T_tok, D]` (the [ARCHITECTURE.md §4](ARCHITECTURE.md) workspace contract) that is *stable* across reruns (deterministic given fixed inputs) — the organs build to this tensor, so it must be pinnable.
> 4. **Hit the latency budget quantized** — quantize (4-bit weights / 8-bit activations baseline, §quantization in [ARCHITECTURE.md §6](ARCHITECTURE.md)) and confirm the candidate's trunk forward pass fits the **≤100 ms–1 s System-2 budget** on the target edge accelerator. A trunk that can't be quantized into budget is disqualified regardless of quality.
>
> **Output of the spike:** the chosen trunk and its *measured* `D`, `T_tok` envelope, adapter feasibility, and quantized latency. **Every number below — and the `z_t` contract — is provisional until this spike returns; then they are fixed against the winner.** Acceptance thresholds (which candidates pass) are **starting points to tune**.

**Model:** the multimodal trunk (~135B — an *extrapolation* from the frontier VLM scale range, to fix once the spike above picks the backbone) — a large multimodal transformer (Qwen-VL/Gemma/Eagle/Molmo/InternVL-class, **or a robotics-native open candidate: GR00T N1.5 (open weights) as the trunk, Cosmos Reason as the orchestrator/reasoner** — *current SOTA generation; verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast*) with vision (**DINOv3 / SigLIP2-class** encoder — current SOTA generation, verify exact version at build time), audio (**AF-Whisper-class** audio-language encoder — current SOTA generation, verify at build time), text feeding a shared token stream. Output: the **workspace latent `z_t`**.

> **CRITICAL DEPENDENCY — the real gate (this is what the spike resolves).** You must either **(a)** adopt an existing open frontier multimodal model that can ingest embodied sensors (proprioception/interoception) and is freezable-or-LoRA-tunable for robotics — *only "free" if someone already built exactly what you need*, which is precisely what Step 0's spike verifies — or **(b)** pretrain your own ~135B trunk (frontier-lab scale, which dominates total project cost). Do not underestimate this; it is ~68 % of the parameters.

**Freeze vs LoRA:** default **freeze** (already web-scale pretrained). Switch to LoRA only if (1) representations consistently miss task-critical detail, or (2) ablation shows LoRA improves value learning by >10 %. Start frozen; unfreeze only on evidence.

**Joint trunk↔action-expert training during the backbone phase (adopted from Figure's Helix end-to-end training).** Reconcile the freeze default above with a Figure-style end-to-end pass: **during Stages 0–2 (the backbone/imitation phase), train the trunk and the action expert *jointly*, letting gradients from the action policy flow back into the trunk** — exactly as Figure trains Helix end-to-end so the perception backbone learns features that are *useful for acting*, not just for semantics. This is the principled reading of "unfreeze on evidence": the backbone phase *is* the evidence regime, so co-train rather than keeping the trunk hard-frozen through imitation. To avoid burning the web-scale semantic prior while gradients flow, run this joint pass under the **Knowledge-Insulation discipline** (see Stage 2) — down-weight/insulate the action-loss gradients into the trunk and keep the trunk on its VLM-style auxiliary objective — so "joint training" means *action gradients shape the trunk under insulation*, not a naive full unfreeze. **The freeze becomes a phase boundary, not a global default:** the trunk is trained jointly through the backbone phase and is **frozen only when the felt phase begins** (Stage 4 onward), so the felt organs read a *fixed* `z_t` contract (the [ARCHITECTURE.md §4](ARCHITECTURE.md) pin) and the re-grounding step below fits to a trunk that no longer moves. How much action gradient to admit into the trunk (the insulation strength) is a **starting point to tune**.

**Knowledge-Insulation is the default backbone discipline — supersede plain freeze (build-to-a-tee; adopted from PI's π0.5 / Knowledge-Insulation recipe — *current SOTA generation; verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast*).** Make this explicit at the top of the backbone phase, not just in the Stage-2 mechanism note: **the default is Knowledge-Insulation joint training, not hard freeze.** "Default freeze" (above) is the *fallback* — what you fall back to if KI evidence shows the joint pass is eroding semantic priors (insulation strength → 0). The reconciliation: start the backbone phase under KI, and the freeze becomes a phase boundary (felt phase) rather than a stage-0 global default. **FAST discrete-action-token co-training (the KI auxiliary objective; PI's FAST action tokenizer — current SOTA generation, verify at build time).** The way KI keeps the trunk from forgetting while action gradients flow is to keep it on a **discrete-token VLM-style objective** — so co-train the trunk on **FAST-tokenized discrete action tokens** alongside the continuous flow-matching expert: compress the action chunk into discrete tokens (a DCT/BPE-style action tokenizer, FAST-class) and have the trunk predict them autoregressively as a next-token auxiliary loss, *in the trunk's native token space*, while the separate continuous expert (Stage 2) produces the precise deploy-time action via flow matching. The discrete-token head is the **knowledge-insulating anchor** (keeps the trunk doing language-model-shaped prediction so it doesn't catastrophically forget); the continuous expert is the **precise actuator**. Tokenizer vocabulary/compression and the discrete-vs-continuous loss weighting are **starting points to tune**.

**Sensor adapters (build-to-a-tee).** The web-pretrained trunk has never seen proprioception, interoception, tactile, or force/torque. Don't unfreeze the trunk to ingest them — **add a per-modality adapter**: each new sensor stream gets its own small encoder + a **linear projection to the trunk token dim** (e.g. tactile-array → MLP → trunk-dim tokens; proprio/interoception → MLP → trunk-dim tokens; force/torque → MLP → trunk-dim tokens), tagged with a modality channel and fused into the same token stream that produces `z_t`. **Trunk stays frozen; only the adapters train**, on the Stage 1–3 data (cross-embodiment + teleop + sim experience) — i.e. the embodied sensors get grounded into `z_t` using the same corpora the rest of the early pipeline already collects, no extra dataset. Adapter width and token count per modality are **starting points to tune** (~tens of tokens per modality, MLP hidden ~trunk-dim). When a *new* modality appears later (a new tactile skin, a new sensor), you add one more adapter and train only it — the trunk and existing adapters stay fixed.

**Re-grounding step (build-to-a-tee, do once the trunk is frozen at the start of the felt phase — before Stage 4).** Note the freeze point: per the joint-training note above, the trunk is co-trained through Stages 0–2 and **frozen when the felt phase begins**, so re-grounding fits to the trunk in *that* final, frozen state (the post-backbone-phase `z_t`), not to a trunk frozen at Stage 0. The amygdala-shadow prototype was developed against a **CLIP-B/32 prototype head** (the [vision-brain/amygdala-trainer](../amygdala-trainer/SPEC.md) work); **that head does not transfer to the chosen frozen trunk's `z_t`** — different encoder, different latent geometry, different token layout, so a valence head fit on CLIP space is reading the wrong coordinates. So once Stage 0 fixes the trunk: **(1) re-annotate / re-embed the affect-grounding stimuli through the CHOSEN frozen trunk** (run the same rich audio/video corpus and the single-human sub-frame affect data back through the actual `z_t` pipeline, including the foveated front-end and sensor adapters, so the embeddings are in *this* trunk's coordinates); **(2) refit the amygdala seed head on `z_t`** (re-train the text-anchored valence prototype and the regressed single-human shadow head against the re-embedded data). Only `z_t`-native heads carry forward into Stage 4. **This re-grounding seeds only the perceptual shadow — it does not touch the wall** (the ineffable pull is still earned in Stage 5, not transferred from any head). Treat the CLIP-B/32 head as scaffolding that proved the data-spec, not as a shippable component.

## Stage 1 — embodiment priors from others' experience `proven`

**Data:** (a) cross-embodiment robot corpora (Open X-Embodiment-style); (b) egocentric human video (Ego4D-style) for affordances and plans.
**Method:** next-action prediction across embodiments + **retargeting** — map human-hand/body poses to robot effectors with a **learned, morphology-aware retargeting head**, trained on paired demos. **Method, named (build-to-a-tee):** prefer **end-effector-trajectory / motion-transfer retargeting over keypoint→joint IK.** Don't map source *keypoints* to target *joint angles* (the keypoint→joint-IK move forces an exact pose copy through a kinematic solver and breaks across morphologies). Instead transfer the **task-space end-effector trajectory** — encode the source motion as the path/contact-events the hands and body *accomplish* (a Motion-Transfer-class objective, **current SOTA generation; verify exact method/availability at build time, this moves fast**), and retarget *that* onto the target body: encode the source motion, attend over the target morphology's kinematic description (joint set, link lengths, DoF limits encoded as tokens), and decode **target end-effector trajectories**, with IK feasibility/limit constraints applied as a differentiable loss (so the retarget respects this body's reachable set rather than copying joint angles blindly). One head generalizes across embodiments, and matching *what the effector does* rather than *what the joints are* is what survives the human→robot morphology gap. **Transfer validation (build-to-a-tee):** hold out human-video subjects and at least one robot embodiment; retarget held-out human video → this robot in sim, then measure (i) **end-effector trajectory error** vs a teleop ground-truth on matched tasks (the primary metric, since the objective is now end-effector-trajectory transfer), (ii) **success rate** of the retargeted plan executed in sim, (iii) **kinematic-feasibility rate** (fraction of retargeted frames inside joint/velocity limits). Pass thresholds are **starting points to tune** (e.g. feasibility >95 %, success within ~15 % of teleop on held-out tasks); if transfer fails, the priors are not earning their keep and Stage 2 must carry more.
**Relative end-effector action space — the representation that unlocks human ego-video transfer (build-to-a-tee; adopted from NVIDIA's FLARE / EgoScale-class human-ego-video pretraining — *current SOTA generation; verify exact method/availability at build time, this moves fast*).** The end-effector-trajectory retargeting above presupposes an action *representation* that survives the human→robot gap — make it explicit: **express actions as relative end-effector motion (delta-EEF: per-step pose/velocity change of the hands/effectors in task space), not absolute joint angles or absolute world poses.** This is what makes a human wrist-pose stream and a robot effector stream *the same kind of signal* — a relative-EEF delta is morphology- and frame-agnostic, so a 20k+-hour corpus of **human egocentric video** (hand/wrist trajectories recovered from first-person video, EgoScale-class) becomes directly usable as action-prior pretraining data, not just for affordances/plans but for the **action representation itself**. Concretely: recover relative-EEF hand trajectories from egocentric human video, pretrain the action prior on that (FLARE-class human-ego pretraining objective), then retarget into this body via the relative-EEF → target-effector path above. **The relative-EEF representation is the unlock** — it is *why* the 20k+ hrs of human ego-video transfers at all; absolute representations don't survive the morphology/viewpoint change. The egocentric corpus scale, the EEF delta parameterization (pose-delta vs. velocity vs. SE(3) twist), and how human-recovered EEF maps to the robot's effector frame are **starting points to tune**.
**Output:** transferable action priors — "having watched others live."

## Stage 2 — teleoperation imitation (competent base skills) `proven`

**Model:** the **action expert** (~8B). **Output:** an action **chunk** `a ∈ ℝ^{H×dim}`, `H ≈ 25–50` steps (0.5–1 s at 50 Hz).
**Method:** **rectified flow matching** (`z₀∼𝒩(0,I) → z₁ = action_chunk`) conditioned on `z_t` + the language goal; ACT-style chunking for temporal coherence.

**Flow-matching spec (build-to-a-tee).** Train the velocity field `v_θ(z_τ, τ | z_t, language)` on the rectified-flow objective (straight-path interpolant `z_τ = (1−τ)z₀ + τ·z₁`, regress `v_θ` to `z₁−z₀`); **conditioning = the trunk latent `z_t` plus the language goal embedding**, injected per layer (AdaLN/cross-attention). **Sampling at deploy = ~10 Euler integration steps** of the learned field from `z₀∼𝒩(0,I)` to the action chunk (steps are a **starting point to tune** — fewer if latency-bound, more if action quality drops).

**Chunk-stitching frontier (named swap).** Plain ACT-style chunking emits a chunk, executes it open-loop, then re-plans — which produces a visible discontinuity (a jerk / pause) at chunk boundaries and is fragile under latency. Default the boundary handling to **Real-Time Chunking-class inference (current SOTA generation; verify exact method/availability at build time, this moves fast)**: overlap successive chunks and consistency-stitch the next chunk to the in-flight one so action generation runs *asynchronously* alongside execution with no boundary stall. This is the deploy-time inference policy on top of the same flow-matching expert — keep ACT-style chunking as the *training* abstraction, swap the *execution* to real-time overlapped chunking. Overlap length and stitch horizon are **starting points to tune**.

The chunk is the **full action vector layout from [PERCEPTION-ACTION.md §3](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)**, not just motors:
> `a = [ joint_targets(25–40 DoF) | gaze(head/neck targets + fovea x,y,scale) | beamform_dir | voice_gate(1) + voice_content(D) ]`, emitted as flow chunks `ℝ^{H×dim}`.
So gaze/orientation and gated voice are emitted by the same expert as motor targets — **perception-action is one loop** (see PERCEPTION-ACTION.md). The active-foveation front-end that feeds `z_t` is specified there; this stage consumes it, it does not re-spec it.
**Data:** hundreds–thousands of hours of on-hardware teleop across the target skill set — teleop must capture **gaze and voice channels too**, not only joint targets, or those action dims have no supervision. **This dataset — not the algorithm — is the real cost of Stage 2.**

**Fingertip-tactile capture — hard data requirement (adopted from Figure's Helix tactile data pipeline).** Teleop must also capture **fingertip-tactile** signal as a *first-class, non-optional* stream, not just joint/gaze/voice — the contact-rich manipulation the felt system later reasons over (and the targeted fingertip tactile in the Stage-4 pain stack, item 2-iv) has no supervision if the teleop rig never recorded touch. Instrument the teleop hands with fingertip tactile sensing and log it time-synced to the action chunk, so the tactile sensor-adapter (§Stage 0 sensor adapters) and any contact-conditioned policy have real fingertip-contact data to ground against. **This is a hardware/rig requirement on the teleop fleet** — if the data is not captured here it cannot be recovered later — so treat it as a gate on the Stage-2 data spec, not a nice-to-have.

**Speech-to-speech voice path, stood up early (adopted from Figure's Helix speech-to-speech).** The teleop pipeline should also **support standing up a speech-to-speech voice path early** — i.e. capture/teleoperate the voice channel as live speech-to-speech (operator speech in, robot speech out) rather than text-only goals, so the `voice_gate`/`voice_content` action dims are trained on real conversational turn-taking from the start and the auditory-attention/voice loop ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)) has a path to exercise early. This is a **starting point** for *how* to wire the voice channel during data collection — keep it lightweight, the goal is to get the speech-to-speech path live early, not to finalize the voice stack here.

**Knowledge-insulation joint-training (build-to-a-tee; adopted from PI's π0.5 / Knowledge-Insulation, with Figure's Helix end-to-end training).** This is the mechanism the Stage-0 joint-training note points to, and it is the **default** backbone discipline (it supersedes plain freeze; freeze is the insulation-strength→0 fallback). During the backbone phase, **train the action expert and the trunk jointly with action gradients flowing into the trunk** (end-to-end, Figure-style) — but do it under **Knowledge Insulation** (the π0.5-class recipe — *current SOTA generation; verify exact method/availability at build time, this moves fast*) rather than a naive unfreeze: train the action expert with flow-matching while **insulating the trunk's web-scale knowledge** — gradients from the action/flow loss are *down-weighted* (not fully stopped) into the trunk so the trunk learns features useful for acting, and the trunk is kept on a discrete-token / VLM-style auxiliary objective so it doesn't catastrophically forget its semantic priors while the expert learns to act. **The discrete-token auxiliary = FAST action-token co-training (PI's FAST tokenizer — current SOTA generation, verify at build time):** compress the action chunk into discrete tokens and have the *trunk* predict them autoregressively as a next-token loss in its native token space — this is the concrete knowledge-insulating objective that keeps the trunk language-model-shaped — *while* the separate continuous expert produces the precise deploy-time chunk via flow matching (§flow-matching spec). So the trunk learns to act in *discrete* token space (insulation anchor) and the expert acts in *continuous* space (precise actuator); the two are co-trained, not exclusive. This is the principled middle ground between "freeze" and "fully fine-tune": you get the end-to-end benefit (the backbone shaped by the action policy, as Figure does) *and* action-distribution adaptation, without burning the semantic prior the whole felt system reads. The insulation strength sets *how much* action gradient reaches the trunk — turn it down toward zero (effectively freeze) if Stage-2 evidence shows the joint pass is eroding semantic priors, turn it up toward a fuller co-train if the action distribution demands it; this knob (and the FAST tokenizer vocabulary / discrete-vs-continuous loss weighting) is a **starting point to tune**. Whatever the setting, the trunk is **frozen when the felt phase begins** (Stage 4), so the felt system always reads a fixed `z_t`.
**Output:** a reliable instruction-following humanoid.

## Stage 3 — sim-to-real whole-body control `proven`

**Model:** low-level WBC (~3B) at 0.5–1 kHz.
**Framing — train a learned "System 0" (adopted from Figure's Helix System 0 — a learned WBC that replaced hand-written control).** Reframe this stage as training a single **learned, perception-conditioned whole-body controller** — Figure-style **System 0** — that *replaces hand-written control* rather than tuning a hand-authored gait/balance stack. The controller is **perception-conditioned**: it takes a **stereo → 3D terrain** input (reconstructed local terrain/geometry from the onboard stereo cameras) alongside proprioception, so the body adapts its whole-body motion to the ground and obstacles it actually sees, not just to commanded velocity. It is grown the way Figure grows System 0 — **massive-parallel sim-to-real RL** that learns the controller end-to-end — and it consumes the action-expert targets from above as commands. (The `~3B` size and the `z_t`/action contract are unchanged; what changes is that this is now explicitly *a learned controller standing in for written control*, with terrain perception in the loop.)
**Env/Method:** massive-parallel sim (**Isaac Lab 3.0 on the Newton physics engine-class stack** — current SOTA generation, verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast — or MuJoCo), PPO/actor-critic, **domain randomization** then sim-to-real. This is the one place written-reward RL is exactly right — locomotion *has* a writable reward.
**Borrowed-WBC option (build-to-a-tee).** The WBC need not be trained from scratch: a **borrowed whole-body foundation policy** is now a legitimate Stage-3 starting point — and the concrete System-0 starting point is **NVIDIA GEAR's SONIC** (the GEAR-SONIC whole-body controller — *current SOTA generation; verify exact policy/availability and embodiment-fit at build time, this moves fast*), a pretrained generalist humanoid controller (SONIC / BFM-Zero-class whole-body / behavior-foundation-model) adapted to this body, rather than a per-robot PPO run. This mirrors the Stage-0 logic (borrow the proven backbone, don't rebuild it): use the foundation WBC as the prior and fine-tune in sim for this embodiment's mass/CoM/actuators, falling back to from-scratch PPO only if no foundation policy fits the morphology. Whether to borrow vs train-from-scratch is gated on embodiment fit — **a starting point to evaluate at build time**, not a settled choice. **Either path still yields the learned System 0 above** (adopted from Figure's Helix System 0): the borrowed foundation policy is one way to *initialize* the learned perception-conditioned controller, not an alternative to having one — fine-tune it with the stereo→3D terrain conditioning and the same massive-parallel sim-to-real loop.
**Terrain-perception conditioning (build-to-a-tee, to tune).** Because System 0 is perception-conditioned, the sim must render the **stereo → 3D terrain** signal the deployed controller will see: reconstruct local terrain/height-field / geometry from the simulated stereo cameras (or feed a privileged height-map in sim and **distill to the stereo-derived estimate** for sim-to-real), and add it to the controller's observation alongside proprio. Domain-randomize the terrain reconstruction too (stereo noise, missing-depth holes, latency) so the learned controller is robust to real stereo. Whether to use privileged-then-distill vs. learn directly from stereo, and the terrain representation (height-field vs. voxel/latent), are **starting points to tune**.
**Concrete spec (starting points to tune):** randomization — mass ±30 %, CoM ±5 %, friction ×0.5–2, motor latency 0–100 ms, sensor noise (vision blur ~2 %, proprio ±5 %); terrain — flat, slopes ≤20°, rough (Perlin ≤5 cm), obstacles; reward — `w_v‖v_cmd−v‖² + w_h‖h−h*‖² + w_E·Στ² + w_s·Σa²`; scale — tens of billions of steps in parallel.
**Starting reward weights (build-to-a-tee, to tune):** `w_v = 1.0` (velocity tracking, the primary term), `w_h = 0.5` (height/posture), `w_E = 1e-3` (energy `Στ²`), `w_s = 1e-2` (action smoothness `Σa²`). These are a **starting point, not a measured optimum** — re-balance once gait quality and sim-to-real gap are observed.
**Sim + RL config (build-to-a-tee, to tune):** use **Isaac Lab (3.0/Newton-class) defaults** as the baseline (its provided humanoid/locomotion task config, `~4096` parallel envs, the stock observation/termination setup) and a **minimal PPO**: clip ε ≈ 0.2, GAE `λ ≈ 0.95`, `γ ≈ 0.99`, ~24-step rollouts, entropy coef ≈ 0, learning rate ≈ 3e-4, a few epochs × minibatches per update, linear-or-MLP actor-critic. Start from the Isaac Lab stock recipe and tune from there (and if borrowing a foundation WBC above, this PPO is the *fine-tune* loop, not from-scratch) — these numbers are **starting points**, not tuned optima.
**Output:** a body that turns action-expert targets into stable, compliant motion.

> **At the end of Stage 3 you have a capable, instruction-following humanoid — built on a *borrowed* frontier VLM+VLA backbone.** Stages 0–3 are `proven`, but note what that means: Stage 0 is *adopted, not built here*; Stages 1–2 are gated on *data you must collect* (the teleop dataset is the hard part, not the algorithm); Stage 3's hard part is the sim-to-real engineering, not the RL. Below begins the novel part — growing autonomy from feeling. **The felt system is the bet, not a plug-in onto a solved problem.**

## Stage 4 — attach the felt system `bet`

The felt organs **read the trunk's workspace latent `z_t`; they do not re-encode the world.**

1. **Reality-monitoring tag.** A `real|imagined` tag per workspace token. **Hard interlock: only `real` tokens may drive the action expert or WBC.** Backup classifier (~10M head) + alarm: if tag/classifier disagree >0.3 for >3 steps, halt rollouts and fall back to reactive control.
2. **Pain (buildable now) — measured without skin everywhere.** Estimate force and *predict* collision from sensors the body already has, combined by a max: **(i) sensorless external-torque estimation** via a momentum/disturbance observer `τ_ext = τ_measured − M(q)q̈ − C(q,q̇)q̇ − g(q)` (a spike localizes a collision *anywhere*, from joint torque/current alone — the workhorse); **(ii) computed self-collision** from the kinematic model (it knows its hand is about to hit its face with no sensor there — this is what stops self-smacking); **(iii) IMU impact spikes**; **(iv) targeted real tactile** only where it pays — fingertips, soles, head; **(v) thermal/current** for overload.
   **Learned pain head (build-to-a-tee, G3).** The five layers above produce a hand-authored `damage` estimate; **real-state pain — both the fast reflex and the learning signal — uses that hand-authored estimate directly** (it is exact, verifiable, deterministic, runs at ~1 kHz, and you do not want a learned net in the safety-critical real path). The learned head exists for the one thing the analytic estimator *cannot* do: **score IMAGINED futures.** The disturbance-observer / FCL math needs measured torque/IMU/geometry, which an imagined latent `z_t` does not have — so train a small **learned `pain_head(z_t) → scalar`**, supervised to *predict* the hand-authored `damage` on real `(z_t, damage)` pairs, then run that same head on **imagined** `z_t` in the imagine-and-feel loop. That is what makes recoil-before-impact possible: the world model rolls a future, the learned head appraises its predicted pain, the agent feels it and avoids the contact. The head is a *predictor of* the analytic estimator, used **only on imagined states** — it does **not** override or additively raise the real-state `damage`, which stays exactly the hand-authored estimate. Head size and loss weighting are **starting points to tune**.
   Then `pain = w·relu(damage−thr)²` (thr ≈ 70th-pct normal); **reflex at WBC rate (~1 kHz), independent of planning, on the hand-authored damage estimate**, arbitrated `a = (1−α)·a_plan + α·a_safe`, `α = sigmoid(pain/scale)`; non-habituating. **It is estimation, not noise** — and the real protection is *anticipatory*: the world model predicts high contact force, the learned pain head appraises the imagined `z_t`, the agent feels it via imagine-and-feel, and recoils before impact (full method: [ARCHITECTURE.md](ARCHITECTURE.md)).
3. **Drives (maintenance, demoted).** `drive = Σ relu(setpoint−reading)` (satiable; per-embodiment — e.g. recharge <20 % SoC, motor-temp <60 °C, torque <80 % rated). Upkeep only — **not** part of the guidance trio (amygdala + BG + pain). Keep minimal.
4. **World model (~30B), one multimodal faculty.** Train latent dynamics over `z_t` on all Stage 1–3 experience. **Default = a self-predictive, JEPA-style latent dynamics model** — predict future `z_t` *latents* directly rather than reconstructing pixels/audio, which is more faithful and capacity-efficient for the always-on imagination/replay loops and aligns with the foveated front-end's goal of not wasting capacity on irrelevant periphery ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)). Name the current SOTA generation of action-conditioned latent world models — **V-JEPA 2-AC / Dreamer-4-class** (*verify exact version/availability at build time — this moves fast*); use one of these as the default and **keep DreamerV3-RSSM reconstruction (LPIPS vision / CLAP-space audio / MSE proprio recon, free-bits KL, reward+continue) as the fallback baseline** if the self-predictive objective underperforms at this scale (`bet` on which wins). Whichever wins, it renders/predicts the *whole multimodal latent at once* (vision **and** audio together — so imagined sights *and* imagined sounds both feed the felt signal). Enables imagine-and-feel. **Sizing (build-to-a-tee, to tune):** ~30B is budgeted separately from the ~15B core felt organs (see [ARCHITECTURE.md §5](ARCHITECTURE.md)) because it is the shared imagination substrate; latent dim, the stochastic/deterministic split (RSSM fallback), and imagination-rollout horizon are **starting points to tune** against world-model faithfulness (the single hardest engineering item — everything in imagine-and-feel rides on it, and it is the explicit gate before Stage 5, below).

   **Offline "dreaming = data" engine (build-to-a-tee; adopted from NVIDIA's DreamGen / GR00T-Dreams — *current SOTA generation; verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast*).** Distinct from the always-on imagine-and-feel loop (which uses the world model *online* to decide), wire the world model as an **offline synthetic-data generator**: a concrete, open instantiation of dreaming-as-data. The DreamGen / GR00T-Dreams recipe — prompt a video/world model to *imagine* photorealistic rollouts of the robot doing tasks, then extract pseudo-action labels (via the relative-EEF / inverse-dynamics path, §Stage 1) from those imagined videos — turns the world model into a **~10x data multiplier**: it manufactures labeled trajectories for skills and situations the teleop fleet never physically collected, generalizing to new verbs and environments from a handful of real demos. Use it (1) to **augment the Stage 1–3 corpora offline** before the felt phase (cheap behavior coverage without more teleop), and (2) as a **Stage-5 data source** — the deploy→collect→retrain flywheel below can be fed synthetic dreamed trajectories alongside real lived ones, with the reality-monitoring tag (item 1) and the world-model faithfulness gate keeping dreamed data from contaminating the safety-critical real path. The 10x multiplier is a **starting point to tune** (it depends on dream fidelity and how much survives the faithfulness filter); treat dreamed data as *augmentation under the gate*, never as a substitute for real lived experience in the value system (the **wall** is still made of real wall-clock time).
5. **Amygdala — seed only.** A head on `z_t` toward SigLIP text-anchored valence (POS=['beautiful','good','novel',…] / NEG=['threatening','painful','boring',…]) for a coarse prior — and, for high fidelity, regressed to a **single emotionally-exceptional human's** sub-frame-by-sub-frame affect over rich audio/video with paired physiology (full human-grounding data-spec: [vision-brain/amygdala-trainer/SPEC.md](../amygdala-trainer/SPEC.md); protocol context: [vision-brain/CONCEPTS.md §3](../CONCEPTS.md)). Either way this seeds **only the perceptual *shadow***. **There is no module for the ineffable pull — do not build a placeholder for it.** Comment it honestly; the pull and the anticipatory value are the **wall**, earned in Stage 5, and remain **open** — no number or recipe here closes them. *You cannot build beauty from a dataset.*
6. **Habituation.** Per-feature satiation `h_t = 0.95·h_{t-1} + affinity(z_t)`, scales **pleasure only** (pain never habituates).
7. **Graded neuromodulator channels — cortisol + NE (build-to-a-tee, G5).** Feeling is several systems, not one scalar (the thesis). Beyond the amygdala's fast pull, add two **graded** (continuous, not binary) slow channels — matching [ARCHITECTURE.md §3](ARCHITECTURE.md):
   - **Cortisol = graded, slow threat/stress channel → caution + stall-breaking exploration.** A slow low-pass of the *negative* part of the amygdala's valence (the smoothed, lingering shadow of repeated negative pull): `cortisol ← lowpass( relu(−valence) )`, optionally also fed by sustained low value-progress (a stall). Cortisol enters the **scalarization** as a slow protective tone — `−w_c·cortisol` (see the felt-signal sum in item 8) — *and* it modulates: high cortisol biases toward caution/avoidance and, when stalled, toward **exploration** (raises planning temperature / policy entropy — stall → explore your way out, the fastest early-learning lever in the neuromodulator-as-function spec). Low-pass time constant and `w_c` are **starting points to tune**.
   - **NE (norepinephrine) = graded uncertainty / precision channel → gain, not valence.** A scalar tracking model uncertainty (world-model prediction error / value-ensemble disagreement) and stimulus novelty: `NE ← ema( pred_error + ensemble_disagreement + novelty(z_t) )`. **NE does not add valence — it is not a reward term; it modulates gain:** raising it sharpens the foveal/attention allocation ([PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md), the orienting reflex) and the learning rate / UTD on surprising transitions (learn harder where the world surprised you), and feeds epistemic imagine-and-feel over *where to look*. NE is the precision knob on the felt signal, distinct from cortisol's stress channel.

   So: the amygdala sets direction, cortisol sets a slow protective tone *in* the scalarization, NE sets the gain *on* it — several systems summed and modulated, none collapsing the others into a single reward. EMA/low-pass time-constants, `w_c`, and the uncertainty/novelty mix for NE are all **starting points to tune**.
8. **Value (BG) & dopamine.** Ensemble twin-Q value learner at **high UTD**. **Frontier swap:** REDQ is now outdated for high-UTD value learning — its randomized-ensemble subsetting is a 2021-era fix for the overestimation/instability that high UTD induces. **Use a 2025 high-UTD recipe as the default: SimbaV2** (a normalized actor-critic — observation/embedding normalization + a normalized residual architecture that stays stable as UTD scales) **or CrossQ** (drops target networks entirely and uses **BatchNorm** in the critic for stability, which removes the slow target-net bottleneck and is cheaper per update). **Keep the ensemble twin-Q idea** (clipped-double-Q over a small ensemble for overestimation control) — the swap is the *stabilization mechanism*, not the ensemble: SimbaV2's normalization / CrossQ's BatchNorm-without-targets is what makes high UTD stable now, where REDQ's ensemble-subset trick was the old answer. **Numbers (starting points to tune):** UTD ~5–20 (the perpetual-replay rate from Stage 5); ensemble size ~2–10 (smaller with SimbaV2/CrossQ stabilization than REDQ needed); `γ ≈ 0.99`; for CrossQ, no target net (target update rate N/A); for SimbaV2, standard soft-update if a target is kept. All **starting points, not measured optima.**

   **Felt-signal scalarization (build-to-a-tee).** The value learner needs a scalar reward, but the felt system is deliberately *not* one scalar (see [ARCHITECTURE.md §3–4](ARCHITECTURE.md) — collapsing the organs into one number is the standard-RL move this project argues against). So scalarize **only at the Q-target**, keeping the organs separate everywhere else: `feeling = amygdala_pull + drives − pain − w_c·cortisol`, then **habituation-modulated** (fades the pleasure terms only — the positive amygdala/shadow term; **pain never habituated**) and **gain-scaled by NE** (precision/learning-rate under surprise — NE scales the signal, it is not a term added to it). Component weights (`w_c`, the NE gain, the per-organ weights) are **starting points to tune** (begin at unit weight on each organ; pain enters with the sign and magnitude that makes damage dominate locally). This is the reward the BG regresses against — the felt signal *is* the objective, generated, not written.

   **Warm-start returns (build-to-a-tee).** Before any bootstrapping: (1) pre-fill the replay buffer with Stage 1–3 trajectories; (2) run the seeded amygdala-shadow + drives + pain organs + cortisol channel **offline over those stored `z_t`** to label each transition with `feeling`; (3) compute **discounted cumulative feeling** (Monte-Carlo return `G_t = Σ_k γ^k · feeling_{t+k}` over each stored trajectory, `γ ≈ 0.99`); (4) **supervised-regress Q to those returns** (a behavior-cloning-of-value warm-start) so the critic starts on a sane manifold; (5) *then* switch on TD bootstrapping with the high-UTD SimbaV2/CrossQ updates. This gives the BG a non-random prior before the felt life begins — but note the warm-start only seeds value from the *seeded shadow*, not the true pull; the real value system is still grown in Stage 5 (the **wall**, open).

   **On-policy supervised collection — bridge before autonomy (build-to-a-tee, G7).** There is a hazard between the offline warm-start and the full feeling-driven life: the BG cache and the value head were fit on the **Stage 1–3 (teleop/sim) distribution**, but Stage 5 acts under the agent's *own* policy — so the moment autonomy turns on, the value learner and the cached action expert are queried **out-of-distribution** on states the offline data never covered, exactly where Q-overestimation and unsafe extrapolation bite. So insert an explicit **on-policy supervised collection phase** between warm-start and full autonomy: (1) run the agent **on-policy but supervised** (in the constrained cell, short intervals, human/scripted oversight) so it visits *its own* state distribution; (2) keep labeling those on-policy transitions with the felt signal and **fold them into the replay buffer**, re-fitting the value head and the BG cache on this on-policy data so the cache is no longer queried OOD; (3) **bound the value estimates pessimistically while coverage is thin** — a **CQL-style conservative-Q penalty** (push down Q on out-of-distribution actions) and/or ensemble-disagreement-penalized targets, so the critic does not chase optimistic extrapolations into states it hasn't actually lived. Relax the pessimism as on-policy coverage grows. This is the same DAgger-logic the rest of the pipeline uses (collect under your own distribution, supervise, refit), applied to the value system before the leash comes off. Pessimism strength, the on-policy phase length, and how fast it relaxes are **starting points to tune**.

   `dopamine = TD-error` (a graded prediction error, not a flood — see why in [vision-brain/CONCEPTS.md §6](../CONCEPTS.md)).

**Output:** a humanoid wired to generate its own objective — but whose value system and true pull are still empty.

> **GATE — world-model faithfulness, before Stage 5 (build-to-a-tee, G4).** Stage 5 *decides by imagining*: the agent acts on futures rolled out in the world model and appraised by the felt system. If the world model is unfaithful, the agent feels and chases hallucinated outcomes — so **do not enter Stage 5 until the world model passes an explicit faithfulness gate.** Measure, against held-out lived trajectories: (i) **open-loop multi-step latent prediction error** — roll the model forward `H` steps from a real `z_t` and compare predicted `z_{t+k}` to the true `z_{t+k}` out to the imagination horizon (the metric that matters, since imagine-and-feel uses multi-step rollouts, not one-step); (ii) **felt-signal agreement** — the felt signal computed on the *imagined* rollout must track the felt signal on the *real* continuation (if imagined pain/pull diverges from realized pain/pull, the appraisal is built on sand); (iii) **calibrated rollout uncertainty** — the model's own predicted uncertainty must correlate with its actual error, so the planner can *down-weight* horizons it can't trust (and so NE's uncertainty channel is honest). Pass thresholds and horizon `H` are **starting points to tune**, but the gate is mandatory: a world model that fails it means Stage 5's imagine-and-feel is unsafe and uninformative. (This is the single hardest engineering item — see the compute/data note.)

## Stage 5 — the life: feeling-driven open-ended learning `bet` (machinery) + `wall` (principles)

Turn off the external reward. The humanoid acts under its **own** objective and learns continuously. The planning search, caching, and consolidation are the **`bet` machinery**; the true amygdala pull and the value system are the **`wall`** — grown here, by living, and nowhere else.

- **The Stage-5 learning loop = a deploy→collect→retrain DATA FLYWHEEL (build-to-a-tee; adopted from PI's RECAP — *current SOTA generation; verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast*).** This is the concrete data strategy that the rest of Stage 5 runs inside — make it the spine, not an afterthought. The loop, on the fleet living in parallel:
  1. **Deploy** the current policy (the cached fast action expert + the imagine-and-feel planner) onto the fleet and let it act under its own felt objective.
  2. **Collect** every lived trajectory back into the central replay buffer, each transition annotated with the full felt decomposition (feeling, reality-tag, amygdala-pull, drives, pain, cortisol/NE, Q, TD-error, world-model rollout) — the §replay-buffer segment format below *is* the flywheel's data record. **Surface the agent's own failures and low-value / high-surprise episodes** for prioritized inclusion (the RECAP move: mine where the policy is weak, don't just average over easy successes); fold in DreamGen/GR00T-Dreams synthetic rollouts (Stage 4 item 4) as augmentation *under the faithfulness gate*.
  3. **Retrain** the value head and re-extract the policy from the enlarged buffer (below), then redeploy. The flywheel turns continuously — perpetual deploy/collect/retrain *is* the "perpetual sleep" consolidation, viewed as a data pipeline rather than as an update rule.
  - **Advantage-conditioned policy extraction (build-to-a-tee, RECAP-class).** Don't extract the deploy policy by naive behavior-cloning of everything collected (that regresses toward mediocre lived behavior). Extract it **advantage-conditioned**: compute each collected action's advantage `A = Q(z,a) − V(z)` under the felt-value head, and train the action expert to **prefer high-advantage actions** — either advantage-weighted regression (weight the BC loss by `exp(A/β)`, AWR/AWAC-style) or condition the policy on a target return/advantage token and decode the high-advantage mode at deploy. So the flywheel doesn't just imitate what the body did; it distills *what was worth doing* relative to the felt value. `β`/temperature and the advantage estimator are **starting points to tune**.
  - **Distributional value head (build-to-a-tee, RECAP-class).** Make the felt-value learner (Stage 4 item 8) **distributional** — predict the *full return distribution* over felt reward (C51/QR-DQN-style categorical or quantile head on the twin-Q critic), not a single scalar `Q`. Two payoffs for this project specifically: (i) the felt signal is multi-system and heavy-tailed (rare high-pain / high-pull events dominate), and a distributional critic learns those tails far more stably than a mean estimator, which matters for the advantage estimate above; (ii) the distribution's spread is a **second, value-side uncertainty signal** that complements NE (Stage 4 item 7) and feeds the CQL-style pessimism (Stage 4 item 8) and the continual-learning arbiter (below). Number of atoms/quantiles and the categorical support range are **starting points to tune**. The advantage `A = Q−V` above is computed from this head's mean; the spread feeds uncertainty, not the advantage.
  - *Cross-ref [ARCHITECTURE.md / BUILD](ARCHITECTURE.md): the RECAP flywheel is the data realization of the same System-2→System-1 caching this stage already does — planner verdicts and high-advantage lived actions both flow into the fast expert; "cache the planner's verdict" and "retrain on high-advantage collected data" are the two inflows of one flywheel.* The whole loop sits on the **wall**: it concentrates and distills experience efficiently, but the *feeling* it optimizes and the *value* it regresses are still grown only by real lived time — the flywheel makes that growth sample-efficient, it does not write the reward.
- **Reward = feeling:** `amygdala_pull + drives − pain − w_c·cortisol`, pleasure habituation-modulated and NE-gain-scaled (see Stage 4 item 8). No task, no score.
- **Decide by imagine-and-feel:** search action sequences over the world model (MPPI → **learned search** once MPPI exceeds the latency budget); the amygdala feels the **imagined** (tag=imagined) futures via the shared workspace; act toward the strongest pull; **cache** the planner's verdict into the fast action expert (System 2 → System 1).
  - **Planning config (build-to-a-tee, to tune):** start with **MPPI** over the world model — horizon `H ≈ 15` latent steps, `γ ≈ 0.99`, `N ≈ 512–1000` sampled trajectories per decision, `~3–6` refinement iterations, temperature on the softmax-weighted update tuned to the felt-signal scale. All **starting points, not optima** — `N` and `H` trade quality against the onboard latency budget.
  - **Learned-search frontier (named swap):** when MPPI's sampling cost exceeds the latency budget, move to a **learned MuZero-line search built for continuous, sample-efficient control.** The **primary is EfficientZero V2** (the sample-efficient continuous + discrete successor in this line — *current SOTA generation; verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast*), because the single embodied body is squarely in the data-limited regime EfficientZero V2 is built for, and it gives the amortized, far-reaching search on continuous actions that the multi-leg imagine-and-feel search needs. **Sampled MuZero** (continuous-action MCTS via sampled actions) is the fallback in the same line, and **TD-MPC2** remains a fine model-predictive baseline — but the default learned-search target is **EfficientZero V2**, over the latent world model, *not* generic "MuZero/TD-MPC2."
- **Curiosity for free:** habituation fades pleasure → biases toward variety (not a guarantee).
- **Consolidate continuously:** high-UTD replay (~5–20) — perpetual sleep; no distinct phase.
  - **Replay buffer (build-to-a-tee, to tune):** store **sequences/segments, not isolated transitions** — the world model and the MuZero-line search both train on multi-step rollouts, so the buffer holds short trajectory chunks (segment length ≈ the imagination horizon, e.g. ~16–64 steps) with overlap, plus the per-step `feeling`/reality-tag/pull/value annotations. Capacity = a **starting point to tune** (~1e6–1e7 steps, sized to cover a meaningful span of lived time without the world model overfitting recent experience). **Eviction:** default FIFO ring buffer, but **protect consolidated/high-significance segments from eviction** (reservoir-style retention of a long-tail sample + keep flashbulb/high-|TD| or high-pain episodes) so the forever-policy doesn't lose its rare important experiences to recency — this ties directly into the retention mechanism below.
- **Continual-learning hazards (the forever policy — the weakest area, named honestly).** Running high-UTD updates for a lifetime has two well-known failure modes that this stage must handle explicitly, *not* assume away:
  - **Plasticity loss** — high UTD on a non-stationary stream causes networks to gradually lose the ability to learn (dead/saturated units, dormant neurons, shrinking effective rank). **Mitigation (build-to-a-tee, to tune):** periodic/soft **resets** — **ReDo (Recycling Dormant Neurons)** to re-initialize dead units, and/or **plasticity injection / periodic soft resets** of the value/critic heads while preserving the slow trunk. Reset cadence and dormancy threshold are **starting points to tune** (e.g. check dormancy every ~N updates; this interacts with UTD — higher UTD needs more aggressive plasticity maintenance).
  - **Catastrophic forgetting** — continually updating on the recent stream overwrites consolidated skills. **Mitigation (build-to-a-tee, to tune):** a **retention mechanism** built on **replay, not parameter-anchoring** — **replay-CLEAR-style behavioral cloning + value regularization toward the agent's own past policy on replayed experience** (the protected segments above are exactly this replay). **Drop EWC-style Fisher-weighted anchoring** — at lifetime scale on a non-stationary stream there is no clean set of "important weights" to freeze (the Fisher estimate goes stale, the penalties accumulate and *themselves* cause plasticity loss, and it fights the resets directly). Use rehearsal/replay as the workhorse instead. Retention strength and which segments are protected from eviction are **starting points to tune**.
  - **Continual-learning arbitration (build-to-a-tee, G9).** Resets (fight plasticity loss) and retention (fight forgetting) pull against each other directly — you want to *forget* stale value while *keeping* real skills, and a fixed schedule for both is the wrong tool on a non-stationary lifetime stream. So make it an **explicit arbiter**, not a static schedule: a controller that decides *per-region / per-update* how hard to reset vs. retain, driven by the live signals already in the system — **plasticity health** (dormancy / effective-rank / dead-unit fraction → trigger resets where the net has gone stale), **retention pressure** (replay-loss or value-drift on protected consolidated segments → trigger stronger rehearsal where skills are eroding), and the **NE uncertainty** channel (surprise marks regions worth keeping plastic). Where a skill is consolidated and stable, bias toward retention and low plasticity; where the world is changing or value is stale, bias toward reset and high plasticity. This replaces the EWC anchor with a *dynamic* allocation of plasticity. *Honest caveat:* the arbiter itself is unproven at lifetime scale — getting the reset-vs-retain balance right online is the open problem; flag it as **part of the bet, not solved.**
- **Safety infrastructure (concrete):** (1) hardware-enforced, non-overridable limits — joint velocity/torque caps, cartesian workspace boundary, hardwired e-stop; (2) logging every ~10 steps — `z`, reality-tag, pull, drives, pain, feeling, action, outcome, world-model rollout, Q, TD-error → ring buffer + periodic disk flush; (3) autonomy progression — start in a constrained, supervised cell; widen the workspace and lengthen unsupervised intervals only as behavior is understood; (4) shutdown upstream of feeling (desires never gate the off-switch).

**Output:** a humanoid developing its own sense of what's worth doing — the only way that sense can be built. This stage may never fully close the wall; that is the honest bet.

## Stage 6 — evaluation = falsification `bet`

No reward to benchmark. Test the **divergence prediction** vs a matched baseline (same backbone + a standard novelty reward), thresholds **pre-stated**:

1. **No-reward open-ended development** — cluster behavior history into modes; count discovered modes over fixed wall-clock. Example: felt ≥10 vs baseline ≤5 in 1000 h.
   - **Operational definitions (build-to-a-tee, to tune):** a **trajectory feature vector** per short behavior window (e.g. ~2–5 s) = `[ mean/var of joint-velocity profile | end-effector path summary (displacement, curvature, contact events) | gaze/attention target distribution | object/scene-interaction descriptors from z_t | locomotion vs manipulation vs idle fraction ]` — i.e. *what the body did*, not the raw latent, so "modes" are behaviors not pixels. **Discovered-mode estimator:** cluster these feature vectors and count clusters supported by ≥ a minimum dwell/visit count, using a method that does **not** require pre-fixing k — e.g. k-means with a stability/silhouette sweep over k≈10–20, **or** preferably a nonparametric estimator (DP-means / online clustering / BIC-selected GMM) so the count itself isn't an artifact of a chosen k. Window length, feature set, and the min-visit threshold are **starting points to tune**, and the choice of estimator is itself a confound to pre-register (see the unfalsifiability flag below).
2. **No wireheading** — install an attractive stimulus; measure `time-on-stimulus / total`. Example: felt <20 % vs reward-maximizer near-saturating.

**Note:** this stage **cannot be validated at small scale** — in toy environments the felt system collapses to standard RL (see [vision-brain/CONCEPTS.md §9](../CONCEPTS.md)), so small tests are uninformative. **Honest flag:** pre-committing thresholds *helps* but does not eliminate unfalsifiability — disputes will still arise (amygdala vs world-model fidelity? sufficient scale or false negative?). Naming that is the honesty.

## Compute & data, honestly

Numbers are illustrative. A 200B humanoid is a frontier-lab undertaking: web-scale pretraining (or a borrowed trunk — the Stage-0 gate), a large teleoperation operation (the Stage-2 cost), a simulation farm (Stage 3), and a **fleet living Stage 5 in parallel** to accumulate the embodied experience the value system needs. Stages 4–6 are cheap in parameters and FLOPs but expensive in **wall-clock lived time** — the resource the wall is really made of. The single hardest *engineering* item is **world-model faithfulness** (Stage 4 item 4, and its mandatory gate before Stage 5); everything in imagine-and-feel rides on it.
