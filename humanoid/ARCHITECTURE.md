# Architecture — this humanoid spec

The full stack, from sensors to actuators, with the felt system attached as a separate module reading `z_t`. Two halves: the **backbone** (borrowed, proven robot-foundation-model technology) and the **felt system** (the bet — the [root thesis](../README.md) guiding principle, embodied).

---

## 1 · Sensing

A humanoid is a partially-observed system; it needs rich, time-extended sensing.

- **Vision** — multiple cameras: head-mounted stereo (egocentric), wrist cameras for manipulation, wide-FOV for situational awareness. Encoded by a pretrained vision encoder (ViT / **DINOv3** / **SigLIP 2**-class — current SOTA generation; consider a **C-RADIO**-style unified encoder that distills multiple teachers into one backbone; verify exact version/availability at build time — this moves fast), and **foveated, not uniform high-res** — a wide low-res peripheral stream plus a movable high-res foveal crop aimed by attention. Full active-perception design (vision, audio, and outputs) in **[PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)**.
- **Audio** — microphones for speech, environmental sound, and contact acoustics. Pretrained audio encoder (**AF-Whisper** / Audio Flamingo 3-class — current SOTA generation; verify exact version/availability at build time — this moves fast).
- **Touch & proprioception** — joint encoders, IMUs, and tactile arrays on hands/feet, plus force/torque at wrists and ankles. Not optional: contact-rich manipulation and balance are impossible without them. Encode tactile through a **semantic-aligned tactile encoder** (a learned encoder that maps touch into the shared latent alongside vision/language — e.g. a touch-vision-language-aligned tactile backbone), **not** a raw per-taxel MLP; this lets contact signals carry meaning the trunk and amygdala can read, the same way the vision encoder does (`standard` direction, current SOTA generation — verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast). **Fingertip tactile is a hard requirement, fused end-to-end (adopted from Figure's Helix — fingertip tactile fused into the policy).** Upgrade from "tactile where it pays": **high-sensitivity fingertip sensing (~3-gram-class detection — a starting point to tune)** is a **hard requirement**, not a strategic option, and its signal is **fused end-to-end into the policy** through the semantic-aligned encoder above (kept), not bolted on as a separate threshold. Figure proves this is buildable *and* load-bearing for contact-rich manipulation — fine-grained fingertip force is what lets a hand grasp, slip-detect, and modulate force on delicate objects. (The sparse-but-strategic targeting in §3 still applies to *non-fingertip* surfaces — soles, head/face; fingertips are now a floor, not a trade-off.)
- **Visual proprioception** — the agent **sees its own hands and body in-frame** and uses that to self-calibrate (adopted from Figure). Observing its own limbs in the camera stream lets it close the loop between commanded pose and *seen* pose — correcting kinematic drift, calibration error, and wear without external instrumentation. It is cheap (no added hardware — it reuses the §1 cameras) and **fleet-scalable** (every body self-calibrates from its own video, so calibration improves across a fleet without manual per-robot tuning). This complements joint-encoder proprioception rather than replacing it: encoders give the internal estimate, vision grounds it against the world (`standard` direction, adopted from Figure — verify framing at build time).
- **Interoception** — the body's own state (battery/energy, actuator temperature and load, structural strain). This is what the **drives** and **pain** organs read.

All exteroceptive streams are tokenized and fused; interoception feeds the felt system directly.

## 2 · The backbone — a tiered VLA (borrowed)

> **Framing — this is the v1 de-risked path toward a unified world-model cortex (read this first).** The *target* architecture is **one unified world-model-centric cortex**: a single co-trained model that *predicts and acts* in one network sharing a latent `z_t` — that is the thesis. The tiered backbone specced below is the **pragmatic v1 / de-risked bootstrap toward it**, not the end state. We borrow a frontier VLM-VLA trunk (the deployed-frontier shortcut) and run a **separate ~30B world model as an interim measure — precisely because today's borrowed trunks are *not* self-predictive** (they fuse and act but don't model their own future `z_t`). As the trunk line moves to self-predictive (the JEPA / Cosmos line), the separate world model **folds into the cortex** and the stack converges to the target. Read the whole of §2 as "v1 path toward the unified cortex," not "the backbone, full stop." (One more reconciliation up front: this design **rejects the LLM as the sensorimotor skeleton/cortex** — language is the wrong substrate for the felt, embodied core — but **accepts an LLM-class model as a deliberation *tier above*** it, the Tier-0 orchestrator below. The NL-reasoning orchestrator is therefore not a contradiction with "LLM rejected": it sits over the cortex, it is not the cortex.)

The current frontier for general robot control is the **vision-language-action (VLA)** foundation model, organized as coupled systems running at different speeds — the pattern shared by GR00T, π0, and Helix-class systems. The original dual-system split (trunk + action expert) is now generalizing to a **three-tier** organization in the current SOTA generation (verify exact framing/availability at build time — this moves fast): an **embodied-reasoning orchestrator** that does slow deliberate reasoning and task decomposition, a **thinking trunk** that fuses modalities into the shared latent, and an **action expert** that emits motion. The tiers below map onto this; the felt system **attaches as a separate module reading `z_t`** above all of them.

**Figure's Helix validates this tiered shape (independent convergence).** Figure's Helix stack — a slow deliberative tier handing intent to a fast onboard action layer, with a learned System 0 controller beneath — arrived at the same coupled-tier organization independently of GR00T/π0, which is corroborating evidence that the tiered-VLA shape this backbone borrows is the right one (not a single-lab artifact). Several of the specifics below are adopted directly from Helix and flagged inline as such.

### Tier 0 — embodied-reasoning orchestrator (slowest)
A reasoning layer that sits *above* the trunk: it does explicit, deliberate task reasoning — multi-step planning, tool/skill selection, long-horizon goal decomposition — at the slowest rate, handing structured intent down to the trunk. This is the "System-2-of-System-2" the frontier has split out from the single multimodal trunk; in the dual-system framing its work lived inside the trunk's semantic-reasoning head, now made its own tier. It is where the agent's *deliberation* lives; the amygdala's pull and imagine-and-feel (§3) feed it the objective it reasons toward. This is an LLM-class deliberation tier *over* the cortex — the configuration this design **rejects as the sensorimotor skeleton but accepts as the orchestrator above it** (see the §2 framing note). (`standard` direction, current SOTA generation — verify exact framing at build time.)

**The orchestrator↔felt interface / arbitration (build it, don't leave it implied).** Pin how the deliberation tier and the felt system divide the decision, because both produce "what to do" and the boundary must be explicit: **the felt pull + the BG value are surfaced to the orchestrator as the objective it reasons toward** (not a command it obeys, an objective it deliberates over); **the orchestrator sets a subgoal / selects a skill**; **imagine-and-feel + the BG cache (§3) then choose the concrete action sequence that maximizes felt value *toward that subgoal*** over the world model. So the orchestrator picks *where to go*, the felt system picks *how to get there by feeling the futures*, and the felt value is also the orchestrator's own criterion. The safety interlocks still bound the whole exchange: **real-only actuation** and the **`real | imagined` provenance tag** (§3, §4) apply unchanged — the orchestrator's subgoal and the imagined search run over `z_t` of any provenance, but only `real`-tagged states ever drive the action expert. (Build stage: TRAINING Stage 4.5.)

**Backbone candidate — don't build one, borrow it (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA).** The orchestrator is a borrowed reasoning VLM, not a thing this project trains from scratch. The named starting-point backbone is **Cosmos Reason (NVIDIA)** — a ~8B open-weights embodied-reasoning model built for exactly this tier: long chain-of-thought over video, explicit spatial/temporal grounding, and physical-commonsense planning. Use it as the Tier-0 orchestrator's reasoning core and adapt downward; don't reinvent a deliberative VLM (current SOTA generation — verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast). Its open weights also keep the deliberation tier inspectable, which the §Safety story wants.

**The concrete Tier-0 mechanism — an interleaved embodied-thinking trace (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA/Gemini).** Make the orchestrator's deliberation *concrete* (not an abstract "reasoning layer") by having it emit an **interleaved natural-language embodied-thinking trace** — short legible thoughts produced *before and between* action chunks, with **mid-rollout re-planning**: the orchestrator narrates its plan, the action expert executes a chunk, and the orchestrator re-reads the world and revises the trace before the next chunk rather than committing the whole plan up front. This is what closes the Tier-0 loop in practice — the trunk and expert run their fast tempos while the orchestrator thinks in language on its slow clock and steers between chunks. It is also a **free observability/safety win**: the trace makes the agent's *wants and reasoning legible in plain language* (logged and inspectable), which directly serves the §Safety "legible wants" goal — the felt pull supplies the objective, the trace exposes the deliberation toward it (current SOTA generation — verify exact framing at build time, this moves fast).

**Spatial grounding + a progress/success-estimation head (currently ABSENT, load-bearing).** The orchestrator cannot close its loop on language alone — it needs to *point at the world* and *know whether it is making progress*. Add two pieces, both currently missing from this stack and both load-bearing:
1. **Explicit spatial grounding** — the orchestrator emits and consumes **2D points / 3D bounding boxes / multi-view correspondence** over the camera streams, so its plan references concrete locations ("grasp *here*", "the gap is *there*") that the trunk and expert can act on. This is the spatial half of Cosmos-Reason-class grounding; ground the trace in coordinates, not just words.
2. **A progress / success-estimation head** — a **success detector** the orchestrator reads to judge whether a step worked, when to re-plan, and when a subgoal is done. Without it the mid-rollout re-planning above has no feedback signal. It is a small head whose **input is `z_t` and/or the subgoal embedding** and whose **output is a per-subgoal progress scalar**; it is **consumed by the orchestrator as its re-plan trigger** (progress stalls ⇒ re-read the world and revise the trace). Labels come from **teleop episode boundaries + sparse human success in Stage 2, distilled Gemini-style during the felt phase** (build details + build stage: TRAINING Stage 4.5). (This head is distinct from, and complementary to, the felt `pain(z)` head and the BG value: it estimates *task* progress/success, not affect or long-horizon value.)
Both are `standard` direction (adopted from NVIDIA) — current SOTA generation, verify exact framing/availability at build time, this moves fast.

**The orchestrator calls the controller as a tool (native tool-use interface, `standard`, adopted from NVIDIA/Gemini).** The interface from Tier 0 down is **native tool-use**: the orchestrator treats the trunk/action-expert (and external skills, APIs, and sensors) as **tools it calls**, emitting structured tool calls inline in its embodied-thinking trace and consuming their results. "Orchestrator calls controller-as-a-tool" is the concrete handoff — the deliberative tier doesn't directly emit motion, it *invokes* the lower tiers as named, typed tools, which keeps the boundary clean and makes the trace (above) a literal log of which controller/skill was called when (current SOTA generation — verify exact framing at build time, this moves fast).

### System 2 — the thinking trunk (~135B)
A large multimodal transformer that fuses vision, audio, touch, proprioception, and language over a **time window** (seconds of history = working memory). It carries internet-scale semantic priors from VLM pretraining and produces:
- a **latent world state** — the representation everything downstream reads, and
- **semantic reasoning** — goal understanding, language grounding, task decomposition (now shared with / handed up to the Tier-0 orchestrator).

Runs slowly (~1–10 Hz): it sets *intent*, not joint torques.

**Sensor adapters (borrowing a VLM means it has no body).** A pretrained VLM trunk ingests vision/audio/language but has **no native channel for proprioception or interoception** — joint encoders, IMUs, tactile arrays, force/torque, battery/thermal/strain. Add a small per-modality **adapter**: a lightweight encoder (MLP / small transformer per stream) that projects each non-VLM sensor into the trunk's token space `D` and tags it with a learned modality embedding, so proprio/intero arrive as just more tokens the trunk attends over. The adapters are trained (the frozen/borrowed trunk needn't be); this is the standard "tokenize a new modality into a frozen backbone" move (LLaVA-style projector, but for body sense). Token counts per stream are starting points to tune — see the §4 contract.

### System 1 — the action expert (~8B)
A **flow-matching / diffusion** policy that consumes the trunk's latent state and emits **action chunks** — short sequences of whole-body targets — at high frequency (~50–200 Hz). Action chunking gives temporal coherence; flow matching handles the multimodal, high-DoF continuous action distribution that plain regression can't. This is the manipulation/skill layer.

The chunking scheme is the frontier here: plain ACT-style chunking re-plans each chunk from scratch and stutters at chunk boundaries, so move to **Real-Time Chunking (RTC)** — overlapping, asynchronously-refreshed chunks that stay coherent across boundaries and tolerate inference latency, the right fit for a partly-tethered trunk feeding an onboard expert (current SOTA generation — verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast).

**Action representation — add a relative / state-relative end-effector mode (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA EgoScale).** The default action target here is an **absolute joint-target vector** (the WBC tracks commanded poses). Add a **relative / state-relative end-effector (EEF) action mode** alongside it: actions expressed as *deltas of the end-effector pose relative to the current state* rather than absolute joint angles. This is the action space NVIDIA's EgoScale uses, and it is **what makes human ego-video transfer**: a relative-EEF action is **shared across human↔robot embodiments** (a human reaching and a robot reaching produce the same relative-EEF trajectory even with different kinematics), so the vast pool of human egocentric video becomes usable demonstration data for the expert — which an absolute-joint-target space, tied to one body's kinematics, cannot ingest. Keep absolute joint targets as the WBC-facing representation; add relative-EEF as the **policy-facing mode that unlocks ego-video pretraining** (current SOTA generation — verify exact framing/availability at build time, this moves fast).

**Two conditioning paths into the expert — the rich workspace *and* a cheap async intent latent (adopted from Figure's Helix S2→S1 handoff).** The action expert is conditioned on the trunk in **two** ways, kept distinct on purpose:
1. **The full `z_t` workspace (kept, the rich path).** The trunk's working-memory state — the shared workspace the felt organs and world model read and write (§4). This is the high-bandwidth representation; it is *not* given up.
2. **A compact intent latent (added, the cheap async fast-path).** Alongside the workspace, the S2/trunk distills a **compact intent latent** — a small projected vector summarizing current intent — that is **projected into and concatenated with the action expert's token stream** and **updated asynchronously, in the background, on a decoupled clock** from the expert's fast loop. The expert runs at its own ~50–200 Hz tempo against the latest available intent latent without blocking on the slow trunk; the trunk refreshes the latent whenever it next completes a pass. This is Figure's proven Helix handoff — decoupled-clock conditioning so a fast onboard expert and a slow trunk run at independent rates — and it pairs naturally with RTC's latency tolerance. The division of labor: **`z_t` is the rich workspace the felt organs and world model read; the intent latent is the cheap, async fast-path conditioning the expert tokens consume** (`standard` direction, adopted from Figure Helix — verify exact framing/availability at build time, this moves fast).

**Freeze vs. fine-tune — Knowledge Insulation is the default (`standard`, adopted from PI).** When adapting the borrowed trunk for the action expert, choose among: (i) **freeze** the trunk entirely and train only adapters/heads (cheapest, preserves priors, least plastic); (ii) **LoRA / low-rank fine-tune** (mid-ground); (iii) **Knowledge Insulation (KI)** (the π0.5-style approach — train the action expert while *insulating* the VLM trunk's pretrained knowledge from being washed out by action-only gradients, so you get the adaptation of fine-tuning without the catastrophic forgetting of the trunk's web-scale priors). **Make KI the default trunk-training approach** where you can afford it — it dominates the trade-off, buying fine-tuning's adaptation without losing the trunk's web-scale priors (`standard` direction, current SOTA generation — verify at build time). **How this reconciles with the "freeze the trunk for the felt phase" rule (G6):** the two are *sequential phases against different gradients*, not a contradiction. KI is the default for the **backbone/action-training phase** (adapting the borrowed trunk + action expert on demonstration/RL data); the **hard freeze** then applies to the **felt phase** (§3, G6), where the amygdala/BG/pain heads ground against a *fixed* `z` distribution and any trunk drift would silently de-ground them. Read it as: **KI while training the body, then freeze-and-version the trunk once the felt organs start grounding on it** — unfreezing thereafter is the explicit, versioned, re-grounded operation of G6 (and re-training the trunk under KI is itself such an event).

### Low-level whole-body controller (~3B)
A separately-trained controller for balance, locomotion, and contact/force regulation, running at ~0.5–1 kHz. Trained by massive-parallel RL in simulation with domain randomization, then transferred sim-to-real. It turns the action expert's targets into stable, compliant whole-body motion.

**A learned, perception-conditioned WBC — not a blind tracker (adopted from Figure's Helix System 0 — a learned WBC that replaced hand-written control).** Frame this as a single **learned net** in Figure's "System 0" mold: massive-parallel sim-to-real RL (100k+ parallel envs is the starting point to tune) that **replaces hand-written control entirely** — Figure's System 0 replaced ~109k lines of C++ — rather than a hand-tuned controller the action expert merely steers. Critically, it is **perception-conditioned**, not a blind joint-target tracker: it ingests **stereo → 3D terrain perception** (the §1 head-mounted stereo, lifted to a 3D terrain/contact representation) so the controller *sees* stairs, slopes, gaps, and contact geometry and pre-shapes footfall and balance against them. Figure's System 0 is now terrain-conditioned in exactly this way (`standard` direction, adopted from Figure Helix — verify exact framing/availability at build time, this moves fast). The action expert still hands down whole-body targets; the difference is the WBC realizes them with its own learned, terrain-aware sense of the ground rather than executing them blind.

**System-0 starting point — GEAR-SONIC, with our terrain-conditioning on top (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA).** Don't train the System-0 controller from zero either: start from an **open behavior-foundation whole-body controller** and add the terrain-conditioning above. The named starting point is **GEAR-SONIC** (NVIDIA GEAR's open whole-body-control / behavior-foundation model) — a pretrained, general WBC for humanoid locomotion and balance — used as the System-0 base, with our **stereo→3D terrain-perception conditioning** layered on top so the borrowed controller gains the terrain-aware footfall/balance shaping it doesn't have out of the box. Borrow the behavior foundation; add the terrain sense (current SOTA generation — verify exact version/availability at build time, this moves fast).

This backbone, on its own, is a competent instruction-following humanoid. What it lacks is *what to do when no one is instructing it* — which is the felt system's job.

## 3 · The felt system — the bet (embodied)

The felt organs sit **above** the backbone. They read the trunk's latent world state (and interoception) and produce the agent's own objective. They are separate modules on purpose — collapsing them into one scalar reward is the standard-RL move this whole project argues against. **The *guidance* is a trio — amygdala (the pull) + basal ganglia (value) + pain (the floor).** The hypothalamus is demoted to maintenance.

### Hypothalamus — drives `maintenance, demoted`
Reads interoception. Innate homeostatic setpoints — energy, thermal, structural load — that **pull the agent toward** resources (charge, rest). Tonic, slow, satiable. **Not** part of the guidance trio: it's upkeep (recharge when low, don't overheat) plus the infant bootstrap. An imitation-pretrained agent with a seeded amygdala-pull and pain already has its attractor and repulsor — keep this minimal.

### Pain — nociception
Reads interoception for **tissue/hardware damage** — impact, over-torque, joint-limit strain, thermal overload. Two outputs: a **fast protective reflex** at ~1 kHz that overrides the action expert (withdraw, brake, protect), and a **strong, non-habituating** aversive learning signal. Pain is the one felt organ that is cleanly buildable, because its target — damage — is specifiable. Distinct from the hypothalamus: damage/withdraw, not deficit/approach; and it must **never habituate**.

**Measuring pain without skin everywhere.** A humanoid can't have tactile sensors on every surface — so you don't measure contact, you **estimate force and predict collision** from sensors it already has. Five layers, combined by a max:

1. **Sensorless external-force estimation (the workhorse).** A momentum/disturbance observer estimates external torque from the gap between commanded torque and the dynamics model: `τ_ext = τ_measured − M(q)q̈ − C(q,q̇)q̇ − g(q)`. Every joint already has torque/current sensing, so an unexpected `τ_ext` spike *localizes* a collision **anywhere on the body** — no skin needed. This is standard collision detection in robot arms; it gives whole-body coverage.
2. **Computed self-collision.** The robot knows its own kinematics, so it computes predicted self-contact geometrically (capsule/mesh proximity) — it knows its hand is about to hit its face *without a sensor there*. Imminent self-contact = pain. This is how it stops smacking itself.
3. **IMU impact spikes.** Sudden acceleration/jerk = impact; cheap and global.
4. **Targeted real tactile, only where it pays.** Sparse but strategic: fingertips/palms (manipulation), soles (contact), and high-value surfaces (head/face). You don't need skin everywhere — you need it where damage is likely or costly. (Note: fingertip tactile is no longer optional here — §1 makes high-sensitivity fingertip sensing a *hard requirement* fused end-to-end, adopted from Figure's Helix; the "only where it pays" trade-off now governs *non-fingertip* surfaces — soles, head/face — while fingertips are a floor.)
5. **Thermal/current.** Motor over-current and temperature = strain/overload.

**It's estimation, not noise.** You do *not* inject noise to "feel around" — the signal is a precise model-prediction *discrepancy*, not a stochastic probe. And the real protection is **anticipatory**: the world model + self-model let the agent *imagine* a trajectory, predict high contact force, and feel anticipatory pain through imagine-and-feel — so it recoils from the *imagined* collision and never makes the hard contact. Reactive estimation is the floor; predicted pain is what keeps it from hitting things hard in the first place.

**Anticipatory pain needs a *learned* pain head — the analytic estimator can't run on latents (G3).** The five-layer estimator above runs on *real* sensors (measured torque, kinematics, IMU, taxels). An imagined future is a predicted **latent `z`**, not a sensor stream — there is no `τ_measured` to difference against, so the momentum/disturbance observer simply cannot be evaluated on it. So train a **learned pain head `pain(z) → scalar`**: a small regression head that maps the trunk latent (real or imagined) to expected nociceptive magnitude, **supervised by the analytic estimator on real experience** (the analytic max is the label; the head learns to predict it from `z`). At wake time the analytic estimator is still the ground truth (and the reflex source); the learned head exists so anticipatory pain can be felt on imagined `z` during imagine-and-feel, where the analytic estimator is undefined. It carries the same `real|imagined` provenance as any other read of `z` (§4). Distillation target, label source, and head size are starting points to tune.

### Amygdala — the ineffable pull
The heart, and the wall. Reads the trunk's latent state and produces our **innate dispositions** — the pre-verbal pull toward and away, and so what it attends to and for how long. It is **not** a good/bad classifier. Its target is unverbalizable, so it cannot be supervised the way everything else is; a vision-language model captures only the *verbalizable shadow* (semantic valence), not the pull that casts it. So it cannot be pretrained or grounded in language — at most, human affect data seeds the perceptual *shadow*. The highest-fidelity seed obtainable is a **single emotionally-exceptional human** mapping their felt affect *sub-frame-by-sub-frame* over rich audio/video — the felt report is the grounding signal; paired physiology, if recorded, is a later validation aid, not part of it (the protocol in [CONCEPTS.md §3](../CONCEPTS.md); the concrete data-spec, annotation tool, and shadow-head trainer already exist at **[amygdala-trainer/SPEC.md](../amygdala-trainer/SPEC.md)** — reference it, it is not duplicated here) — but even that only seeds the shadow. The pull itself is the wall: earned through lived experience, not built from data. You cannot build an innate disposition from a dataset.

### Threat / cortisol and NE / uncertainty — the felt signal is several systems (G5)
Feeling is not one scalar — it is several systems running at once (the thesis is explicit that the felt signal is a *trio* plus modulators, not a single reward). Beyond the amygdala's fast pull, two slower neuromodulatory channels enter the felt signal and its scalarization:

- **Threat / cortisol — a graded, slow stress channel.** A **graded** threat estimate (not the binary pain reflex) integrated into a slow stress level. Model it as a **slow low-pass of `relu(−valence)`** — i.e. cortisol accumulates from the *negative* part of the amygdala's valence over time and decays slowly, so sustained aversive context raises a tonic stress that a single bad moment does not. This is the standard "fast affect vs. slow stress hormone" split: the amygdala spikes, cortisol is the smoothed, lingering shadow of repeated negative pull. High cortisol biases the scalarization toward caution/avoidance and (per the root-thesis spec) toward **exploration when stalled** — a stall-breaking drive, not just a damper. The low-pass time constant and the cortisol weight in the scalarization are starting points to tune.
- **NE / uncertainty — a precision/arousal channel.** A norepinephrine-style channel carrying **uncertainty / surprise** (model-prediction error, value-ensemble disagreement, novelty). It does not add valence; it **modulates gain** — raising it sharpens attention and learning rate under surprise (and feeds the orienting reflex in PERCEPTION-ACTION §1–2, where novelty pulls gaze). It also feeds epistemic action (imagine-and-feel over *where to look* — PERCEPTION-ACTION §3). NE is the precision knob on the felt signal, not a term added to it.

These are consistent with "feeling is several systems": the amygdala sets direction, cortisol sets a slow protective tone, NE sets the gain. All three feed the scalarization in §4; none collapses the others into a single reward.

### Habituation
Felt reward (pleasure, **not** pain) fades with repetition and recovers slowly. Blocks single-stimulus wireheading and turns "seek good" into "seek *new* good" — curiosity that emerges from the affect dynamics. It biases toward variety, it doesn't guarantee it (an agent could still time-share satiated sources); promising, untested at scale.

**What habituates — attended content, not the whole scene (G11).** Habituation must key on *what the agent is actually attending to*, not a mean-pool of the whole frame — otherwise a busy background would dilute the habituation signal and you could re-trigger the same pleasure by jittering irrelevant periphery. So compute the habituation state over **attended/foveal tokens** (§4 `fovea|attended` tag): a small **learned key** `k = f(z_attended)` maps the attended tokens to a habituation-relevant embedding, and a per-key recency/familiarity trace `h(k)` decays the felt reward for stimuli near a recently-visited key (recovering slowly). This is what makes "seek *new* good" mean *new attended content*, not new pixels anywhere. **Is `h` an input to `Q`?** No — `h` modulates the **felt reward** (the BG's training signal), it is **not** appended to the basal-ganglia value input. The BG value `Q(z)` stays a function of the latent state only; habituation enters through the reward the value is trained on, so satiation shapes *what becomes valuable* without making the value head's input non-stationary in a second way. (If a future variant wants the agent to *anticipate* its own satiation, `h` could be added to `Q`'s input — flagged as an option, not the default; `bet`.) Key dimension, decay/recovery rates are starting points to tune.

### Imagination — one multimodal faculty (~30B)
A single learned latent dynamics model over the trunk's representation — **not one imaginer per sense.** When it imagines a future it renders the *whole multimodal latent at once* (vision, audio, touch, proprioception together), because the world is all of them together; auditory and visual imagery are the same faculty in different channels (inner speech is the one near-exception — it also borrows the speech-motor/voice head, covert articulation). The imagined latent is written to the same workspace the real senses write to (§4), tagged `imagined` and carrying the same per-token modality and `fovea|periphery` tags as a real frame — including the foveated structure, so imagination wastes no capacity rendering periphery it didn't attend to (active/foveated perception is specced in **[PERCEPTION-ACTION.md](PERCEPTION-ACTION.md)**, cross-referenced here, not re-specced). Enables **imagine-and-feel**: roll futures forward, let the amygdala feel them, act toward the future that pulls hardest. The bottleneck is faithful, far-reaching, multi-leg **search** — not the feeling. Both are load-bearing: the world model predicts the futures and the amygdala must appraise them; neither works without the other.

**Frontier defaults (`standard` baselines, all numbers are starting points to tune):**
- **World model:** the *faithfulness* frontier has moved **reconstruction-free** — self-predictive / **JEPA-style** dynamics that predict in latent space rather than reconstructing pixels/audio, which avoids spending capacity on perceptually-irrelevant detail (exactly the foveation argument, applied to the world model). So **make JEPA-style latent prediction the default**, not just the upgrade path: the current SOTA generation is **V-JEPA 2-AC** (an action-conditioned video-JEPA world model trained for planning/control) and **Dreamer-4** (the latest Dreamer line, scaled and faster); verify exact version/availability at build time — this moves fast. **DreamerV3** remains the proven reconstruction-based baseline and a sane fallback if a JEPA-style model won't train at this scale. Which objective wins at this scale is still a `bet`; the default is now JEPA, with DreamerV3 as the known-good floor.
- **Planning / learned search:** for the search stage, name the continuous/efficient MuZero line rather than generic MCTS — **Sampled MuZero** (handles continuous, high-DoF action spaces by planning over sampled actions, which a humanoid requires) and **EfficientZero V2** (sample-efficient learned-model search, strong in the data-limited regime that matters for a single embodied body). TD-MPC2 remains a viable model-predictive-control alternative for the continuous-control inner loop. The search runs over the latent world-model rollout and is appraised by the amygdala.

**World-model faithfulness acceptance gate — before Stage-5 reliance (G4).** Imagine-and-feel is only as safe as the world model is faithful: a model that rolls out plausible-but-wrong futures will let the agent feel — and then act toward — things that won't happen. So **the agent must not *rely* on imagined rollouts to drive behavior until the world model passes an explicit faithfulness gate.** Concretely, gate the transition to the imagination-reliant stage (the felt/autonomous "Stage 5") on held-out metrics over real trajectories, all **starting points to tune**: multi-step **latent open-loop prediction error** stays bounded out to the planning horizon `H` (not just one step); **calibration** — the model's predicted uncertainty tracks its actual error (so the agent can down-weight rollouts it shouldn't trust); **`pain(z)` agreement** — anticipatory pain on imagined `z` matches the analytic estimator's value when those futures are actually executed (the safety-critical channel must be faithful before it's trusted); and **no reward/feasibility hallucination** — imagined high-pull or low-pain futures are not systematically more optimistic than realized ones. Below threshold, imagine-and-feel runs in **shadow mode** (logged, scored, not in the action loop); it earns the right to steer behavior only after it clears the gate, and re-clears it after any major re-grounding. This gate is itself part of the safety story (§Safety).

**One workspace, two writers.** Both the real senses and the world model write to the **same perceptual latent** (the trunk's working-memory state), and the amygdala reads that workspace **blind to real-vs-imagined** — which is why the agent can feel a future it only imagined. The required safeguard is a **reality-monitoring tag** on every state (real vs imagined) so feeling an imagined threat never triggers acting on it as if present.

**The tag is a hard provenance bit, not a learned guess (G10).** Do *not* implement reality-monitoring as a classifier that infers "does this look real?" — make it a **hard, immutable provenance bit set by the writer at the plumbing level**. The real sensory front-end sets `real` when it writes a token; the world model sets `imagined` when it writes; the bit travels with the tensor and **no downstream module may flip it**. The action path then enforces a **`real`-only mask at the plumbing level** — the action expert physically cannot consume `imagined` tokens, because the masking happens in the tensor pipeline below any learned component, not as a policy the network could learn around. This makes "act only on reality" a structural guarantee rather than a behavior we hope the agent acquires. A reality-monitoring **classifier is still useful, but demoted**: it runs as a watchdog whose *only* job is to catch **pipeline bugs** — a mismatch between the provenance bit and what the content looks like flags a plumbing fault (a writer mis-tagging, a leak between buffers), not an agent decision. So hallucination here is reclassified from "the agent believed an imagined thing" to "the pipeline mis-set or leaked a bit," which is a testable, fixable engineering fault rather than an emergent behavior.

### Basal ganglia — the value system
What is worth pursuing over long horizons. The anticipatory pull made durable — and the second wall: it **cannot be pretrained** (it's about this body's lived consequences, the unlived value system — keep this flagged as the wall, not given fake certainty). It develops through embodied experience and serves as a **cache** of the imagine-and-feel computation (System 2 → System 1). Dopamine — reward-prediction-error — trains it.

**Freeze the trunk for the felt phase; re-ground with versioning if you ever unfreeze (G6).** The felt organs (amygdala, BG, pain head, habituation key) all learn *on top of* the trunk latent `z`. If the trunk drifts underneath them, everything grounded in `z` silently de-grounds — a value or a pull learned against one representation is meaningless against another. So **commit a frozen trunk for the duration of the felt phase**: the felt organs ground against a fixed `z` distribution, which is the only way their lived-experience learning is even well-defined. If a later need forces unfreezing the trunk (new modality, capability upgrade), treat it as a **versioned re-grounding event**, not a quiet continuation: bump a trunk **version id**, tag every consolidated felt-memory and value with the trunk version it was grounded against, and **re-ground / re-validate** the felt organs against the new `z` (re-clearing the world-model faithfulness gate, G4) before trusting old values under the new trunk. Anything tagged with a stale trunk version is replay/re-grounding fodder, not directly trusted. Freeze is the default; unfreeze is an explicit, versioned, re-grounded operation.

**Value-learner recipe (`standard`, starting point to tune).** Keep the **ensemble twin-Q** idea (robust, low-overestimation value estimates), but the current default is **not** REDQ — REDQ is outdated for the high-UTD (update-to-data) regime this design runs in (continuous high-UTD replay, §memory). Use a **2025 high-UTD recipe** instead:
- **SimbaV2** — a normalized actor-critic that stays stable at high UTD via architectural normalization (the network can absorb many gradient steps per environment step without diverging), or
- **CrossQ** — uses **BatchNorm** in the critic and **removes target networks** entirely, getting high sample efficiency at low overhead (no slow-moving target copy to maintain).

Why the swap: at high UTD, target-network and ensemble-only stabilization (REDQ-style) leave performance on the table and are prone to the **plasticity loss** that high-UTD training induces (see memory/consolidation below); SimbaV2/CrossQ are designed for exactly the many-updates-per-step regime the BG runs in. Name SimbaV2 or CrossQ as the default; keep the twin-Q ensemble on top. Ensemble size, UTD ratio, and normalization details are all starting points to tune.

### Memory / hippocampus (~7B budget = the replay buffer, not a separate parametric model) and consolidation
An episodic store with **continuous high-UTD replay** — *experience replay is perpetual sleep*. There is no distinct sleep phase; consolidation runs while the agent acts. (A real offline pass is warranted only if the deployed body genuinely cannot learn while acting.) **What the ~7B budget line actually buys (pin this — it has read as a mystery model):** it is the **replay buffer / episodic store**, not a separate ~7B *parametric* network. The "hippocampus" function here is the stored stream of lived segments (the §Stage-5 replay-buffer segment format) plus the consolidation machinery that replays it into the value/world-model/felt heads — there is no extra trained model to build for it. (If a future variant wants a *learned* episodic module — a parametric read/write interface over `z_t` with its own build step — that is a flagged option, `bet`, not the default; the default is "the budget is the buffer.")

**The "forever policy" gap — continual-learning hazards (the weakest area, flagged honestly).** A body that learns for a lifetime under high-UTD updates faces two failure modes the rest of this design does *not* automatically solve. Name them and budget mitigations in from the start; all knobs below are starting points to tune, and whether these mitigations are *sufficient* for true lifelong embodied learning is itself open — `bet`, not `proven`.

1. **Plasticity loss (the network stops being able to learn).** High-UTD training degrades a network's ability to fit new targets — dead/saturated units, collapsing effective rank, dormant neurons. This is *the* hazard the SimbaV2/CrossQ value swap partially addresses (normalization), but it needs explicit handling too. Mitigations: **periodic / soft resets** of the most-saturated parameters — **ReDo** (recycle dormant neurons), and broader **plasticity injection** / soft-reset schedules that re-randomize a fraction of weights while preserving learned ones. Reset cadence and the fraction reset are starting points to tune.
2. **Catastrophic forgetting (new skills overwrite consolidated ones).** Continual updates on the recent stream erode skills the body already earned. The retention mechanism is **replay**, layered on the episodic store: **replay-CLEAR-style** behavioral-cloning + value-distillation off a long-horizon replay buffer (keep rehearsing consolidated skills, not just recent experience). Replay is the workhorse. **Drop EWC** — Fisher/quadratic-anchor regularization is a known-failed mitigation in this regime (it tends to collapse to plain fine-tuning and entrenches without actually protecting skills); do not budget for it. Lean on replay coverage instead, widened by the consolidation store.

**The arbitration — reset vs. retain, and on which sub-nets (G9).** Resets and retention pull against each other (resets fight plasticity loss but can *cause* forgetting; retention fights forgetting but can *entrench* and reduce plasticity), so the continual-learning subsystem must **arbitrate** between them rather than run both blindly. Make the trigger explicit and **per-sub-net** (the value heads, the world model, the felt heads, and the adapters each get their own decision — never reset the frozen trunk, G6). Monitor three metrics per sub-net (all thresholds are starting points to tune):
- **dormancy fraction** — share of dead/saturated units (the ReDo signal);
- **effective rank** — collapse of the representation's effective dimensionality;
- **forgetting on a held-out probe** — a frozen, periodically-evaluated probe set of already-consolidated skills.

Arbitration rule: high dormancy / collapsed effective rank with *stable* probe performance ⇒ **plasticity loss is the binding problem ⇒ reset** (ReDo recycle the dormant units / soft-reset that sub-net's most-saturated parameters, re-randomizing a fraction while preserving learned ones). Rising probe-forgetting with *healthy* dormancy/rank ⇒ **forgetting is the binding problem ⇒ retain** (lean harder on replay-CLEAR rehearsal, throttle the update-to-data ratio). The two metric families point at *different* sub-nets at different times, which is why the decision is per-sub-net and metric-gated rather than a global schedule. Reset cadence, the fraction reset, probe contents, and all thresholds are starting points to tune; whether this arbitration is *sufficient* for true lifelong embodied learning remains the open problem of the forever policy — `bet`, not `proven`.

## 4 · How the felt system attaches as a separate module reading `z_t` (the interface)

```
   trunk latent state ──► amygdala (pull, valence) ─┬─────────────┐
                                                    │             │ relu(−valence), slow low-pass
   trunk latent ────────► threat/cortisol (slow) ◄──┘             ▼
   interoception ───────► drives  ─────────────────────────┐   cortisol (tonic stress) ─┐
   interoception ───────► pain ────────────────────────────┤                            │
   trunk latent / WM PE ► NE / uncertainty (gain) ──────────┤                            │
                                                            ▼                            ▼
                       felt signal = pull + drives − pain − w_c·cortisol,  modulated by habituation, gain-scaled by NE
                                                            │
                                                            ├─► reward for online learning  ──► basal ganglia (value)
   world model ◄── trunk latent ──► imagine-and-feel ───────┘                                       │
                                                                          dopamine = RPE ◄─────────────┘
   pain reflex ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────► overrides action expert (fast loop)
```

The scalarization is **`felt = pull + drives − pain − w_c·cortisol`**, then **modulated by habituation** (fades the pleasure terms) and **gain-scaled by NE** (precision/learning-rate under surprise, §3). It is deliberately *not* one organ's scalar — the trio (pull, value, pain) plus the slow channels (cortisol, NE) and habituation are several systems summed and modulated, which is the whole point. `w_c` and the NE gain are starting points to tune.

Four rules: **it reads, it doesn't replace** (the organs take the trunk's latent state as input, not raw pixels); **it generates the objective** (the felt signal *is* the reward — no external reward function); **it plans by feeling futures** (imagine-and-feel over the world model, cached by the BG); **it never wireheads** (habituation fades pleasure only).

### The `z_t` workspace tensor contract (authoritative)

`z_t` is the trunk's working-memory state — the **shared workspace** every felt organ reads and both writers (real senses, world model) write. This is the load-bearing interface; pin it once and build to it. All counts and dims below are **starting points to tune**, not measured optima.

**Shape.** `z_t` is a **sequence of tokens**, `bfloat16`, shape **`[B, T_tok, D]`** — batch × token-count × trunk hidden width. It is *not* a flat vector; the organs attend over tokens.

- **`D` = trunk hidden width** — the System-2 trunk's model dimension. Every modality is projected into this one width (the §2 sensor adapters do this for proprio/intero). Starting point: `D ≈ 4096` (tie to the chosen ~135B trunk — fix when the backbone is chosen).
- **`T_tok` = sum of per-modality token counts**, `T_tok = N_vision + N_audio + N_proprio + N_intero + N_lang`, where `N_vision = N_fovea + N_periphery` (per camera). Example starting counts (per timestep, single camera; tune):

  | modality | tokens (`N_*`) | source |
  |---|---|---|
  | vision — fovea | ~256 | high-res foveal crop → DINOv3 ViT-L-class (PERCEPTION-ACTION §1) |
  | vision — periphery | ~96 | wide low-res stream → DINOv3 ViT-S-class (PERCEPTION-ACTION §1) |
  | audio | ~32 | attended stream + ambient mixture (PERCEPTION-ACTION §2) |
  | proprioception | ~16 | joint encoders, IMUs, F/T → §2 adapter |
  | interoception | ~8 | energy, thermal, load, strain → §2 adapter |
  | language | ~64 | instruction / inner-speech tokens |
  | **`T_tok`** | **~472** | (per camera; multiply vision by #cameras) |

  These are seeds. Multi-camera multiplies the vision rows; a history window over the trunk's time window multiplies `T_tok` (or is summarized into memory tokens) — both are tuning decisions.

**Per-token tags (every token carries them).** Alongside its `D`-vector, each token has:
- **modality tag** — `{vision, audio, proprio, intero, lang}` (a learned modality embedding, the §2 adapter's output).
- **`real | imagined` tag** — the **reality-monitoring** bit, implemented as a **hard, immutable provenance bit set by the writer** (G10), not an inferred label. Real senses write `real`; the world model writes `imagined`; no downstream module may flip it. The amygdala reads blind to this tag (so it can feel an imagined future), but it is a **hard safety interlock**: only `real`-tagged states may drive the action expert, enforced as a **`real`-only mask at the plumbing level** below any learned component. A reality-monitoring classifier is demoted to a watchdog for **pipeline bugs** (provenance-vs-content mismatch = a plumbing fault), not the safeguard itself. Tag mis-set or buffer leak = the failure mode (the engineering version of "hallucination").
- **`fovea | periphery` tag** — for vision tokens (and by analogy `attended | ambient` for audio), so downstream knows what was attended vs. ambient. Carried through imagination too, so imagined frames keep the foveated structure (§3 imagination).
- positional / gaze metadata — gaze `(x, y, scale)` and beamform direction enter as tokens (PERCEPTION-ACTION §1, §3).

**Who reads `z_t`:** the amygdala (feels it), the basal ganglia (values it), the threat/cortisol and NE/uncertainty channels (§3), the learned **`pain(z)` head** (anticipatory pain on real or imagined `z`, §3), the drives/pain pathways (via interoception tokens), and the world model (predicts the next `z_t`). **Who writes `z_t`:** the real sensory front-end (`real`) and the world model (`imagined`) — one workspace, two writers (§3). The `real | imagined` tag is a hard, writer-set provenance bit (§3, G10); only `real`-tagged tokens reach the action expert (a plumbing-level mask).

**The action expert is conditioned by a *derived* compact intent latent, not by reading `z_t` directly (§2, adopted from Figure Helix).** `z_t` is the rich workspace for the felt organs and world model; the action expert instead consumes a **compact intent latent distilled from the trunk** and concatenated into its own token stream, refreshed **asynchronously on a decoupled clock** (§2 System 1). This keeps the heavy workspace on the slow felt/deliberative side while the fast expert runs against a cheap, async summary — the two paths are deliberately separate.

## 6 · Actuation & embodiment

- **Actuators** — high-torque-density electric actuators with series elasticity or torque sensing for compliance and safe contact; ~25–40 DoF (legs, arms, torso, neck, dexterous hands).
- **Hands** — multi-fingered with tactile sensing; the limiting factor for general manipulation.
- **Voice** — an audio-output head for vocalization.
- **Onboard compute** — a 200B model does not run fully onboard at these rates. Realistic split: the low-level WBC, pain reflex, and action expert run **onboard** (hard real-time, latency-critical); the System-2 trunk, world model, and amygdala appraisal run **on a tethered/edge accelerator** or are **distilled** (the BG cache is exactly this — compiling slow deliberation into a fast onboard reflex). Quantization and MoE sparsity make the trunk tractable.

**Export path (`standard`, adopted from NVIDIA).** The onboard models reach the robot through an **ONNX → TensorRT** export/optimization path: train in PyTorch, export to ONNX, compile with TensorRT for the target accelerator (kernel fusion, the NVFP4/FP8 numeric formats below, layer-wise precision). This is the standard route onto Jetson Thor-class onboard silicon and edge Blackwell — name it as the deployment path for the onboard/distilled loops (current SOTA generation — verify exact toolchain/version at build time, this moves fast).

**Latency budget per loop (starting points to tune, set by the control-rate hierarchy):**

| loop | rate | budget / cycle | where it runs |
|---|---|---|---|
| pain reflex | ~1 kHz | **≤1 ms** | onboard, hard real-time |
| low-level WBC | ~0.5–1 kHz | **≤1–2 ms** | onboard |
| action expert (System 1) | ~50–200 Hz | **≤5–20 ms** | onboard |
| world model / imagine-and-feel | ~1–10 Hz | **≤100 ms–1 s** | edge/tethered |
| System-2 trunk + amygdala appraisal | ~1–10 Hz | **≤100 ms–1 s** | edge/tethered |

The onboard loops have hard deadlines (missing one is a fall or a collision); the felt/deliberative loops are slow by design (the amygdala sets direction, not torques). The BG cache exists precisely to pull amygdala-quality decisions *down* into the action-expert budget.

**Measured anchors for the action-expert rate (replacing pure guesswork; current SOTA generation — verify at build time, this moves fast).** The "~50–200 Hz" expert rate above is no longer a bare starting point — anchor it to two reference deployments:
- **Onboard, distilled (NVIDIA Jetson Thor / RTX-class).** A VLA action expert distilled and TensorRT-compiled (export path above) runs an end-to-end policy at roughly **~23 Hz on Jetson Thor-class onboard silicon** and **~50–80 Hz on an RTX-class accelerator** — measured anchors for what the fully-onboard expert loop actually achieves today, not an aspiration.
- **Cloud/edge backbone, async (Gemini Robotics-class).** A tethered reasoning backbone in the cloud answers with a **<160 ms backbone latency** and **~250 ms end-to-end** including the round trip, while the onboard control loop still runs at **~50 Hz** against the latest available intent — i.e. the slow backbone and the fast loop run on decoupled clocks (exactly the §2 async-intent-latent / RTC design, adopted from Gemini). This is the measured profile that justifies the tethered-trunk + onboard-expert split.

These are *anchors to tune against*, not contractual numbers — but they replace the earlier pure guesswork with what current-generation silicon and cloud backbones are measured to deliver.

**Quantization & sparsity defaults (starting points to tune):**
- **Trunk:** weight-quantize to **4-bit** (e.g. NF4/GPTQ/AWQ-class) for the edge-resident trunk where it holds accuracy; fall back to **8-bit** for accuracy-sensitive layers and for the onboard-distilled paths. Default: **4-bit trunk weights, 8-bit activations**, profile and lift any layer that loses too much.
- **Deployment numeric format:** target **NVFP4** — the 4-bit floating-point format on current-generation accelerators (**Blackwell** datacenter / **Jetson Thor**-class onboard) that holds accuracy far better than integer 4-bit by keeping a floating exponent, with hardware support for the matmuls. Default to NVFP4 where the silicon supports it, **fall back to FP8** for accuracy-sensitive layers and on hardware without FP4 support (current SOTA generation — verify exact format/hardware availability at build time, this moves fast).
- **MoE:** if the trunk is mixture-of-experts, the cost that matters is **active** parameters, not total. Starting point: **top-2 of N experts active per token** (≈ activate ~2/`N` of expert params) — tune the active-expert count against the per-loop latency budget above; fewer active experts buys latency at an accuracy cost.

## Safety

An agent with self-generated, persistent, grounded desires it pursues **with a body** is the maximal-autonomy configuration AI safety treats as the core risk — and this design is explicitly that. The thesis (*build the human guiding system because nothing else scales to open-ended embodied life*) is simultaneously the strongest argument for, and the strongest reason to be careful with, this exact agent. Concretely:

- **Hard-bounded actuation** — physical torque/velocity/force limits below the controller, non-overridable by any learned policy. Pain's reflex protects the body; a separate hard layer protects the world.
- **Observability** — the felt signal, the amygdala's pull, the value estimates, and imagine-and-feel rollouts are all logged and inspectable. An agent whose *wants* are legible is far safer than one whose objective is hidden in weights.
- **Bounded autonomy, earned slowly** — the open-ended feeling-driven stage runs under supervision and in constrained settings first; autonomy widens only as behavior is understood.
- **The off-switch is upstream of feeling** — drives and reflexes can be externally clamped; the agent's desires never gate its own shutdown.

Build it small, observable, and slow. The autonomy that makes it interesting is the autonomy that makes it dangerous.
